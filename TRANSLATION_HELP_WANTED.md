# TRANSLATION_HELP_WANTED.md

## We need your help! (native speakers especially)

AI Brain — Australia ships with onboarding guides in 4 languages, but the non-English translations are **AI-assisted** and may contain errors, awkward phrasing, or culturally inappropriate expressions.

We would love **native-speaker corrections** via Pull Request.

## Currently supported languages

| Language | File | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **English** | [GETTING_STARTED.md](GETTING_STARTED.md) | Authoritative · maintained by publisher |
| **Simplified Chinese (简体中文)** | [GETTING_STARTED_CHINESE.md](GETTING_STARTED_CHINESE.md) | AI-assisted · needs native review |
| **Vietnamese (Tiếng Việt)** | [GETTING_STARTED_VIETNAMESE.md](GETTING_STARTED_VIETNAMESE.md) | AI-assisted · needs native review |
| **Arabic (العربية)** | [GETTING_STARTED_ARABIC.md](GETTING_STARTED_ARABIC.md) | AI-assisted · needs native review |

## What to look for when reviewing

- **Terminology:** Australian legal terminology translated correctly? (e.g., "solicitor" vs "lawyer," "directions hearing," "LPUL," "APP 8")
- **Grammar and fluency:** Does the translation read naturally to a native speaker?
- **Cultural appropriateness:** Is the tone right for legal professionals in the target language community?
- **RTL rendering (Arabic):** Does the Arabic text render correctly with right-to-left layout? Any Unicode issues?
- **Completeness:** Any sections missing or truncated?

## How to contribute

1. Fork the repo
2. Edit the relevant `GETTING_STARTED_*.md` file
3. Remove the AI-assisted disclaimer banner from the top of the file (it shows `> ⚠️ AI-assisted translation...`)
4. Add your name/nickname to a `## Contributors` section at the bottom
5. Open a PR with a brief description of what you changed

## Why this matters

Australia has significant migrant communities whose first language is Mandarin (over 685,000 speakers), Vietnamese (over 320,000), and Arabic (over 367,000). Many of these community members are legal professionals or aspiring practitioners. Accurate translations make the tool genuinely useful to them, rather than a gesture.
