## Section-by-Section Digest

### Section 12 — Meaning of security interest

**Plain-English summary:**
Adopts a "functional" definition: if a transaction *in substance* secures a payment or obligation, it's a security interest, regardless of its name or who has legal title. Includes Retention of Title (ROT) and Hire Purchase.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A **security interest** in personal property means an interest... provided for by a transaction that, in substance, secures payment or performance of an obligation (without regard to the form of the transaction or the identity of the person who has title to the property).
> (2) ...includes... (a) fixed charge; (b) floating charge... (d) conditional sale agreement (including... retention of title); (i) lease of goods...

**Citation format:** s 12 Personal Property Securities Act 2009 (Cth)

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Prevents the "I still own it" argument in insolvency. If you didn't register your interest on the PPSR, you lose it to the liquidator even if you have title.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

### Section 19 — Enforceability: Attachment

**Plain-English summary:**
Attachment is when the security interest becomes "real" between the parties. It happens when the grantor has rights in the collateral and value is given by the secured party (e.g. money lent).

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A security interest **attaches** to collateral when: (a) the grantor has rights in the collateral... and (b) either: (i) value is given... or (ii) the grantor does an act by which the security interest arises.
> (5) ...a grantor has rights in goods... under a PPS lease... or... retention of title when the grantor obtains possession of the goods.

**Citation format:** s 19 Personal Property Securities Act 2009 (Cth)

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 21 — Perfection (The Gold Standard)

**Plain-English summary:**
Perfection makes a security interest enforceable against third parties (like other creditors) and secures its priority. Usually achieved by registration on the PPSR.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A security interest... is **perfected** if: (a) the security interest is attached... (b) ...enforceable against a third party; and (c) any of the following applies: (i) registered; (ii) possession... (iii) control...

**Citation format:** s 21 Personal Property Securities Act 2009 (Cth)

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Priority disputes. The first to perfect (usually the first to register) wins. Control (ss 25-29) beats registration.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 267 — Vesting (The "PPSA Trap")

**Plain-English summary:**
If a security interest is not perfected at the moment a company enters liquidation or administration (or a person becomes bankrupt), the security interest "vests" in the grantor. Effectively, the secured party loses their security and becomes an unsecured creditor.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) An unperfected security interest granted by a grantor **vests in the grantor** if: (a) any of the following events occurs: (i) winding up... (ii) administrator appointed... (iv) liquidator appointed... (v) bankrupt... and (b) the security interest is unperfected at the time...

**Citation format:** s 267 Personal Property Securities Act 2009 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Corporations Act 2001 s 588FL (The 20-business-day rule for registration).

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- The most important "gotcha" in commercial law. Registration must be done immediately; even a one-day delay can be fatal if the client goes bust within 6 months.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED
