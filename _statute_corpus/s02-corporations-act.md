## Section-by-Section Digest

### Section 124 — Legal capacity and powers of a company

**Plain-English summary:**
Confirms that a company has the same legal capacity and powers as an individual, including the power to issue shares and grant security.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A company has the legal capacity and powers of an individual both in and outside this jurisdiction. A company also has all the powers of a body corporate, including the power to:
> (a) issue and cancel shares in the company;
> (b) issue debentures...
> (e) give security by charging uncalled capital;
> (f) grant a floating charge over the company's property...
> (2) A company's legal capacity to do something is not affected by the fact that the company's interests are not, or would not be, served by doing it.

**Citation format:** s 124 Corporations Act 2001 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Other sections: s 125 (Constitution may limit powers), s 126 (Agent exercising power).

**Currency:**
- Last amended: 2001 (Original enactment)

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Foundation for confirming a company's ability to enter contracts and be sued.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

### Section 180 — Care and diligence—civil obligation only

**Plain-English summary:**
Requires directors and officers to exercise their powers with the degree of care and diligence that a reasonable person in their position would exercise. Includes the "Business Judgment Rule" as a defense.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) Degree of care and diligence: A director or other officer of a corporation must exercise their powers and discharge their duties with the degree of care and diligence that a reasonable person would exercise if they:
> (a) were a director or officer of a corporation in the corporation's circumstances; and
> (b) occupied the office held by, and had the same responsibilities within the corporation as, the director or officer.
> (2) Business judgment rule: A director or other officer of a corporation who makes a business judgment is taken to meet the requirements of subsection (1)... if they:
> (a) make the judgment in good faith for a proper purpose; and
> (b) do not have a material personal interest...
> (c) inform themselves...
> (d) rationally believe that the judgment is in the best interests of the corporation.

**Citation format:** s 180 Corporations Act 2001 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Leading cases: *ASIC v Hellicar* [2012] HCA 17; *Cassimatis v ASIC* [2020] FCAFC 52.

**Currency:**
- Last amended: 2001

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- The primary duty for director negligence. Often used by ASIC in enforcement actions.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 181 — Good faith—civil obligations

**Plain-English summary:**
Requires directors and officers to act in good faith in the best interests of the corporation and for a proper purpose.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A director or other officer of a corporation must exercise their powers and discharge their duties:
> (a) in good faith in the best interests of the corporation; and
> (b) for a proper purpose.

**Citation format:** s 181 Corporations Act 2001 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Other sections: s 184 (Criminal equivalent).

**Currency:**
- Last amended: 2001

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Used when a director prioritizes personal interests or the interests of a third party over the company.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 182 — Use of position—civil obligations

**Plain-English summary:**
Prohibits directors, officers, and employees from improperly using their position to gain an advantage or cause detriment to the company.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A director, secretary, other officer or employee of a corporation must not improperly use their position to:
> (a) gain an advantage for themselves or someone else; or
> (b) cause detriment to the corporation.

**Citation format:** s 182 Corporations Act 2001 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Leading cases: *Angas Law Services Pty Ltd (in liq) v Carabelas* [2005] HCA 23.

**Currency:**
- Last amended: 2001

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Broad prohibition against "self-dealing" or using company resources for personal gain.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 183 — Use of information—civil obligations

**Plain-English summary:**
Prohibits current and former officers/employees from improperly using information obtained through their role to gain an advantage or cause detriment to the company.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A person who obtains information because they are or have been a director, other officer or employee of a corporation must not improperly use the information to:
> (a) gain an advantage for themselves or someone else; or
> (b) cause detriment to the corporation.

**Citation format:** s 183 Corporations Act 2001 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Other statutes: Common law duty of confidentiality.

**Currency:**
- Last amended: 2001

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Prevents "insider" use of company data or trade secrets after departure.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

### Section 588G — Director's duty to prevent insolvent trading by company

**Plain-English summary:**
Imposes a personal liability on directors if they allow a company to incur debt while it is insolvent, or if incurring the debt makes it insolvent, provided there were reasonable grounds for suspicion.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) This section applies if: (a) a person is a director... (b) the company is insolvent... (c) there are reasonable grounds for suspecting that the company is insolvent...
> (2) By failing to prevent the company from incurring the debt, the person contravenes this section if: (a) the person is aware... or (b) a reasonable person in a like position... would be so aware.
> (3) [Criminal offence for dishonest failure to prevent insolvent trading].

**Citation format:** s 588G Corporations Act 2001 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Other sections: s 588H (Defences), s 588GA (Safe harbour).
- Leading cases: *ASIC v Plymin* [2003] VSC 123.

**Currency:**
- Last amended: 2017 (Safe harbour intro), 2021 (Small business restructuring).

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Critical for insolvency litigation and advising directors on personal risk during financial distress.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED
