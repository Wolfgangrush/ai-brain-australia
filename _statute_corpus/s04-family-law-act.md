## Section-by-Section Digest

### Section 4AB — Definition of family violence

**Plain-English summary:**
Provides a broad definition of family violence, including physical, sexual, emotional, and economic abuse. Recent amendments explicitly include "economic or financial abuse" and "coercive control".

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) For the purposes of this Act, family violence means violent, threatening or other behaviour by a person that coerces or controls a member of the person’s family (the family member), or causes the family member to be fearful.
> (2) Examples... include (but are not limited to): (h) economic or financial abuse; (i) preventing the family member from making or keeping connections...
> (2A) [Detailed definition of economic abuse including sabotaging employment or withholding support].

**Citation format:** s 4AB Family Law Act 1975 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Other sections: s 60CC (Best interests safety factor), s 79(4)(ca) (Effect of violence on contributions).

**Currency:**
- Last amended: June 2025 (Economic abuse expansion).

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Central to both parenting (safety) and property (contributions/future needs) disputes.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

### Section 60B — Objects of Part VII (Parenting)

**Plain-English summary:**
States the primary goals of parenting orders: ensuring children's best interests (prioritizing safety) and giving effect to the Convention on the Rights of the Child.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> The objects of this Part are:
> (a) to ensure that the best interests of children are met, including by ensuring their safety; and
> (b) to give effect to the Convention on the Rights of the Child...

**Citation format:** s 60B Family Law Act 1975 (Cth)

**Currency:**
- Last amended: May 2024 (Simplified to focus on safety).

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Used to frame the overarching argument in parenting applications.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 60CA — Child’s best interests paramount consideration

**Plain-English summary:**
The "Golden Thread" of family law: the best interests of the child must be the most important factor in any parenting decision.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> In deciding whether to make a particular parenting order in relation to a child, a court must regard the best interests of the child as the paramount consideration.

**Citation format:** s 60CA Family Law Act 1975 (Cth)

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 60CC — How a court determines what is in a child’s best interests

**Plain-English summary:**
Lists the factors the court *must* consider when deciding what is in a child's best interests. The 2024 reform replaced the old "primary/additional" hierarchy with a streamlined list prioritizing safety.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) In determining what is in the child’s best interests, the court must consider the matters set out in subsection (2).
> (2) The matters are: (a) what arrangements would best promote the safety... (b) any views expressed by the child; (c) the developmental, psychological, emotional and cultural needs... (d) capacity of each person... (e) benefit to the child of being able to have a relationship with parents... where it is safe to do so...
> (3) [Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander cultural rights].

**Citation format:** s 60CC Family Law Act 1975 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Leading cases: *M v M* [1988] HCA 68 (Child abuse allegations).

**Currency:**
- Last amended: May 2024 (Major overhaul).

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- The checklist for drafting any parenting affidavit. Every point in s 60CC(2) should be addressed.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 61D — Parenting orders and parental responsibility

**Plain-English summary:**
Clarifies that parenting orders confer responsibility only as specified. Crucially, the old *presumption* of "equal shared parental responsibility" (former s 61DA) has been repealed.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A parenting order confers parental responsibility... only to the extent to which the order confers on the person duties, powers, responsibilities or authority...
> (3) A parenting order that deals with the allocation of responsibility for making decisions about major long-term issues... may provide for joint or sole decision-making...

**Citation format:** s 61D Family Law Act 1975 (Cth)

**Currency:**
- Last amended: May 2024 (Repeal of s 61DA presumption).

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Shift in strategy: Advocates no longer start from a baseline of "equal" responsibility; they must argue for the specific allocation that serves the child's best interests.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 79 — Alteration of property interests

**Plain-English summary:**
Gives the court power to redistribute the property of the parties. Requires the court to be satisfied the order is "just and equitable".

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (2) The court must not make an order... unless it is satisfied that, in all the circumstances, it is just and equitable to make the order.
> (4) [Contributions factors]: (ca) the effect of any family violence... on the ability of a party... to make contributions...
> (5) [Future needs factors]: (a) the effect of any family violence... on the current and future circumstances... (d) the effect of any material wastage...

**Citation format:** s 79 Family Law Act 1975 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Leading cases: *Stanford v Stanford* [2012] HCA 52 (Just and equitable requirement).

**Currency:**
- Last amended: June 2025 (Property settlement framework codification).

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- The engine room for property settlements. The new explicit mention of family violence (ss 4ca and 5a) is a major tool for victims whose "contributions" were hindered by abuse.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 79AD — Orders with respect to companion animals (Pets)

**Plain-English summary:**
A brand new framework for deciding who gets the pet, moving away from "property" logic to a list of factors including who cared for the animal and any attachment by children.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) In considering... ownership of... a companion animal, the court is to take into account: (a) circumstances of acquisition; (b) who has possession; (c) care/maintenance; (d) family violence; (f) attachment by a party or child...

**Citation format:** s 79AD Family Law Act 1975 (Cth)

**Currency:**
- Last amended: June 2025 (Newly introduced).

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Solves a very common and emotionally charged point of contention for clients.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED
