## Section-by-Section Digest

### Section 6 — Designated Services (Professional Services)

**Plain-English summary:**
Defines the activities that bring a person under the Act's regulations. Table 6 (newly expanded) specifically captures lawyers and accountants when they perform "gatekeeper" tasks like transferring real estate or managing client funds.

**Statutory text (summary of Table 6 items):**
- Item 1: Assist in purchase/sale of real estate.
- Item 2: Manage client funds, securities, or other assets.
- Item 3: Create, operate, or manage companies or trusts.
- Item 4: Provide a registered office/business address.

**Citation format:** s 6 AML/CTF Act 2006 (Cth)

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 32 — Customer identification procedure (KYC)

**Plain-English summary:**
Reporting entities (now including law firms providing designated services) must identify their customers *before* providing the service. This is the "Know Your Customer" (KYC) requirement.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A reporting entity must not provide a designated service to a customer unless:
> (a) the reporting entity has adopted an AML/CTF program... and
> (b) the reporting entity has carried out the applicable customer identification procedure...

**Citation format:** s 32 AML/CTF Act 2006 (Cth)

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Law firms must now implement formal ID verification processes for conveyancing and commercial transactions, similar to banks.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 41 — Reports of suspicious matters (SMRs)

**Plain-English summary:**
If a reporting entity suspects on reasonable grounds that a service relates to a crime (money laundering, tax evasion, etc.), they must file a "Suspicious Matter Report" (SMR) with AUSTRAC.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) This section applies... if: (a) the reporting entity provides... a designated service... and (b) the reporting entity suspects on reasonable grounds that: (i) the customer is not who they claim to be... or (ii) information... may be relevant to the investigation... of an offence...
> (2) ...must give the AUSTRAC CEO a report... within 3 business days [or 24 hours for terrorism].

**Citation format:** s 41 AML/CTF Act 2006 (Cth)

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 242 — Legal Professional Privilege (LPP)

**Plain-English summary:**
Explicitly preserves Legal Professional Privilege. Lawyers are not required to provide information to AUSTRAC if it is protected by LPP. Note: General "client confidentiality" is NOT a defense against disclosure.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) This Act does not affect the law relating to legal professional privilege.
> (2) To avoid doubt, a person is not required to give information or produce a document under this Act if the information or document is subject to legal professional privilege.
> (3) However, a person is not entitled to refuse... on the ground of a duty of confidence.

**Citation format:** s 242 AML/CTF Act 2006 (Cth)

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- The critical boundary for Tranche 2 compliance. You must report suspicious *facts* of a transaction, but you do not disclose the *legal advice* given to the client.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED
