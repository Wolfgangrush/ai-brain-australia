## Section-by-Section Digest

### Section 116 — General power to cancel visas

**Plain-English summary:**
Provides the Minister with discretionary power to cancel a visa for reasons including: the circumstances for the grant no longer exist, non-compliance with conditions, or the holder posing a risk to the community.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) ...the Minister may cancel a visa if he or she is satisfied that:
> (a) any circumstances which permitted the grant... no longer exist;
> (b) its holder has not complied with a condition...
> (e) the presence of its holder in Australia is or may be... a risk to: (i) the health, safety or good order of the Australian community... or (ii) the health or safety of an individual...

**Citation format:** s 116 Migration Act 1958 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Other sections: s 128 (Cancellation of visas of people outside Australia), s 133A (Minister's personal power to cancel).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

### Section 501 — Refusal or cancellation on character grounds

**Plain-English summary:**
The "Character Test". Allows the Minister to refuse or cancel a visa if they suspect the person isn't of good character. Includes **mandatory** cancellation for substantial criminal records (s 501(3A)). 2026 amendments added grounds for "prohibited hate groups" and "hate-motivated conduct".

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) The Minister may refuse... if the Minister reasonably suspects that the person does not pass the character test...
> (3A) The Minister **must** cancel a visa... if: (a) the person does not pass the character test because... (i) substantial criminal record... (ii) sexually based offences involving a child...
> (6) [The Character Test]: ... (a) substantial criminal record... (bb) [New 2026] association with a prohibited hate group... (bc) [New 2026] engaged in hate-motivated conduct...

**Citation format:** s 501 Migration Act 1958 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Other sections: s 501CA (Revocation of mandatory cancellation).
- Leading cases: *Minister for Home Affairs v Falzon* [2018] HCA 2.

**Currency:**
- Last amended: January 2026 (Hate speech/extremism grounds).

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- High-stakes litigation. Mandatory cancellation (3A) occurs automatically on sentencing for ≥12 months prison; the advocate must then fight for revocation under s 501CA.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

### Section 338 — Definition of ART reviewable decisions

**Plain-English summary:**
Defines which decisions can be reviewed by the Administrative Review Tribunal (ART). Crucially, many decisions made personally by the Minister (especially under s 501) are **excluded** from ART review and must go to the Federal Court.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A decision is an ART reviewable decision if: (a) refuse to grant... (b) cancel... (c) refuse to revoke...
> **and the decision is not:** ... (f) a decision to which **section 501** applies...

**Citation format:** s 338 Migration Act 1958 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Other sections: s 476 (Jurisdiction of the Federal Circuit and Family Court).

**Currency:**
- Last amended: 2024 (ART transition).

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Used to determine the correct "pathway" for a challenge. If it's 338-reviewable, go to the ART. If it's excluded, look for jurisdictional error in the Federal Court.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED
