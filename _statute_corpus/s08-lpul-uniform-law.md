## Section-by-Section Digest

### Section 297 — Professional misconduct

**Plain-English summary:**
Defines the highest level of lawyer misconduct. Includes consistent failures in competence or conduct that shows a person is not "fit and proper" to practice law.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) For the purposes of this Law, professional misconduct includes—
> (a) unsatisfactory professional conduct... where the conduct involves a substantial or consistent failure to reach or maintain a reasonable standard of competence and diligence; and
> (b) conduct... that would, if established, justify a finding that the lawyer is not a fit and proper person to engage in legal practice.

**Citation format:** s 297 Legal Profession Uniform Law

**Cross-references:**
- Other sections: s 296 (Unsatisfactory professional conduct), s 298 (Conduct capable of constituting misconduct).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 298 — Conduct capable of constituting misconduct

**Plain-English summary:**
Provides a non-exhaustive list of behaviors that can be prosecuted as misconduct, including breaking any part of the Uniform Law or Rules, or being convicted of a serious offence.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> Without limitation, the following conduct is capable of constituting unsatisfactory professional conduct or professional misconduct—
> (a) conduct consisting of a contravention of this Law...
> (b) conduct consisting of a contravention of the Uniform Rules;
> (c) conduct involving conviction for—(i) a serious offence; (ii) a tax offence; (iii) an offence involving dishonesty...

**Citation format:** s 298 Legal Profession Uniform Law

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

### ASCR Rule 3 — Paramount duty to the court

**Plain-English summary:**
The core ethical rule for all Australian solicitors: your first duty is to the court and the system of justice. This duty overrides even your duty to your client.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> 3.1 A solicitor’s duty to the court and the administration of justice is paramount and prevails to the extent of inconsistency with any other duty.

**Citation format:** r 3 Australian Solicitors' Conduct Rules 2015

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Must be cited when explaining to a client why you cannot follow an instruction that would mislead the court (e.g., filing a false affidavit).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### ASCR Rule 4 — Other fundamental ethical duties

**Plain-English summary:**
Lists the "Big Five" duties: act in the client's best interests, be honest/courteous, be competent/diligent, maintain integrity/independence, and comply with the law.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> 4.1 A solicitor must also:
> 4.1.1 act in the best interests of a client...
> 4.1.2 be honest and courteous in all dealings...
> 4.1.3 deliver legal services competently, diligently and as promptly as reasonably possible;
> 4.1.4 avoid any compromise to their integrity and professional independence; and
> 4.1.5 comply with these Rules and the law.

**Citation format:** r 4 Australian Solicitors' Conduct Rules 2015

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### ASCR Rule 9 — Confidentiality

**Plain-English summary:**
Solictors must keep client info secret. Lists the very limited exceptions where you can break that secret (e.g., client gives permission, law requires it, or to prevent a serious crime).

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> 9.1 A solicitor must not disclose any information which is confidential to a client and acquired by the solicitor during the client’s engagement...
> 9.2 A solicitor may disclose... if: 9.2.1 the client expressly or impliedly authorises... 9.2.2 permitted or compelled by law... 9.2.4 for the sole purpose of avoiding the probable commission of a serious criminal offence...

**Citation format:** r 9 Australian Solicitors' Conduct Rules 2015

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### ASCR Rule 10 — Conflicts concerning former clients

**Plain-English summary:**
Prevents a lawyer from acting against a former client if they have confidential info about them that would be helpful to the new client and harmful to the old one.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> 10.1 A solicitor and law practice must avoid conflicts between the duties owed to current and former clients...
> 10.2 A solicitor... in possession of information which is confidential to a former client where that information might reasonably be concluded to be material to the matter of another client and detrimental to the interests of the former client... must not act... UNLESS: 10.2.1 informed written consent... 10.2.2 effective information barrier...

**Citation format:** r 10 Australian Solicitors' Conduct Rules 2015

**STATUS:** VERIFIED
