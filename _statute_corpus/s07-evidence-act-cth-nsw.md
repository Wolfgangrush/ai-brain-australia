## Section-by-Section Digest (Uniform Evidence Law)

### Section 55 — Relevant evidence

**Plain-English summary:**
The first hurdle for any evidence: it must be "relevant". Relevance is defined as anything that could "rationally affect" the assessment of a fact in issue.

**Statutory text (verbatim - both Cth/NSW):**
> (1) The evidence that is relevant in a proceeding is evidence that, if it were accepted, could rationally affect (directly or indirectly) the assessment of the probability of the existence of a fact in issue in the proceeding.

**Citation format:** s 55 Evidence Act 1995 (Cth) | s 55 Evidence Act 1995 (NSW)

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 59 — The hearsay rule

**Plain-English summary:**
Prohibits using a "previous representation" (something said/written out of court) to prove the truth of what was asserted. The rule is intended to ensure witnesses can be cross-examined.

**Statutory text (verbatim - both Cth/NSW):**
> (1) Evidence of a previous representation made by a person is not admissible to prove the existence of a fact that it can reasonably be supposed that the person intended to assert by the representation.

**Citation format:** s 59 Evidence Act 1995 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Exceptions: s 60 (Non-hearsay purpose), s 63/64 (Maker unavailable/available), s 65 (Criminal exceptions).
- Leading cases: *Lee v The Queen* [1998] HCA 60.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 76 — The opinion rule

**Plain-English summary:**
General rule that witnesses should testify to facts, not their "opinions".

**Statutory text (verbatim - both Cth/NSW):**
> (1) Evidence of an opinion is not admissible to prove the existence of a fact about the existence of which the opinion was expressed.

**Citation format:** s 76 Evidence Act 1995 (Cth)

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 79 — Exception: opinions based on specialised knowledge

**Plain-English summary:**
Allows "expert evidence" if the person has specialized knowledge based on training, study, or experience. **Crucial Jurisdictional Difference:** NSW explicitly includes child development/behavior in s 79(2).

**Statutory text (verbatim - Cth):**
> (1) If a person has specialised knowledge based on the person's training, study or experience, the opinion rule does not apply to evidence of an opinion... wholly or substantially based on that knowledge.

**Statutory text (verbatim - NSW Addition):**
> (2) "Specialised knowledge" includes specialised knowledge of child development and child behaviour...

**Citation format:** s 79 Evidence Act 1995 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Leading cases: *Dasreef Pty Ltd v Hawchar* [2011] HCA 21 (Requirement for a logical link between knowledge and opinion).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 97 — The tendency rule

**Plain-English summary:**
Prohibits using evidence of character/conduct to prove a "tendency" to act in a certain way, unless reasonable notice is given and the evidence has "significant probative value".

**Statutory text (verbatim - both Cth/NSW):**
> (1) Evidence of the character... or a tendency... is not admissible to prove that a person has or had a tendency... to act in a particular way... if: (a) reasonable notice [not given]... or (b) the court thinks... would not... have significant probative value.

**Citation format:** s 97 Evidence Act 1995 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- NSW Specific: s 97A (Presumption of significant probative value for child sexual offences).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 118 — Legal advice (Client Legal Privilege)

**Plain-English summary:**
Protects confidential communications between a client and lawyer if the "dominant purpose" was providing legal advice.

**Statutory text (verbatim - both Cth/NSW):**
> Evidence is not to be adduced if... the court finds that adducing the evidence would result in disclosure of: (a) a confidential communication... for the dominant purpose of the lawyer... providing legal advice to the client.

**Citation format:** s 118 Evidence Act 1995 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Other sections: s 119 (Litigation privilege), s 121-126 (Loss of privilege).
- Leading cases: *Esso Australia Resources Ltd v Commissioner of Taxation* [1999] HCA 67 (Adoption of "dominant purpose" test).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 131 — Exclusion of evidence of settlement negotiations

**Plain-English summary:**
Protects "without prejudice" communications made during attempts to settle a dispute. Encourages settlement by ensuring parties aren't penalized for compromises.

**Statutory text (verbatim - both Cth/NSW):**
> (1) Evidence is not to be adduced of: (a) a communication that is made between persons in dispute... in connection with an attempt to negotiate a settlement...

**Citation format:** s 131 Evidence Act 1995 (Cth)

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 135 — General discretion to exclude evidence

**Plain-English summary:**
The "unfairly prejudicial" rule. The Court can throw out otherwise admissible evidence if it's too risky/time-wasting relative to its value.

**Statutory text (verbatim - both Cth/NSW):**
> The court may refuse to admit evidence if its probative value is substantially outweighed by the danger that the evidence might: (a) be unfairly prejudicial... (b) be misleading... (c) cause... undue waste of time.

**Citation format:** s 135 Evidence Act 1995 (Cth)

**STATUS:** VERIFIED
