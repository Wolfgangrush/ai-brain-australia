## Section-by-Section Digest

### Section 40 — Acts of bankruptcy

**Plain-English summary:**
Lists the events that allow a creditor to file for a debtor's bankruptcy. The most common "act of bankruptcy" is failing to comply with a Bankruptcy Notice (s 40(1)(g)).

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A debtor commits an act of bankruptcy in each of the following cases: ...
> (g) if a creditor who has obtained... a final judgment... has served on the debtor... a bankruptcy notice... and the debtor does not... (i) within the time specified... comply with the requirements... or satisfy the Court that he or she has a counter-claim, set-off or cross demand equal to or exceeding the amount...

**Citation format:** s 40 Bankruptcy Act 1966 (Cth)

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

### Section 41 — Bankruptcy notices

**Plain-English summary:**
Specifies the requirements for a valid Bankruptcy Notice. It must be based on a final judgment for at least the "statutory minimum" ($10,000) and give the debtor 21 days to pay.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (2A) The notice must specify a period for compliance...: (a) if the notice is served in Australia—the period of 21 days after service...
> (3) A bankruptcy notice shall not be issued...: (a) except on the application of a creditor who has obtained... a final judgment... for an amount of at least the statutory minimum [$10,000].

**Citation format:** s 41 Bankruptcy Act 1966 (Cth)

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 44 — Conditions on which creditor may petition

**Plain-English summary:**
The criteria for a creditor to file a petition in court for a Sequestration Order (making the debtor bankrupt). The debt must be at least $10,000 (prescribed amount) and the act of bankruptcy must have occurred within the last 6 months.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A creditor's petition shall not be presented... unless:
> (a) the debt... amounts... to $5,000 or, if a greater amount is prescribed, that greater amount [currently $10,000];
> (b) the debt... is a liquidated sum...
> (c) the act of bankruptcy... was committed within 6 months before the presentation...

**Citation format:** s 44 Bankruptcy Act 1966 (Cth)

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 116 — Property divisible among creditors (Asset Protection)

**Plain-English summary:**
Defines what property the trustee can take and what the bankrupt is allowed to keep. Bankrupts generally keep household property, tools of trade (up to $4,450), and a vehicle (up to $9,600 equity).

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) ...property divisible among the creditors... includes: (a) all property that belonged to... the bankrupt at the commencement... or acquired... before his or her discharge...
> (2) Subsection (1) does not extend to: (a) property held... in trust... (b) household property... (c) property... used... in earning income... [up to limit]; (ca) property used... as a means of transport... [up to limit]; (f) the interest... in a regulated superannuation fund...

**Citation format:** s 116 Bankruptcy Act 1966 (Cth)

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 120-122 — Clawback provisions (Undervalued / Preferential)

**Plain-English summary:**
Allows a trustee to "claw back" property that was transferred away before bankruptcy to prevent creditors from getting it. s 120 covers undervalued sales; s 121 covers transfers intended to defeat creditors; s 122 covers payments that gave one creditor an unfair preference over others.

**Statutory text (summary):**
- **s 120:** Transfers for less than market value within 4-5 years.
- **s 121:** Transfers where the main purpose was to prevent/delay property becoming divisible.
- **s 122:** Transfers to a creditor that gave them a preference over other creditors (within 6 months).

**Citation format:** ss 120-122 Bankruptcy Act 1966 (Cth)

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Critical for advising on "pre-insolvency" planning (what not to do) and for trustees seeking to recover assets.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED
