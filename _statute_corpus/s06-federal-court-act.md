## Section-by-Section Digest

### Section 37M — Overarching purpose of civil practice and procedure

**Plain-English summary:**
Mandates that the Court and parties facilitate the "just resolution of disputes" according to law and as "quickly, inexpensively and efficiently as possible". This is the "North Star" for all Federal Court litigation.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) The overarching purpose of the civil practice and procedure provisions is to facilitate the just resolution of disputes: (a) according to law; and (b) as quickly, inexpensively and efficiently as possible.
> (2) ...includes: (a) just determination... (b) efficient use of resources... (e) resolution at a cost that is proportionate to importance and complexity...

**Citation format:** s 37M Federal Court of Australia Act 1976 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Other sections: s 37N (Parties' duty).
- Leading cases: *Expense Reduction Analysts Group Pty Ltd v Armstrong Strategic Management and Marketing Pty Limited* [2013] HCA 46 (High Court endorsement of the "overarching purpose" in similar NSW legislation).

**Currency:**
- Last amended: 2009 (Insertion of overarching purpose).

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Used to argue against obstructive behavior, excessive discovery, or unnecessary delays. The Court is increasingly willing to use this section to curb "standard" litigation tactics that are disproportionately expensive.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

### Section 37N — Parties' duty to cooperate

**Plain-English summary:**
Imposes a legal duty on parties and their lawyers to conduct proceedings in a way consistent with the overarching purpose in s 37M. Lawyers must assist clients to comply, and cannot pass on personal costs orders to clients.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) The parties to a civil proceeding before the Court must conduct the proceeding... in a way that is consistent with the overarching purpose.
> (2) A party's lawyer must... assist the party to comply...
> (4) If the Court or a Judge orders a lawyer to bear costs personally... the lawyer must not pass on those costs to the lawyer's client.

**Citation format:** s 37N Federal Court of Australia Act 1976 (Cth)

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 51A — Interest on judgments

**Plain-English summary:**
Ensures that judgment debts carry interest from the date the judgment is entered, at a rate set by the Rules of Court (usually 6% above the cash rate).

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A judgment debt under a judgment of the Court carries interest from the date as of which the judgment is entered.
> (2) The interest is at the rate for the time being fixed by the Rules of Court.

**Citation format:** s 51A Federal Court of Australia Act 1976 (Cth)

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Rule 1.32 — Court may make any order appropriate in interests of justice

**Plain-English summary:**
Provides the Court with a "blanket" power to make any order it deems necessary for justice, even if it contradicts the formal Rules of Court.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> The Court may make any order that the Court considers appropriate in the interests of justice, including an order that is inconsistent with these Rules.

**Citation format:** r 1.32 Federal Court Rules 2011

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- The "ultimate safety net" for procedural fairness. Used when the strict application of the rules would lead to an absurd or unjust outcome.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Rule 8.01 — Starting proceeding—application

**Plain-English summary:**
Sets out the mandatory method for starting a Federal Court case: filing an "Originating Application" (Form 15) accompanied by a Statement of Claim or an Affidavit.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A proceeding must be started by filing an originating application in accordance with Form 15.
> (2) An originating application must be accompanied by: (a) a statement of claim; or (b) an affidavit.

**Citation format:** r 8.01 Federal Court Rules 2011

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Rule 10.42 — Service outside Australia

**Plain-English summary:**
Specifies the types of cases where the Federal Court can permit service on a defendant located outside Australia. 2023 amendments greatly simplified this "long-arm" jurisdiction.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> An originating application may be served on a person in a foreign country... [if the claim relates to]:
> 1. Cause of action arising in Australia.
> 2. Breach in Australia of a contract.
> 3. Contract made in or governed by the law of Australia.
> 4. Tortious act/omission in Australia or damage sustained in Australia.
> 10. Proceeding brought under the Corporations Act 2001.
> 17. Proceeding brought under the Competition and Consumer Act 2010.

**Citation format:** r 10.42 Federal Court Rules 2011

**Currency:**
- Last amended: 2023 (Major harmonisation with state court rules).

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Essential for international commercial disputes, especially in the digital/online space.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED
