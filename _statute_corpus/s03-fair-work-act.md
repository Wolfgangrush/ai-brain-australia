## Section-by-Section Digest

### Section 15AA — Determining the ordinary meanings of employee and employer

**Plain-English summary:**
Introduces a "real substance" test to determine if someone is an employee or a contractor. Overrides recent High Court decisions that prioritized written contracts, returning to a multifactorial test that includes how the work is actually performed.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) For the purposes of this Act, whether an individual is an employee... is to be determined by reference to the real substance, practical reality and true nature of the relationship...
> (2) ...regard must be had not only to the terms of the contract... but also to other factors... including, but not limited to, how the contract is performed in practice.

**Citation format:** s 15AA Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Leading cases: *ZG Operations Australia Pty Ltd v Jamsek* [2022] HCA 2 (Preceding common law approach); *Construction, Forestry, Maritime, Mining and Energy Union v Personnel Monitoring Pty Ltd* [2022] HCA 1 (Preceding common law approach).

**Currency:**
- Last amended: August 2024 (Closing Loopholes No. 2)

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Critical for sham contracting claims and determining eligibility for unfair dismissal or leave entitlements.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

### Section 333M — Employee right to disconnect

**Plain-English summary:**
Gives employees a legal right to refuse to monitor or respond to work-related contact outside of working hours, unless the refusal is unreasonable.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) An employee may refuse to monitor, read or respond to contact... (a) from their employer... or (b) a third party... outside of the employee’s working hours; unless the refusal is unreasonable.
> (2) [Factors for unreasonableness]: (a) reason for contact; (b) disruption caused; (c) compensation/availability pay; (d) nature of role; (e) personal circumstances.

**Citation format:** s 333M Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Other sections: s 333P (FWC orders to stop refusing contact / stop contacting).

**Currency:**
- Last amended: August 2024 (Commenced Aug 2024 for large businesses; Aug 2025 for small businesses).

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- New ground for workplace disputes regarding "always-on" culture. Can lead to FWC stop orders.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 340 — Protection (General Protections)

**Plain-English summary:**
Prohibits taking "adverse action" against a person because they have or have exercised a "workplace right". This is a "strict liability" style provision where the burden of proof shifts to the employer to prove the action was *not* for a prohibited reason.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A person must not take adverse action against another person:
> (a) because the other person has a workplace right; or
> (b) because the other person has, or has not, exercised a workplace right; or
> (c) because the other person proposes to... exercise a workplace right...

**Citation format:** s 340 Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Other sections: s 341 (Workplace right), s 342 (Adverse action), s 361 (Reverse onus of proof).

**Currency:**
- Last amended: 2009

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- The most powerful tool for non-dismissal disputes or when a person is not eligible for unfair dismissal (e.g. high income or short service).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 341 — Meaning of workplace right

**Plain-English summary:**
Defines what constitutes a "workplace right", including being entitled to a benefit under a law (e.g. leave) or the ability to make a complaint or inquiry about employment.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) A person has a workplace right if the person:
> (a) is entitled to the benefit of... a workplace law...
> (b) is able to initiate... a process or proceeding...
> (c) is able to make a complaint or inquiry: ... (ii) if the person is an employee—in relation to his or her employment.

**Citation format:** s 341 Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Leading cases: *Shea v TRUenergy Services Pty Ltd (No 6)* [2014] FCA 271 (Complaint must be underpinned by a genuine belief).

**Currency:**
- Last amended: 2009

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Broad definition. Even asking a question about a payslip is a workplace right.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 342 — Meaning of adverse action

**Plain-English summary:**
Defines what "adverse action" is, including dismissal, injuring the employee in their employment, or altering their position to their prejudice.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) The following table sets out circumstances...
> Item 1 (Employer against employee): (a) dismisses the employee; (b) injures the employee in his or her employment; (c) alters the position of the employee to the employee’s prejudice; (d) discriminates...

**Citation format:** s 342 Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Leading cases: *Board of Bendigo Regional Institute of Technical and Further Education v Barclay* [2012] HCA 32.

**Currency:**
- Last amended: 2009

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- Includes "soft" actions like warnings or demotions, not just termination.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 382 — When a person is protected from unfair dismissal

**Plain-English summary:**
Sets the eligibility criteria for unfair dismissal: completion of minimum employment period (6 or 12 months) and either being covered by an award/agreement or earning below the high income threshold.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> A person is protected from unfair dismissal if, at the time of being dismissed:
> (a) the person is an employee who has completed a period of employment... of at least the minimum employment period; and
> (b) one or more of the following apply: (i) a modern award covers the person; (ii) an enterprise agreement applies... (iii) the sum of the person’s annual rate of earnings... is less than the high income threshold.

**Citation format:** s 382 Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Other sections: s 383 (Minimum employment period), s 333N (High income threshold).

**Currency:**
- Last amended: 2009

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- The "gatekeeper" section. Check this first before filing any unfair dismissal claim.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 385 — What is an unfair dismissal

**Plain-English summary:**
The core definition of unfair dismissal: a dismissal that is "harsh, unjust or unreasonable", not a genuine redundancy, and (for small business) inconsistent with the Small Business Fair Dismissal Code.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> A person has been unfairly dismissed if the FWC is satisfied that:
> (a) the person has been dismissed; and
> (b) the dismissal was harsh, unjust or unreasonable; and
> (c) the dismissal was not a case of genuine redundancy; and
> (d) the dismissal was not consistent with the Small Business Fair Dismissal Code...

**Citation format:** s 385 Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Other sections: s 387 (Criteria for considering harshness), s 389 (Meaning of genuine redundancy).

**Currency:**
- Last amended: 2009

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- The substantive test for any unfair dismissal claim.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED

---

### Section 327A — Offence—failing to pay certain amounts (Wage Theft)

**Plain-English summary:**
Criminalises the intentional failure to pay employees their correct entitlements under the Act or a fair work instrument.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> (1) An employer commits an offence if:
> (a) the employer is required to pay an amount to... an employee under: (i) this Act; or (ii) a fair work instrument...; and
> (b) the employer fails to pay the amount... in full...; and
> (c) the employer’s conduct that constitutes the failure is intentional.

**Citation format:** s 327A Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth)

**Cross-references:**
- Other sections: s 327B (Penalties for wage theft).

**Currency:**
- Last amended: January 2025 (Commenced 1 Jan 2025).

**Practical use for solo advocate:**
- A high-stakes provision. Used to threaten/pursue criminal action for egregious and intentional non-payment.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED
