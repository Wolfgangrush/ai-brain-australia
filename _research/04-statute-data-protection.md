## Key Facts
1. **13 Australian Privacy Principles (APPs):** Set out standards for collection, use, disclosure, and security of personal information.
2. **APP 8 (Cross-border disclosure):** An entity is generally accountable for the acts of an overseas recipient (the "deemed liability" principle) unless specific exceptions apply.
3. **Small Business Exemption:** Generally, businesses with <$3m turnover are exempt, BUT this exemption does NOT apply to "health service providers" (which includes many legal practices handling health data) or businesses that trade in personal info.
4. **Notifiable Data Breaches (NDB) Scheme:** Mandatory reporting to OAIC and individuals if a breach is likely to result in "serious harm."
5. **Section 77 My Health Records Act:** Explicitly prohibits holding or taking My Health Record data outside Australia. This is a strict localization requirement.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"Before an APP entity discloses personal information about an individual to an overseas recipient, the entity must take such steps as are reasonable in the circumstances to ensure that the overseas recipient does not breach the Australian Privacy Principles..." — APP 8.1

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Solos must audit their tech stack (SaaS, Cloud storage) for APP 8 compliance.
- If handling personal injury or insurance matters (health data), the solo practitioner is NOT exempt from the Privacy Act regardless of turnover.
- Use of offshore VAs or paralegals requires strict APP 8 "reasonable steps" (contracts/vetting).
- My Health Record data MUST NOT be stored on overseas servers (e.g., standard US-based cloud storage without local region locking).
