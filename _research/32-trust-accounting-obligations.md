## Currency Warning
Trust accounting is a "zero tolerance" area for regulators.

## Key Facts
1. **Trust Money:** Money received in advance for legal costs, or money held on behalf of a client for a transaction (e.g., settlement funds).
2. **The "Trust Account":** Must be held at an "Approved Deposit-taking Institution" (ADI).
3. **Withdrawal:** Can only withdraw for legal costs *after* a bill has been sent and the 7-day objection period has passed (or by agreement).
4. **Record Keeping:** Must maintain receipts, deposit records, cash books, and ledgers. Must reconcile monthly.
5. **Annual Audit:** Every law practice with a trust account must have it audited annually by an external examiner.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"A law practice must not receive trust money unless a principal of the law practice holds an Australian practising certificate authorising the receipt of trust money." — Section 150, LPUL.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Solos often choose NOT to have a trust account ("No Trust" practice) to avoid the audit costs and complexity; they bill in arrears instead.
- If a solo has a trust account, they MUST use compliant accounting software; manual ledgers are a high risk for errors.
- Trust account errors are the most common cause of disciplinary action and "Striking Off" for solo practitioners.
