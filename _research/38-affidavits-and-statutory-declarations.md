## Currency Warning
**PERMANENT REMOTE WITNESSING:** Following COVID-19 trials, most jurisdictions (including NSW and Commonwealth) have passed laws allowing for permanent remote witnessing of documents via AV link.

## Key Facts
1. **Affidavit:** A written statement of facts for use in court proceedings, sworn or affirmed before an authorized witness (Solicitor, JP).
2. **Statutory Declaration:** A written statement of facts for non-court use (e.g., insurance claims), made under the Statutory Declarations Act.
3. **Authorized Witnesses:** Solicitors are authorized witnesses for both affidavits and statutory declarations Australia-wide.
4. **Remote Witnessing:** Witnessing can occur via real-time AV link (e.g., Zoom/Teams) provided the witness sees the person sign and signs a counterpart or the same document.
5. **Jurat:** The concluding part of the document where the witness certifies the swearing/affirming process.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"A requirement... that a person must witness the signing of a document... is satisfied if the witnessing is done by audio visual link." — Electronic Transactions Amendment Act.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Remote witnessing is a massive time-saver for solos with regional or interstate clients.
- Solos must be careful to use the correct "Remote Witnessing Jurat" required by the specific state's Oaths Act.
- Inaccurate witnessing is a professional misconduct risk; never "pre-sign" or witness without actually seeing the signature.
