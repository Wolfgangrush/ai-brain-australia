2026-05-19

## Authority Tier
Statute / Regulator Guidance

## Currency Warning
Stable under the Privacy Act 1988.

## Key Facts
1. **Disclosure vs. Use:** APP 8 applies to "disclosure" (sending to a third party overseas) rather than "use" (accessing data via a server located overseas where the entity maintains control).
2. **The "Deemed Liability" Rule (s 16C):** If an Australian entity discloses data to an overseas recipient, and the recipient breaches an APP, the Australian entity is deemed to have breached it.
3. **Exceptions:**
    - Recipient is subject to a "substantially similar" law (difficult to prove for US/Asia).
    - Individual provides informed consent to the disclosure.
    - Required by law.
4. **Reasonable Steps:** Usually involves a contract with the overseas recipient that mirrors the APPs.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"An APP entity that discloses personal information to an overseas recipient is responsible for any acts or practices of the overseas recipient that would breach the APPs." — OAIC Summary of s 16C.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Solos using offshore VAs or typing services must have a "Data Processing Agreement" (DPA) that includes APP-equivalent protections.
- Consent: If a solo cannot guarantee a "substantially similar" law, they must explicitly warn the client in the retainer that their data may be sent overseas and may not be protected by Australian privacy laws.
- Low-risk strategy: Keep all data within Australian cloud regions.
