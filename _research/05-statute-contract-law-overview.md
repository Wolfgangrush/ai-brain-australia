## Currency Warning
Australian Consumer Law (ACL) is a uniform law applied at both Federal and State levels.

## Key Facts
1. **Consumer Guarantees:** Automatic guarantees that goods are of "acceptable quality" and services are provided with "due care and skill" (Part 3-2).
2. **Unfair Contract Terms (UCT):** Terms in standard-form "consumer contracts" or "small business contracts" that are unfair may be declared void (Part 2-3). 2023 amendments expanded definitions and introduced heavy penalties.
3. **Misleading or Deceptive Conduct:** A person must not, in trade or commerce, engage in conduct that is misleading or deceptive or is likely to mislead or deceive (s 18).
4. **Unconscionable Conduct:** Prohibition on conduct that is unconscionable within the meaning of the unwritten law (s 20) or in connection with goods/services (s 21).
5. **Remedies:** Broad range including damages, injunctions, and specific performance.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"A person must not, in trade or commerce, engage in conduct that is misleading or deceptive or is likely to mislead or deceive." — Section 18(1), ACL.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Section 18 is the "Swiss Army Knife" of Australian litigation; solos use it for everything from commercial disputes to professional negligence.
- Solos advising small businesses must review standard-form contracts for UCT compliance to avoid significant new penalties.
- Contractual exclusions of liability are often void under ACL Consumer Guarantees.
