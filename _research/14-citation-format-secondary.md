## Key Facts
1. **Books:** Author, *Title* (Publisher, Edition, Year) Pinpoint.
    - Example: J D Heydon, *Cross on Evidence* (LexisNexis, 12th ed, 2019).
2. **Journal Articles:** Author, 'Title' (Year) Volume(Issue) *Journal Title* Starting Page, Pinpoint.
    - Example: Andrew Stewart, 'The Australian Consumer Law' (2010) 38(1) *Federal Law Review* 1.
3. **Reports:** Entity, *Title* (Report Number, Year) Pinpoint.
    - Example: Australian Law Reform Commission, *Elder Abuse—A National Legal Response* (Report No 131, 2017).
4. **Legal Encyclopedias:** 'Title of Entry', *Encyclopedia Title* (at Date) Pinpoint.
    - Example: 'Criminal Law', *Halsbury's Laws of Australia* (at 1 June 2024) [110-5].
5. **Newspapers:** Author, 'Title', *Newspaper* (City), Date, Page.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"Secondary sources should be used to support arguments but are not themselves the law." — AGLC4 Preface.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- When citing a textbook or journal in a submission, the solo practitioner should always verify if a more recent primary source (case/statute) has superseded the commentary.
- Correct secondary citation is particularly important in "Complex Advices" where the law is unsettled.
