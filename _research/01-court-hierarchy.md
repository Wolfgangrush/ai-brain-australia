## Key Facts
1. **High Court of Australia:** The apex of the hierarchy; hears appeals from federal, state, and territory supreme courts. It also has original jurisdiction on constitutional matters.
2. **Federal Court of Australia:** Superior court of record; jurisdiction over most civil matters arising under Commonwealth legislation (e.g., Corporations, IP, Trade Practices).
3. **Federal Circuit and Family Court of Australia (FCFCOA):** Two divisions; Division 1 (Family Law) and Division 2 (General Federal Law/Family Law). Handles high-volume federal matters.
4. **State Supreme Courts:** Highest court in each state/territory; unlimited civil jurisdiction and hears the most serious criminal matters.
5. **District/County Courts:** (Intermediate tier, except in ACT/TAS/NT); hears serious indictable offences and civil matters up to specific limits (e.g., $750k in NSW).
6. **Magistrates/Local Courts:** Lowest tier; handles summary offences, committals for indictable offences, and small civil claims (e.g., up to $100k in NSW).

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The judicial power of the Commonwealth shall be vested in a Federal Supreme Court, to be called the High Court of Australia..." — Section 71, Australian Constitution.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Solo practitioners must correctly identify the jurisdiction (Federal vs. State) based on the governing statute.
- Understanding the appellate path is critical for managing client expectations on litigation finality.
- Most "bread and butter" litigation for solos occurs in the Magistrates/Local Courts or District Courts.
