## Currency Warning
State-based UCPR is stable but frequently updated with new Practice Directions. Federal Rules 2011 remain primary for federal civil matters.

## Key Facts
1. **The Overriding Purpose:** Civil procedure rules (e.g., s 56 NSW CPA, r 1.32 Fed Court Rules) mandate that the court and parties act to facilitate the "just, quick, and cheap" resolution of the real issues in dispute.
2. **Pleadings:** Must be brief and contain only a summary of the material facts on which the party relies (not evidence).
3. **Discovery:** Generally restricted to what is necessary for a fair trial; the court may order "standard discovery" or "limited discovery" by categories.
4. **Summary Judgment:** A party may apply for judgment if the other party has "no reasonable prospect of successfully" defending or prosecuting the claim.
5. **Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR):** Courts have broad powers to refer parties to mediation, often as a mandatory step before trial.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The overriding purpose of this Act and of rules of court... is to facilitate the just, quick and cheap resolution of the real issues in the proceedings." — Section 56(1), Civil Procedure Act 2005 (NSW).

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Solos must aggressively manage discovery to avoid being overwhelmed by larger firms (use "limited discovery" applications).
- Failure to comply with the "overriding purpose" can lead to personal costs orders against the practitioner.
- Mediation is the primary exit point for most civil litigation handled by solos.
