## Key Facts
1. **The "Triage" Morning:** Solos typically start by checking emails/CMS for overnight court orders or urgent client inquiries.
2. **Court Appearances:** If litigating, 9:30 AM - 1:00 PM is often dedicated to "Callovers," "Directions," or "Hearings." Many are now via MS Teams/AV Link.
3. **"Deep Work" Afternoon:** Drafting pleadings, advices, or witness statements. This is the primary "billable" time.
4. **The "Administrative Burden":** Solos spend significant time (20%+) on non-billable tasks: billing, trust accounting, CPD, and IT maintenance.
5. **Business Development:** Client acquisition often happens "after hours" via networking, social media, or referrals.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The life of a solo practitioner is one of constant context-switching between advocate, advisor, and business manager." — Industry Quote.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Time-blocking is essential to prevent administrative tasks from cannibalizing billable work.
- Solos need mobile-friendly tools (iPad/Laptop) to manage practice while at court.
- Automation of "low-value" tasks (billing, data entry) is the difference between a profitable practice and burnout.
