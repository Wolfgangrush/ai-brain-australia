## Currency Warning
Note that in NSW, expiry of the limitation period **extinguishes** the cause of action (s 63), unlike some other jurisdictions where it merely bars the remedy.

## Key Facts
1. **Contract and Tort:** Generally 6 years from the date the cause of action accrued.
2. **Personal Injury:** 3 years from "date of discoverability" (date the plaintiff knew or ought to have known the injury was serious and the defendant was at fault).
3. **Defamation:** 1 year from publication (can be extended to 3 years in limited circumstances).
4. **Deeds:** 12 years (NSW).
5. **Extinguishment (NSW):** Once the period expires, the right itself is gone, not just the ability to sue.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"An action on a cause of action founded on contract... is not maintainable if brought after the expiration of a limitation period of six years running from the date on which the cause of action first accrues..." — Section 14(1), NSW.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Missing a limitation period is the #1 cause of professional negligence claims against solos.
- Solos must implement robust "Statute of Limitations" (SOL) diaries and alerts.
- In PI matters, the "date of discoverability" is often litigated; solos must gather evidence of the client's knowledge early.
