## Key Facts
1. **NCAT (NSW Civil and Administrative Tribunal):** Broad jurisdiction including Consumer/Trader, Guardianship, and Occupational divisions.
2. **VCAT (Victorian Civil and Administrative Tribunal):** Similar structure; handles residential tenancies, planning, and civil disputes.
3. **QCAT (Queensland Civil and Administrative Tribunal):** One-stop-shop for many civil and administrative matters in QLD.
4. **No-Cost Jurisdiction:** Generally, tribunals are "no-cost" jurisdictions (each party pays their own costs) unless there are exceptional circumstances.
5. **Self-Representation:** Designed for self-represented litigants; practitioners often need "leave to appear" (permission from the tribunal) to represent a client.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The Tribunal is to act with as little formality and technicality as the proper consideration of the matter permits." — Section 38, NCAT Act 2013.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Solos often provide "Unbundled Legal Services" for tribunal matters (e.g., drafting submissions but the client appears).
- Understanding the "Leave to Appear" rules is critical; don't assume you can walk into a tribunal hearing and represent a client without permission.
- Tribunals are the primary forum for solo practitioners' consumer law and tenancy cases.
