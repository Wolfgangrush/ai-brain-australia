## Key Facts
1. **Advertising Standards (Rule 36.1):** Advertising must not be false, misleading, deceptive, or offensive.
2. **"Accredited Specialist" (Rule 36.2):** Must not claim to be a specialist unless accredited by a recognized professional association.
3. **Solicitation of Personal Injury Clients:** Strictly regulated by state-based legislation (e.g., *Legal Profession Uniform Law Application Act 2014* (NSW) and *Personal Injury Commission Act 2020* (NSW)) — often prohibits "ambulance chasing" or specific types of online ads.
4. **Professionalism:** Marketing should maintain the dignity of the profession.
5. **Third-party referrals:** Paying for referrals is generally restricted or must be disclosed (Rule 12.4).

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"A solicitor or principal of a law practice must ensure that any advertising, marketing, or promotion in connection with the solicitor or law practice is not: 36.1.1 false; 36.1.2 misleading or deceptive; 36.1.3 offensive; or 36.1.4 prohibited by law." — Rule 36.1

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Solos must be careful when using "growth hacking" or SEO tactics for personal injury practice; state regulators (e.g., LSC) monitor these closely.
- Use of the term "expert" should be backed by objective evidence or replaced with more neutral terms if not an Accredited Specialist.
- Content-led marketing (blogs, AI-generated articles) must be vetted to ensure no "misleading" legal advice is implied.
