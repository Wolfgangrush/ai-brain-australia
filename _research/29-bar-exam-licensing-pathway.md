## Currency Warning
Stable academic (Priestley 11) and PLT requirements.

## Key Facts
1. **Academic (LLB/JD):** Must cover the "Priestley 11" areas of law (e.g., Torts, Contracts, Ethics).
2. **Practical Legal Training (PLT):** A post-degree course covering practical skills (Conveyancing, Civil Practice, Trust Accounting).
3. **"Fit and Proper Person":** Extensive disclosure required of criminal history, academic misconduct, or financial insolvency.
4. **Admission:** Performed by the Supreme Court of a state/territory; once admitted, the lawyer is an "Officer of the Court."
5. **Practising Certificate (PC):** Must be renewed annually; requires PI insurance and payment of the fidelity fund levy.
6. **Barristers' Path:** Requires passing the "Bar Exam," completing the "Bar Practice Course," and 12 months of "Readership" (mentoring).

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The purpose of admission is to ensure that only persons who are sufficiently qualified and of good fame and character are permitted to practice law." — LACC Model Rules.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Solos often start their practice after 2-5 years of post-admission experience (PAE).
- "Restricted PC": New solicitors must practice under supervision for 2 years (or 18 months if PLT completed) before they can practice as a solo (unrestricted PC).
- Continuing to meet the "Fit and Proper" requirement is a lifelong obligation (disclosure of new matters at renewal).
