## Key Facts
1. **Sole Practitioner:** The default for solo solicitors; unlimited personal liability.
2. **Incorporated Legal Practice (ILP):** A company that provides legal services. Benefits include limited liability and tax flexibility.
3. **Director Requirement:** An ILP must have at least one "Legal Practitioner Director" (LPD) who is responsible for the legal services provided.
4. **Notice:** Must notify the Law Society 14 days before starting or stopping an ILP.
5. **Barristers:** Australian barristers are generally prohibited from incorporating; they MUST practice as sole practitioners.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"A corporation is an incorporated legal practice if it is a corporation within the meaning of the Corporations Act and it provides legal services in this jurisdiction." — Section 103, LPUL.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Solo solicitors often start as sole prop and transition to ILP for asset protection and tax advantages as revenue grows.
- Solos must ensure their PI insurance covers the specific business structure (ILP vs. Sole Prop).
- "Shelf companies" cannot be used for ILPs without the 14-day notice and LPD appointment.
