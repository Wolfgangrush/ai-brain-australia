## Key Facts
1. **Current Client Conflict (Rule 11):** A solicitor must avoid acting for two or more clients whose interests are adverse and whose duties to those clients conflict (unless specific informed consent is obtained and an information barrier is possible).
2. **Former Client Conflict (Rule 10):** A solicitor must not act against a former client if they possess confidential information that is material and to the detriment of the former client.
3. **Solicitor's Own Interest (Rule 12):** Must not allow own interests to conflict with those of the client. Includes prohibitions on borrowing from clients or receiving excessive gifts.
4. **Information Barriers:** While technically possible, they are extremely difficult for solo practitioners or small firms to implement effectively.
5. **Informed Consent:** Must be in writing and the client should be advised to seek independent legal advice before consenting.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"A solicitor must avoid conflicts between the duties owed to two or more current clients..." — Rule 11.1.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Solos have no "Information Barrier" capacity; if a conflict exists between two clients, the solo MUST decline to act for one or both.
- Conflict checks are the first mandatory step of any new file; solos should use automated database searches.
- Acting for both parties in a "simple" conveyancing or commercial matter is high-risk and generally discouraged for solos.
