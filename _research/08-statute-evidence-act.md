## Key Facts
1. **Uniform Evidence Law:** The Commonwealth, NSW, VIC, TAS, ACT, and NT have substantially identical evidence acts.
2. **Hearsay Rule (s 59):** Generally prohibits evidence of a previous representation to prove the existence of a fact.
3. **Opinion Rule (s 76):** Generally prohibits opinion evidence unless it falls under exceptions like "Expert Opinion" (s 79).
4. **Legal Professional Privilege (s 118 & 119):** Protects confidential communications for the dominant purpose of providing legal advice or for use in litigation.
5. **Business Records Exception (s 69):** Facilitates the admission of records kept in the course of business without needing the author to testify.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"Evidence of a previous representation made by a person is not admissible to prove the existence of a fact that it can reasonably be supposed that the person intended to assert by the representation." — Section 59(1).

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Solos must ensure client communications are structured to maintain LPP under the "dominant purpose" test.
- Understanding s 69 is vital for admitting electronic evidence (emails, invoices) in small claims litigation.
- Expert evidence (s 79) requires careful instruction letters to satisfy the "specialised knowledge" and "basis" requirements.
