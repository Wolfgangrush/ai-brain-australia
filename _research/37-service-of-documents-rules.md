## Currency Warning
Recent updates allow for electronic service (email) as "ordinary service" in many circumstances.

## Key Facts
1. **Personal Service:** Required for originating processes (Statements of Claim, Subpoenas). Involves leaving a copy with the person.
2. **Ordinary Service:** For subsequent documents (Defences, Notices). Can be by post, DX, or email (if authorized).
3. **Substituted Service:** If personal service is "impracticable," the court can order service by other means (e.g., social media, post to last known address).
4. **Service Overseas:** Governed by the Hague Convention or specific court rules for non-convention countries.
5. **Affidavit of Service:** Mandatory proof that service has occurred according to the rules.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"Personal service is effected by leaving a copy of the document with the person to be served..." — UCPR Rule 10.21.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Solos must use reliable "Process Servers" for personal service to avoid "Service Disputes" later in the matter.
- "Email Service" is a major efficiency gain for solos but must be clearly documented and agreed upon between practitioners.
- Incorrect service is a common technical ground for applications to "Set Aside" default judgments.
