## Currency Warning
AGLC4 is the current standard (2018). Stay tuned for AGLC5 (rumoured for late 2025/2026).

## Key Facts
1. **Case Law (Reported):** *Party A v Party B* (Year) Volume Report Series Starting Page.
    - Example: *Dietrich v The Queen* (1992) 177 CLR 292.
2. **Case Law (Unreported with Media Neutral Citation):** *Party A v Party B* [Year] Court Identifier Judgment Number, [Pinpoint].
    - Example: *Dayal v State of New South Wales* [2024] FedCFamC2F 1166.
3. **Statutes:** *Short Title Year* (Jurisdiction) Pinpoint.
    - Example: *Privacy Act 1988* (Cth) s 8.
4. **Court Hierarchy in Citation:** HCA (High Court), FCA (Federal Court), NSWSC (NSW Supreme Court), VSC (Vic Supreme Court), etc.
5. **Pinpoint References:** Use 's' for section, 'r' for rule, 'cl' for clause, and brackets for paragraphs.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The primary purpose of legal citation is to allow the reader to find the source being cited as quickly and easily as possible." — AGLC4 Introduction.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Automated document generators must handle AGLC4 nuances (e.g., brackets vs. parentheses for years).
- Incorrect citation format can be viewed by the court as a lack of professionalism or competence.
- Solos should use regex-based tools to validate citations before filing.
