## Currency Warning
**CRITICAL:** The Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) was abolished and replaced by the **Administrative Review Tribunal (ART)** on **14 October 2024**. Any reference to AAT in code or docs is outdated.

## Key Facts
1. **The ART:** The new federal body for merits review of administrative decisions by the Australian Government.
2. **Standard of Review:** Merits review (the tribunal stands in the shoes of the original decision-maker to reach the "correct or preferable" decision).
3. **Guidance and Appeals Panel (GAP):** A new feature of the ART to review decisions that raise issues of significance or contain errors.
4. **Transparency:** New requirements for publishing decisions and a more transparent merits-based appointment process for members.
5. **Jurisdiction:** Covers NDIS, Centrelink, Migration, Taxation, and Veterans' Affairs.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The object of this Act is to establish an independent tribunal... to provide a mechanism for review that: (a) is fair and just; (b) is informal, independent and accessible; (c) is timely and efficient..." — Section 3, ART Act 2024.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Solos practicing in migration or NDIS must update all templates from "AAT" to "ART."
- The new Guidance and Appeals Panel (GAP) offers a new strategic avenue for challenging significant decisions without going directly to the Federal Court.
- The ART emphasizes informality, but practitioners must still adhere to the new Practice Directions.
