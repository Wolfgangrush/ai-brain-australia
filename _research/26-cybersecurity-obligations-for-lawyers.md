## Currency Warning
The Essential Eight model is updated regularly (last major update Nov 2023). VLSB+C expectations are the benchmark for "professional competence" in cyber.

## Key Facts
1. **Essential Eight:** A set of technical controls (MFA, Patching, Backups, etc.) designed to mitigate 85% of targeted cyber attacks.
2. **MFA (Multi-factor Authentication):** Now considered a "non-negotiable" standard for email, trust accounts, and CMS.
3. **Patching:** Regulators expect "Extreme" vulnerabilities to be patched within 48 hours.
4. **Duty of Competence:** Failing to implement basic cyber security (like MFA) is increasingly viewed by regulators as a breach of the duty of competence.
5. **Professional Indemnity (PI) Insurance:** Many insurers (e.g., Lawcover) now require a "Cyber Security Checklist" to be completed as part of renewal.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"Legal practitioners have an ethical and professional duty to protect client confidentiality... implementing the Essential Eight is a key part of meeting this duty in a digital environment." — VLSB+C Guidance.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Solos are "soft targets" for business email compromise (BEC).
- Implementation of MFA on *every* system is the single most important defense.
- A cyber breach without "reasonable" security measures may lead to an uninsured loss if the PI insurer determines the solo was grossly negligent.
