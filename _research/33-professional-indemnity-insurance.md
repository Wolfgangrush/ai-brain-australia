## Currency Warning
Policies renew annually on 1 July. Cyber risk is now a mandatory disclosure item for many insurers.

## Key Facts
1. **Mandatory Requirement:** No practitioner can obtain a practicing certificate without approved PI insurance.
2. **Minimum Cover:** Generally $2 million per claim (statutory minimum in most states).
3. **Approved Insurers:** Most states have a "captured" insurer or a list of approved commercial insurers (e.g., Lawcover in NSW, LPLC in Victoria).
4. **Run-off Cover:** When a solo closes their practice, they must maintain "run-off cover" for 7 years to cover potential claims from past work.
5. **Exclusions:** Generally does not cover "fraudulent or dishonest" acts by the practitioner, but may cover "innocent" partners/employees.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"An Australian legal practitioner must not practise law in this jurisdiction unless the practitioner... is covered by an approved insurance policy." — Section 211, LPUL.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- PI Insurance is the single largest fixed overhead for a solo practitioner (after rent).
- Solos must be diligent with "Risk Management" (e.g., using checklists, confirmation letters) to keep premiums low.
- "Run-off" cover costs can be a significant barrier to retirement or closing a solo practice.
