## Currency Warning
The Criminal Code (Schedule 1) is the primary source for Commonwealth offences (e.g., fraud against the Cth, drug importation, cybercrime).

## Key Facts
1. **General Principles:** Part 2.1 codifies the elements of an offence (Physical element + Fault element).
2. **Fault Elements (Mens Rea):** Intention, Knowledge, Recklessness, and Negligence.
3. **Corporate Criminal Responsibility:** Part 2.5 allows a company to be found guilty of an offence based on its "corporate culture."
4. **Cybercrime (Part 10.7):** Covers unauthorized access to data, impairing data, and possession of hacking tools.
5. **Money Laundering (Part 10.2):** Addresses dealing with proceeds of crime (interacts with the AML/CTF Act).

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"An offence consists of physical elements and fault elements." — Section 3.1(1), Criminal Code.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Solos defending federal charges must precisely identify the "Fault Element" required, as it is strictly codified in the Code.
- The "Corporate Culture" provisions are increasingly relevant for solos advising SMEs on compliance and white-collar risk.
- Cybercrime provisions (Part 10.7) are the basis for many modern federal prosecutions handled by specialized solos.
