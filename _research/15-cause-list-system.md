## Currency Warning
Lists are typically updated daily after 4:00 PM for the following day.

## Key Facts
1. **Daily Court Lists:** The official schedule of hearings for a specific day. Includes the judge, time, court room, and matter name.
2. **Case Search:** Tools like "Court List Search" allow practitioners to search by party name or case number to find the next hearing date.
3. **Federal "Daily Lists":** Centralized across the Federal Court and FCFCOA.
4. **Short Notice:** Matters can be added or removed with very little notice; solos must check the list the evening before *and* the morning of a hearing.
5. **Public Access:** Most lists are public, but "Children's Court" or "Closed Court" matters are often suppressed or pseudonymized.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"It is the responsibility of the practitioner to check the Daily Court List to confirm the time and location of their matter." — Registry Notice.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Solos must build a habit of "4:30 PM List Check" to finalize the next day's logistics.
- Use of "Case Tracking" or "RSS Feeds" for court lists can save significant manual search time.
- If a matter is missing from the list, the solo must immediately contact the Associate or Registry.
