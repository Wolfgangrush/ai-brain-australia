## Currency Warning
Post-2024 data reflects significant inflationary pressure on overheads.

## Key Facts
1. **The "Revenue Ceiling":** For a solo, revenue is strictly capped by "billable hours" unless using fixed fees or automation. Average revenue for a junior barrister is ~$200k - $250k.
2. **Overheads (30-50%):** PI Insurance (~$5k-$15k), Practice Certificate (~$1k-$3k), Chambers/Rent (~$20k-$50k), Tech/Subsciptions (~$10k), Superannuation/Tax.
3. **The "Lumpy" Cashflow:** Litigation fees are often paid at the end of a matter; solos face significant "Work in Progress" (WIP) carrying costs.
4. **Trust Accounting:** Mismanagement of client money is the fastest path to losing a practicing certificate.
5. **Outsourcing:** Increasing use of VAs and external bookkeepers to manage the "Admin Burden."

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The economic success of a solo practice is determined less by the hourly rate and more by the collection rate and overhead control." — ALPMA Insight.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Solos must use automated billing and follow-up systems to manage cashflow.
- Maintaining a "3-month cash buffer" is a critical risk management strategy for solo practitioners.
- Diversifying into "Fixed Fee" products can help break the billable hour ceiling.
