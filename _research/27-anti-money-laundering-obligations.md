## Currency Warning
**FORTHCOMING OBLIGATIONS:** The "Tranche 2" reforms were passed in late 2024. Full compliance for lawyers is expected by **July 2026**.

## Key Facts
1. **"Gatekeeper" Professions:** Lawyers, accountants, and real estate agents are now "Reporting Entities" under the Act.
2. **Designated Services:** Obligations are triggered when providing specific services:
    - Real estate transactions.
    - Managing client money, securities, or other assets.
    - Creating, operating, or managing companies or legal arrangements (trusts).
3. **Core Obligations:**
    - Register with AUSTRAC.
    - Implement an AML/CTF Program (Part A and Part B).
    - Perform Customer Due Diligence (CDD / KYC).
    - Report Suspicious Matters (SMRs) and Threshold Transactions (TTRs).
4. **Transition Period:** Use 2025 to build systems; enforcement begins July 2026.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The reforms will extend the AML/CTF regime to certain high-risk professional services... including lawyers, conveyancers, accountants, and trust and company service providers." — AUSTRAC Summary.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Solos must integrate KYC/CDD into their onboarding workflow *before* mid-2026.
- Failure to register with AUSTRAC will carry significant civil penalties.
- Professional privilege (LPP) does NOT generally protect the underlying facts of a transaction from AML reporting, though it protects legal advice.
- Solo practitioners should consider AML software integrations early.
