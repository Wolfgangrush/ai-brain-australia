## Key Facts
1. **Civil Standard (s 140):** Balance of probabilities. The court must be "comfortably satisfied."
2. **The *Briginshaw* Principle:** In civil cases involving serious allegations (e.g., fraud, professional misconduct), the court requires a higher degree of satisfaction within the "balance of probabilities" framework.
3. **Criminal Standard (s 141):** Beyond reasonable doubt.
4. **Burden of Proof:** Generally on the party asserting the fact (e.g., the Plaintiff or the Prosecution).
5. **Reversed Onus:** Rare; found in some specific statutes (e.g., some drug or asset forfeiture laws).

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"In a civil proceeding, the court must find the case of a party proved if it is satisfied that the case has been proved on the balance of probabilities." — Section 140(1).

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Solos pleading fraud or professional negligence must be aware that the *Briginshaw* standard means "scanty evidence" will not suffice, even if it's a civil matter.
- In criminal defense, the solo's focus is on creating or highlighting "reasonable doubt" rather than proving innocence.
- Understanding these standards is critical for advising clients on their "Prospects of Success."
