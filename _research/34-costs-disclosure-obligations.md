## Currency Warning
Stable under the Uniform Law. Note the thresholds for "Standard" vs "Lower" disclosure.

## Key Facts
1. **The "Disclosure" Requirement:** Solicitors must provide clients with information about how costs will be calculated and an estimate of the total costs.
2. **Standard Disclosure:** Required if total costs are likely to exceed $3,000 (excluding GST and disbursements).
3. **Lower Threshold:** If costs are likely to be between $750 and $3,000, a simplified "Uniform standard disclosure form" can be used.
4. **Exemptions:** No disclosure required if costs are <$750, or for "sophisticated clients" (e.g., other law practices, public companies).
5. **Penalty for Non-disclosure:** The costs agreement may be void; the solicitor cannot sue for the costs; and it may constitute unsatisfactory professional conduct.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"A law practice must... disclose to a client in accordance with this Division: (a) the basis on which legal costs will be calculated; and (b) an estimate of the total legal costs." — Section 174, LPUL.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Solos must have a robust "Costs Disclosure" template that is automatically sent at the start of every matter >$750.
- "Scope Creep": If the estimate changes significantly, the solo MUST provide an updated disclosure to the client immediately.
- Failure to disclose is the most common reason for costs being "taxed down" or disallowed during a costs assessment.
