## Currency Warning
Mandatory since the 2021 merger of the federal courts.

## Key Facts
1. **Mandatory Step:** Parties must make a genuine effort to resolve their dispute through "Family Dispute Resolution" (FDR) before filing an application.
2. **Exceptions:** Urgency, family violence, or risk of child abuse.
3. **The "Section 60I Certificate":** Required before filing an application for parenting orders (unless an exception applies).
4. **Disclosure:** Parties must exchange a "genuine" list of documents and information relevant to the issues.
5. **Notice of Intent:** A party must give the other party written notice of their intention to start proceedings and a summary of the orders they will seek.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The court must not hear an application... unless the applicant has filed a certificate... from a family dispute resolution practitioner." — Section 60I, Family Law Act 1975.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Family law solos spend significant time in the "Pre-action" phase managing FDR and disclosure.
- Filing without a 60I certificate or a valid exception will result in the application being rejected or stayed.
- The pre-action procedure is designed to protect solo practitioners' clients from the high costs of unnecessary litigation.
