## Currency Warning
Corporations Act is a Commonwealth Act that applies nationwide. Frequent amendments; check for the latest on "Director Identification Numbers" and "Insolvency Safe Harbour."

## Key Facts
1. **Separate Legal Entity:** A company has the legal capacity of a natural person.
2. **Director's Duties:**
    - Care and diligence (s 180).
    - Good faith and proper purpose (s 181).
    - Use of position/information (s 182, s 183).
    - Duty to avoid insolvent trading (s 588G).
3. **Proprietary vs. Public:** Most solo advocates work with "Pty Ltd" (Proprietary) companies, which have restricted share transfers and <50 non-employee shareholders.
4. **ASIC:** The regulator for companies; handles registration, annual reviews, and enforcement.
5. **Small Business Restructuring:** Simplified insolvency process for companies with debts <$1m.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"A director or other officer of a corporation must exercise their powers and discharge their duties with the degree of care and diligence that a reasonable person would exercise..." — Section 180(1).

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Solos are often the first point of contact for small business owners facing insolvency; understanding s 588G is critical to protect the client's personal assets.
- Drafting "Shareholder Agreements" for Pty Ltd companies is a staple for commercial solos.
- Practitioners must ensure their ILP (if incorporated) complies with both the Corporations Act and the LPUL.
