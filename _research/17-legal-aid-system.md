## Key Facts
1. **The "Means Test":** Eligibility is strictly based on income and assets.
2. **The "Merit Test":** Legal Aid is only granted if the case has "reasonable prospects of success."
3. **Private Practitioner Panels:** Most solo advocates are "Panel Practitioners" who accept Legal Aid grants at fixed (often lower) rates.
4. **Duty Lawyer Services:** Solos often work as duty lawyers in Local/Magistrates courts to provide one-off advice/representation to unrepresented persons.
5. **Grants of Aid:** Must be applied for via specialized portals (e.g., "Grants Online").

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"Legal aid is not a right; it is a limited resource allocated to those in greatest need who have a meritorious case." — Legal Aid NSW Annual Report.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Managing a "Legal Aid Practice" requires high volume and strict efficiency due to the fixed fee structure.
- Solos must be diligent with "Grant Extensions" — if you work beyond the approved hours without an extension, you will not be paid.
- Legal Aid work is a primary client acquisition channel for new solos.
