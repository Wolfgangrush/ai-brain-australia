## Currency Warning
Ongoing transition to "Digital by Default." Most federal and state courts now mandate e-filing for legal practitioners.

## Key Facts
1. **ComCourts Portal:** Primary interface for Federal Court and FCFCOA; allows for "eFiling," viewing orders, and "Daily Court Lists."
2. **NSW Online Registry:** Used for most civil filings in NSW; includes "Online Court" for directions and "eSubpoena."
3. **Mandatory Use:** Legal practitioners are generally required to use e-filing rather than counter filing.
4. **Signatures:** E-signatures (typed or scanned) are widely accepted on e-filed documents under court rules.
5. **Verification:** Requires practitioner ID (e.g., PC number) and individual user accounts.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"Practitioners are required to file all documents through the Portal... except in exceptional circumstances or where the Rules provide otherwise." — FCFCOA Practice Direction.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
- Solos must have high-speed scanning and PDF manipulation tools as part of their "Virtual Office."
- Deadlines: E-filing portals often have 11:59 PM cutoffs, but practitioners should avoid "last minute" filings due to potential system downtime.
- Privacy: Solos must ensure sensitive documents are correctly redacted before e-filing, as some portals allow public access to certain filings.
