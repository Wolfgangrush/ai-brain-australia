## Authority Tier
Primary — Evidence Act 1995 + FCR 2011 + State Civil Procedure Acts + common-law principles (*Calderbank v Calderbank* [1975] 3 All ER 333 applied in Australia).

## Currency Warning
Three distinct settlement-offer regimes in Australia:
- **Without-Prejudice (WP)** — Evidence Act s 131 · classic common-law protection · communications inadmissible.
- **Without-Prejudice Save As To Costs (WPSATC) / Calderbank Offers** — formal offers that become admissible on costs after determination; *Calderbank* doctrine.
- **Formal Offers under FCR Part 25 / state UCPR equivalents** — court-formula offers; automatic costs consequences on non-acceptance.

## Template Type
Procedural Document / Offer Letter

## Structural Skeleton

**(A) WITHOUT-PREJUDICE LETTER**

1. **Letterhead** — solicitor's firm.
2. **Marking** — "WITHOUT PREJUDICE" prominently at top.
3. **Date · Addressee** — opposing solicitor.
4. **Re** — court · case number · parties.
5. **Recital** — *"We refer to the above proceedings. This letter is written WITHOUT PREJUDICE and is subject to the without-prejudice privilege under section 131 of the Evidence Act 1995 (Cth)."*
6. **Offer terms** — substantive settlement proposal.
7. **Deadline for acceptance** — reasonable period (typically 7-21 days).
8. **Signature**.

**(B) CALDERBANK OFFER (WPSATC)**

1. **Marking** — "WITHOUT PREJUDICE SAVE AS TO COSTS" prominently at top.
2. **Recital** — *"This letter is written without prejudice save as to costs. It may be used by either party as to costs orders if the substantive proceedings are determined less favourably to the recipient than the terms of this offer."*
3. **Offer terms** — clear, unambiguous, capable of acceptance without further negotiation.
4. **Deadline** — typically 14-28 days.
5. **Costs consequences** — explicit reservation of right to seek indemnity costs from date of offer if offer not bettered at trial.
6. **Signature**.

**(C) FORMAL OFFER UNDER FCR PART 25 / STATE UCPR**

1. **Form** — court-prescribed form (Federal Court Form 45).
2. **Heading** — court · case number · parties.
3. **Title** — "Offer to Settle Pursuant to Rule 25.01 of the Federal Court Rules 2011".
4. **Offer terms** — payment · non-monetary obligations · costs proposed.
5. **Time-limit for acceptance** — minimum 14 days.
6. **Filing** — with Registrar; served on opposing party.
7. **Costs consequences** (FCR Rule 25.14):
   - If applicant offers + obtains judgment ≥ offer → applicant entitled to indemnity costs from offer date.
   - If respondent offers + applicant fails to obtain judgment > offer → respondent entitled to indemnity / costs from offer date.

**(D) DRAFTING DISTINCTIONS**

| Feature | WP | WPSATC (Calderbank) | FCR Part 25 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Privilege scope | Full inadmissibility | Inadmissible on merits; admissible on costs | Inadmissible on merits; automatic costs consequences |
| Costs consequence | None (cannot be used) | Discretionary indemnity costs | Mandatory unless court otherwise orders |
| Marking | "WITHOUT PREJUDICE" | "WITHOUT PREJUDICE SAVE AS TO COSTS" | Form 45 |
| Filing | Not filed | Not filed | Filed with Registrar |
| Use at trial | Cannot | Tendered for costs only | Tendered for costs (mandatory regime) |

## Drafting Conventions
- **Marking is critical** — failure to mark properly may forfeit privilege.
- **Genuine offer** — not "pretended" offer; must be capable of acceptance.
- **Clear terms** — vague offers fail Calderbank consequences (*MGICA (1992) Ltd v Kenny & Good Pty Ltd* (1996) 70 FCR 236).
- **Reasonable deadline** — 7-21 days for WP; 14-28 days for Calderbank; minimum 14 days for FCR Part 25.
- **Costs reservation** — explicit in Calderbank.
- **Lump-sum offers** — typically include all heads of damage + interest + costs; structured offers permissible.
- **Conditional offers** — permissible but assessed strictly (*MGICA*).
- **Strategic use** — Calderbank offers + FCR Part 25 offers commonly used in tandem for maximum costs leverage.
- **Best timing** — early enough to give respondent meaningful opportunity to evaluate; late offers (post-evidence) may not attract costs consequences.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Evidence is not to be adduced of a communication that is made between persons in dispute, or between one or more persons in dispute and a third party, in connection with an attempt to negotiate a settlement of the dispute, or to negotiate the substance of any settlement." (Evidence Act 1995 (Cth), Section 131(1), paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The three settlement-offer regimes are the principal litigation-leverage tools in Australian civil practice. Solo practitioners should triage early: (a) WP letters for exploratory negotiations; (b) Calderbank offers for serious settlement attempts with cost-protection objective; (c) FCR Part 25 offers for automatic costs-consequence leverage. Combined use of Calderbank + FCR Part 25 is common in heavily-contested matters. Early offers + reasonable deadlines maximise cost-protection.
