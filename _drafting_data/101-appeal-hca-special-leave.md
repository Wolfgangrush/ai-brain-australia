## Currency Warning
Special leave to appeal to the High Court is rarely granted. Statistics — typically less than 10% of applications succeed at oral special leave hearings. Grounds limited under Judiciary Act s 35A: (a) question of law of public importance OR (b) interests of administration of justice require consideration OR (c) judgment below differs from another superior court of record.

## Template Type
Court Form / Application

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Heading** — High Court of Australia · Sydney / Melbourne / Canberra Registry · application number · year · parties.
2. **Form 23** — Application for Special Leave to Appeal.
3. **Identification of decision below**:
   - Court below (FCAFC / State Court of Appeal / Family Court Full Court / etc.).
   - Date · citation · case number.
4. **Special leave grounds** (Judiciary Act s 35A):
   - Question of general or public importance — explain WHY.
   - Conflict between intermediate appellate courts — cite the conflict.
   - Interests of administration of justice.
5. **Proposed grounds of appeal** (if leave granted):
   - Numbered; each a question of law.
6. **Orders sought**:
   - Special leave to appeal granted.
   - Appeal allowed.
   - Orders below set aside.
   - Substituted orders.
   - Costs.
7. **Statement of Issues** (separate document) — concise.
8. **Summary of Argument** (separate document, ≤10 pages) — most important document.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Time-limit** — 28 days from date of judgment below (HCR Rule 41.02).
- **Two-stage process** — written submissions (decided on papers) OR oral hearing (typically 20 minutes per side if listed).
- **Summary of Argument** — the document that decides the application; tight, focused.
- **Conflict of authorities** — strongest ground; cite the conflicting decisions of intermediate appellate courts.
- **Public importance** — explain in 1-2 sentences why this case affects parties beyond the immediate.
- **Reading list** — pre-trial reading list for the panel; 4-6 documents.
- **AGLC4** — neutral citation first, *[YYYY] HCA NN*; intermediate appellate *[YYYY] FCAFC NN* / *[YYYY] NSWCA NN* / *[YYYY] VSCA NN*.
- **Cross-application** for special leave on different grounds — respondent files own application.
- **Filing fee** — verify current HCR Schedule 2 fee (varies by litigant type).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"In considering whether to grant an application for special leave to appeal, the High Court may have regard to any matters that it considers relevant but shall have regard to (a) whether the proceedings in which the judgment to which the application relates was pronounced involve a question of law that is of public importance, whether because of its general application or otherwise; or that requires consideration by the High Court..." (Judiciary Act 1903, s 35A, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Special leave applications are typically counsel-heavy work; solo practitioners running these would conventionally brief specialist appellate counsel (often Senior Counsel). The Summary of Argument is the single decisive document — narrowly focused on the special-leave gateway not the underlying merits. Generic grounds fail; specific identification of a conflict between FCAFC and NSWCA (or VSCA / QCA / WASCA) is the strongest path.
