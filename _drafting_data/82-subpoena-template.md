## Authority Tier
Court Rule Form (Federal Court / UCPR)

## Currency Warning
Note the requirement for **Form 44 (Notice and Declaration)** in the Federal Court, which the addressee must return with the documents. Ensure the "Last Date for Service" complies with the 5-clear-business-day rule.

## Template Type
Subpoena / Court Process

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Court Header:** Case details.
2. **To the Addressee:** [Name and Address of the person/company].
3. **The Order:** "You are ordered to attend Court to produce this subpoena and the documents or things described in the Schedule."
4. **Schedule of Documents:** Numbered list of specific categories (must not be "fishing").
5. **Compliance Details:**
   - Date, Time, and Place for production (often to the Registry).
   - Last date for service.
6. **Conduct Money:** Statement regarding the payment of reasonable expenses for compliance.
7. **Notice to Addressee:** Detailed notes on rights to object (e.g., Privilege, Irrelevancy, Oppression).
8. **Signature:** Registrar and Seal of the Court.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Precision:** The Schedule must describe documents with enough particularity that the addressee knows exactly what to look for.
- **Oppression:** Avoid overly broad requests (e.g., "All emails ever sent by the company") as these will be set aside as "oppressive."
- **Conduct Money:** A subpoena to produce is not valid unless a "reasonable sum" for the costs of searching and delivering documents is tendered at the time of service.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "Failure to comply with this subpoena without lawful excuse is a contempt of court and may result in your arrest." (Mandatory statutory warning).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The primary tool for obtaining evidence from third parties (banks, hospitals, former employers). Solo practitioners use subpoenas to build the evidentiary foundation of their case where the other party refuses to cooperate.
