## Authority Tier
Primary — NMAS + CDRA + Evidence Act 1995.

## Currency Warning
Mediation agreement (the document the parties + mediator sign at the start of the mediation) governs the procedure + confidentiality. Distinct from the mediated settlement agreement (the document executed at the end of a successful mediation — see file 202).

## Template Type
Contract Template

## Structural Skeleton

1. **Parties**:
   - All parties + their legal representatives.
   - Mediator + accreditation (NMAS reference).
2. **Recitals**:
   - Dispute identified.
   - Parties' desire to attempt resolution via mediation.
   - Mediator's appointment.
3. **Mediator's Role + Process**:
   - **Mediator is neutral** — not an advocate · not an arbitrator · not a judge.
   - **Facilitative model** (default) OR evaluative model (specified — provides indicative views).
   - **Joint sessions** + **private sessions** (caucus).
   - **No determination** of merits.
4. **Mediator's Duty + Conflict of Interest**:
   - Independence + impartiality.
   - Disclosure of any prior relationship with parties.
   - Withdrawal if conflict arises.
5. **Confidentiality**:
   - All communications in mediation are confidential.
   - Subject to Evidence Act s 131 + common-law without-prejudice privilege.
   - Mediator cannot be compelled to give evidence in subsequent proceedings.
   - Limited exceptions: criminal disclosures · imminent risk of harm · enforcement of settlement agreement.
6. **Privilege**:
   - Communications inadmissible in subsequent court proceedings.
   - Documents prepared specifically for mediation privileged.
7. **Without Prejudice Principle** — all settlement discussions WP.
8. **Authority to Settle**:
   - Each party warrants that the natural-person attending has authority to settle.
   - Corporate parties — board resolution or executive authority documented.
9. **Mediator's Fees + Cost-sharing**:
   - Fee rate (hourly / daily).
   - Allocation — typically 50/50; subject to court order otherwise.
   - Payment timing.
10. **Procedure**:
    - Date · time · venue (or video format).
    - Position papers exchange deadlines.
    - Joint session sequence.
    - Caucus availability.
    - Termination — any party may terminate by notice.
11. **Outcomes**:
    - If settlement reached → executed Mediated Settlement Agreement (file 202).
    - If no settlement → confidentiality continues; mediation does not prevent resort to court.
12. **Mediator's Notes**:
    - Mediator's notes destroyed at end of mediation OR retained by mediator confidentially.
    - Notes inadmissible.
13. **Liability**:
    - Mediator's limited liability under NMAS framework.
14. **Termination**:
    - At any party's election.
    - At mediator's election (e.g., breach of confidentiality · bad-faith conduct).
15. **Governing Law + Forum**:
    - Governing law of mediation agreement.
    - Subsequent forum if mediation fails.
16. **Signatures** — all parties + mediator + (where required) representatives.

## Drafting Conventions
- **NMAS Standard Form Mediation Agreement** widely used as base; customised per case.
- **Authority to settle** — critical; documented + warranted.
- **Confidentiality scope** — clearly drawn; exceptions narrow.
- **Court-ordered mediation** — court order takes precedence; mediation agreement supplements.
- **Costs allocation** — typically 50/50 initially; may be reallocated in final costs orders if mediation refused unreasonably.
- **Singapore Convention** considerations — for international mediations; flag in agreement if parties want Singapore Convention enforceability for the resulting settlement.
- **CDRA Genuine Steps Statement** — execution of mediation agreement evidences genuine steps for Commonwealth proceedings.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The mediator is a neutral facilitator who assists the parties to identify their interests, generate options, and explore possible resolutions. The mediator does not impose a decision. All communications in the mediation are confidential and subject to without-prejudice privilege." (NMAS framework, synthesised.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The mediation agreement is the procedural foundation for the mediation. Solo practitioners often skip or use boilerplate — both risky. Authority-to-settle warranties are the most-litigated post-mediation issue; over-confident corporate parties without proper board authority can find themselves unable to enforce or to escape the settlement.
