## Authority Tier
Court Rule Form / Statutory Power (Division 7.1 Fed Ct Rules)

## Currency Warning
Ensure compliance with the "usual undertaking as to damages" requirement. Practitioners must also check for specific practice notes if the injunction relates to Intellectual Property or Commercial matters.

## Template Type
Motion / Interlocutory Application

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Court Header:** Case details.
2. **Orders Sought:**
   - An interim injunction restraining the Respondent from [specific act, e.g., "selling the goods marked ABC"] until further order.
   - Abridgment of time for service (if urgent).
   - Leave to serve the application and supporting affidavit by [alternative method, if applicable].
   - Costs.
3. **The "Usual Undertaking":** "The Applicant provides to the Court the usual undertaking as to damages."
4. **Grounds of Application:**
   - There is a serious question to be tried.
   - The balance of convenience favors the granting of the injunction.
   - Damages would not be an adequate remedy.
5. **Supporting Documents:**
   - Affidavit of [Name] (must address the Beecham/O'Neill criteria).
6. **Execution:** Signature of Solicitor.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Precision of Restraint:** The act to be restrained must be described with extreme precision so the Respondent knows exactly what they cannot do.
- **Urgency:** If the application is *ex parte*, the affidavit must explain why notice was not given and disclose all material facts (including those adverse to the Applicant).
- **Duration:** Usually sought "until 4:00pm on [Date]" (return date) or "until further order."

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "The Applicant applies for the following interlocutory orders: 1. Until further order, the Respondent be restrained, whether by itself, its servants or agents, from [Restricted Act]. 2. The Applicant has leave to serve this application and the affidavit of [Name] on the Respondent by [Email/Other]."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Used to "stop the bleeding" in cases of IP theft, breach of non-compete clauses, or imminent destruction of property. A high-stakes application that requires rapid drafting and evidence gathering.
