## Currency Warning
Be mindful of the "Unfair Contract Terms" (UCT) regime under the ACL (effective November 2023). Overly broad or one-sided NDAs in small business contracts may be deemed "unfair" and void.

## Template Type
Contract / Deed

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties:** Disclosing Party and Receiving Party.
2. **Definition of Confidential Information:** What is covered (and exclusions like "public domain").
3. **Purpose of Disclosure:** Why the info is being shared (the "Permitted Purpose").
4. **Obligations of Receiving Party:**
   - Keep secret.
   - Use only for the Purpose.
   - Limit access to employees/advisers on a "need to know" basis.
5. **Permitted Disclosures:** Disclosures required by law (court order/regulator).
6. **Return or Destruction of Information:** At the end of the term.
7. **Term/Duration:** How long the obligation lasts (e.g., 2 years, 5 years, or perpetual).
8. **Remedies:** Acknowledgment that damages may be inadequate and injunctions may be sought.
9. **Indemnity:** (Optional) for breach of confidentiality.
10. **Execution as a Deed:** Often executed as a Deed to avoid "consideration" issues.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Deed vs. Agreement:** In Australia, NDAs are frequently drafted as **Deeds** to ensure they are enforceable even if no money changes hands (lack of consideration).
- **Carve-outs:** Always include a carve-out for "publicly available information" and "information already known to the receiver."
- **Whistleblower Protections:** Ensure the NDA does not prevent a party from making a "protected disclosure" under the *Corporations Act*.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "The Receiving Party must keep the Confidential Information secret and must not disclose it to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party, except as required by law."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Used in the early stages of a business deal, employment negotiation, or technology sale. It is the "front line" of IP protection for small business clients.
