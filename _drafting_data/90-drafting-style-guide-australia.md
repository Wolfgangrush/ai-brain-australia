## Structural Skeleton
1. **Citation Standard:** Mandatory use of **AGLC4** for all footnotes and references.
2. **Case Citations:**
   - Hierarchy: Authorized (CLR, FCR, NSWLR) > Unauthorized (ALR) > Medium Neutral.
   - Format: *Case Name* (Year) Volume Report Starting Page, Pinpoint.
3. **Statutory Citations:**
   - Format: *Short Title Year* (Jurisdiction) Pinpoint.
   - Abbreviations: s (section), ss (sections), pt (part), sch (schedule).
4. **Formatting (Court Standards):**
   - Font: Times New Roman or Arial, 12pt.
   - Line Spacing: 1.5 or Double (for pleadings).
   - Paragraphs: Numbered sequentially (1, 2, 3...).
   - Margins: 25mm left, 30mm top (NSW standard).
5. **Drafting Tone:** Plain English, avoid "legalese" where possible (subject to mandatory statutory wording).
6. **Gender Neutral Language:** Mandatory use of gender-neutral terms unless referring to a specific individual.
7. **AI Disclosure:** Clause for certifying human-in-the-loop verification of drafts.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Pinpoint Citations:** Always provide a pinpoint (page or paragraph) for every proposition of law.
- **Ibid:** Use only for identical preceding footnotes.
- **Defined Terms:** Capitalized and defined at first use or in a definitions section.
- **Punctuation in Citations:** Footnote numbers *after* the full stop. No full stop at the end of a title or heading.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "A citation of an Act must consist of the short title and year of the Act in italics, followed by the jurisdiction in parentheses... Example: *Civil Procedure Act 2005* (NSW) s 100." (Summarized from AGLC4).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Maintains professionalism and prevents "rejection at the registry" or judicial criticism. Following AGLC4 is the hallmark of a competent Australian practitioner.
