## Authority Tier
Court Rule Form (UCPR NSW)

## Currency Warning
Check for Court-specific "Notice of Motion" variations in the Land and Environment Court or the Industrial Relations Commission. The general Form 20 is Version 3.

## Template Type
Motion / Interlocutory Application

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Court Header:** Case details.
2. **Filing Details:** Identity of the Applicant and Legal Representative.
3. **Person Affected:** List of parties to be served.
4. **Hearing Details:** (Registry use only).
5. **Orders Sought:**
   - Order 1: [The primary order sought, e.g., "The Defendant provide discovery"].
   - Order 2: [Ancillary orders].
   - Order 3: "Costs of the motion."
6. **Grounds:** Reference to the supporting affidavit (e.g., "The grounds are set out in the affidavit of [Name] filed herewith").
7. **Signature:** Solicitor for the Applicant.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Affidavit Required:** A Notice of Motion in NSW is almost never filed without a supporting **Affidavit (Form 40)**.
- **Service Period:** Must be served 3 clear business days before the hearing.
- **Terminology:** In NSW, the "Notice of Motion" is the general term for what the Federal Court calls an "Interlocutory Application."

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "To the [Respondent]: The Court will hear this motion at the time and place specified below. If you do not attend, the Court may make orders in your absence." (Standard Form 20 warning).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The primary "workhorse" for moving a case along in NSW courts. Used for everything from seeking more time to file a defence to asking for a case to be dismissed.
