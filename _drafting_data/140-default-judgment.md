## Currency Warning
Default judgment available where respondent fails to file Notice of Address for Service (FCR Rule 5.21) within prescribed period (typically 14 days · 28 days where served outside Australia) OR fails to file Defence within prescribed period. Available administratively for liquidated debt claims (no court hearing) or by interlocutory application for unliquidated claims (assessment of damages).

## Template Type
Application / Court Form / Administrative Request

## Structural Skeleton

**(A) LIQUIDATED CLAIM — REQUEST FOR DEFAULT JUDGMENT**

1. **Heading** — court · case number · parties.
2. **Title** — "Request for Default Judgment".
3. **Statement of default**:
   - Date Originating Application + Statement of Claim served on respondent.
   - Whether NOA filed (date if yes; "none filed" if no).
   - Whether Defence filed (date if yes; "none filed" if no).
   - Period elapsed beyond deadline.
4. **Sum claimed**:
   - Principal.
   - Interest (FCAA s 51A; state Civil Law Acts).
   - Costs (FCR Schedule 3 fixed costs for liquidated claims).
5. **Annexures** — affidavit of service · proof of service.
6. **Signature** — solicitor.

**(B) UNLIQUIDATED CLAIM — INTERLOCUTORY APPLICATION**

1. **Application + supporting affidavit**.
2. **Reliefs sought**:
   - Default judgment against respondent.
   - Assessment of damages by Registrar / Judge.
   - Costs on indemnity basis (if appropriate).
3. **Hearing date** — fixed for assessment of damages.

**(C) ASSESSMENT OF DAMAGES**

- Conducted by Registrar (typical) or Judge.
- Applicant proves quantum + interest.
- Respondent has limited right to participate (cross-examination on quantum only).
- Final judgment + costs.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Affidavit of service** — strict compliance; defective service nullifies default judgment.
- **Time-limit calculations** — calendar days vs business days; date of service vs date of issue.
- **Liquidated vs unliquidated** — different procedural paths.
- **Interest computation** — pre-judgment under FCAA s 51A (Cth) or state equivalent.
- **Costs** — fixed costs under FCR Schedule 3 for liquidated claims; assessed costs for unliquidated.
- **Setting-aside grounds** — see file 141.
- **Service overseas** — different time-limits under Hague Service Convention or state Service of Process Rules.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"If a respondent has not filed a notice of address for service or a defence within the period required, the applicant may apply to the Court for default judgment." (FCR 2011 Rule 5.23, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Default judgment is the principal commercial recovery tool for clean liquidated claims where the respondent does not respond. Common scenarios: unpaid commercial invoices · breach of payment terms · dishonoured cheques. Practitioners must keep meticulous service records — service-step failures nullify the judgment. Once obtained, default judgment is enforceable like any other judgment (see file 150-153 enforcement files).
