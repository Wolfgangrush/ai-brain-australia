## Currency Warning
Written closing submissions filed AFTER evidence closes, typically within 14-21 days. They are the principal vehicle for the judgment — Australian judges write reserved judgments largely on the basis of written closings + the bench's notes of oral closings.

## Template Type
Submission

## Structural Skeleton

1. **Cover sheet** — court · case number · parties · counsel · solicitor · date · page count · word count.
2. **Heading** — "Written Closing Submissions of the [Applicant / Respondent]".
3. **Index of contents**.
4. **Executive summary** (½-1 page) — party's case in one tight paragraph + the result sought.
5. **Procedural background** (½ page) — pleadings · interlocutory orders · trial dates · witnesses called.
6. **Trial issues** — bulleted list of 3-7 issues for determination.
7. **Burden + standard of proof**:
   - Civil trial: balance of probabilities.
   - Fraud / quasi-criminal allegations: higher quality of evidence required (*Briginshaw v Briginshaw* (1938) 60 CLR 336).
   - Specific statutory burdens (e.g., ACL misleading conduct: applicant bears initial burden).
8. **Witnesses called + credibility assessment**:
   - For each witness:
     - Name + role.
     - AEIC paragraphs relied upon.
     - Cross-examination breakdowns (transcript page + line).
     - Credibility submission — credible / not credible / partially reliable + reasons.
9. **Documentary evidence weighed** — contemporaneous documents preferred over witness recollection (*Fox v Percy* (2003) 214 CLR 118).
10. **For each trial issue**:
    - Pleaded case on the issue.
    - Evidence adduced.
    - Findings of fact sought.
    - Legal framework — applicable statute · case-law · principles.
    - Application of law to findings of fact.
    - Conclusion on the issue.
11. **Quantum**:
    - Heads of loss · methodology · computation · interest under FCAA s 51A or state equivalent.
12. **Costs**:
    - Basis (party-party · indemnity).
    - Scale (FCR Schedule 3 fixed or assessed; state-specific scales).
    - Estimated quantum.
    - Reasons for any indemnity basis (Calderbank offers · misconduct).
13. **Disposition sought**:
    - Judgment for `<sum>` against `<respondent>` with interest from `<date>` until payment.
    - Declarations.
    - Injunctions.
    - Costs.
14. **Form of judgment** annexed — short paragraph form for adoption by Court.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Page limits** — typically 80-150 pages for 5-day trial; verify GPN-WRTS.
- **Word count** — page limit AND word count usually both stated.
- **Pinpoint citations**:
  - Court Book (CB pp 234-237).
  - AEICs (AEIC of [Witness] paras 12-15).
  - Transcript (T 234/15 — page 234, line 15).
  - Authorities (*Robinson Helicopter* (2016) 261 CLR 297 at [43]).
- **AGLC4 mandatory**.
- **Quote sparingly** — operative passages from key authorities; paraphrase the rest.
- **Concede minor issues** — focus on determinative issues.
- **Address every key piece of opposing evidence** — silence on damaging documents signals weakness.
- **Cross-examination breakdowns** — quote operative exchanges; explain reliability.
- **Credibility submissions** — be specific; "the witness lied" requires evidence backing.
- **No new evidence in closings** — only argument on evidence already in the record.
- **Reply submissions** — permitted within short window after opposing party's closings; limited to new matters.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"After the close of evidence, parties shall file written closing submissions in accordance with the Practice Note. The submissions shall identify the issues, summarise the evidence, set out the legal arguments, and identify the relief sought." (Federal Court Practice Note GPN-WRTS, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The written closing is THE document of the trial — judgments are written largely from it. Solo practitioners should allocate substantial post-trial time to drafting (3-7 working days for complex case). Common mistakes: (a) re-presenting AEIC narrative without engaging with actual evidence given; (b) failing to address opposing party's strongest documents; (c) treating quantum as an afterthought. The bench grades closings on engagement with the actual contested evidence.
