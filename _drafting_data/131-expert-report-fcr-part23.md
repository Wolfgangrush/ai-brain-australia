## Currency Warning
Expert report format prescribed under GPN-EXPT (Federal Court) + state equivalents. Mandatory elements include statement of expertise, materials reviewed, facts assumed, opinions, reasons, statement of matters not within own knowledge / observed, acknowledgment of Expert Witness Code of Conduct, signature, statement of truth.

## Template Type
Expert Report Template

## Structural Skeleton

1. **Cover sheet**:
   - "Expert Report of [Expert Name]".
   - Date.
   - Prepared for [Applicant / Respondent].
   - Court · case number · parties.

2. **Section 1 — Expert qualifications and experience**:
   - Name · qualifications · current position · firm.
   - Field of expertise.
   - Professional memberships · accreditations.
   - Years of relevant experience.
   - Prior expert witness experience (list of cases — optional).
   - CV annexed.

3. **Section 2 — Acknowledgment of Expert Witness Code of Conduct**:
   - Verbatim: *"I acknowledge that I have read the Expert Witness Code of Conduct (Schedule 1 to GPN-EXPT of the Federal Court of Australia) and agree to be bound by it. I understand that my paramount duty is to the Court and not to the party who has retained me."*
   - Signature + date.

4. **Section 3 — Instructions received**:
   - Date of letter of instruction.
   - Identity of instructing solicitor.
   - Specific questions posed (verbatim from letter).

5. **Section 4 — Materials reviewed**:
   - Schedule of documents · with dates.
   - Other materials (interviews · site visits · physical inspections · samples).

6. **Section 5 — Facts assumed**:
   - Facts the expert has been instructed to assume.
   - Distinguished from facts the expert has independently observed / verified.
   - Acknowledgment: if any assumed fact is wrong, the opinion may need revision.

7. **Section 6 — Independent investigations conducted**:
   - Tests · measurements · calculations · simulations.
   - Methodology.
   - Results.

8. **Section 7 — Opinions**:
   - Each opinion stated clearly and concisely.
   - Each opinion linked to the question posed.
   - For each opinion: reasons + supporting evidence + literature / standards relied upon.

9. **Section 8 — Reasons for opinions**:
   - Logical chain of reasoning from facts to opinion.
   - Citations to peer-reviewed literature / industry standards / codes of practice.
   - Discussion of competing interpretations + why the expert rejects them.

10. **Section 9 — Matters where the expert is unsure / uncertain**:
    - Limitations of available data.
    - Areas where further investigation would be required.
    - Areas outside the expert's specialism.

11. **Section 10 — Statement of truth + signature**:
    - Verbatim: *"I confirm that the opinions expressed in this report are my own, are within my area of expertise, and are based on the facts and assumptions stated. I have made all the inquiries that I believe are desirable. I have not omitted any matter that I believe is significant. My opinion is independent and impartial."*
    - Signature · date · place.

12. **Annexures** — CV · CV photographs · diagrams · charts · raw data · literature references.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Code of Conduct acknowledgment mandatory** — failure renders evidence inadmissible.
- **Statement of truth** at the end is required by Evidence Act s 79 + Code of Conduct.
- **Distinguish observed facts from assumed facts** — failure to do this is a common ground for cross-examination.
- **Reasons must be provided** — bare opinion without reasoning is inadmissible.
- **Literature / standards cited** — peer-reviewed sources preferred; industry standards acceptable.
- **No advocacy** — opinions cannot be argumentative.
- **Concurrent evidence (hot-tubbing)** — expert must be prepared to engage with opposing expert under judicial supervision.
- **Joint conference of experts** — see file 132; report may need amendment post-joint-statement.
- **Single Joint Expert variation** — single report serving both parties; format same.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"If a person has specialised knowledge based on the person's training, study or experience, the opinion rule does not apply to evidence of an opinion of that person that is wholly or substantially based on that knowledge." (Evidence Act 1995 (Cth), Section 79.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The expert report is the principal substantive document in any technical-issue case. Quality of the report dictates outcome. Solo practitioners should brief carefully (file 130), then engage with the draft report — challenge weak reasoning, request additional analysis, ensure compliance with Code of Conduct. Reports that read as advocacy-pieces rather than independent analyses are heavily cross-examined and often disbelieved.
