## Authority Tier
Court Rule Form / Procedural Power

## Currency Warning
The threshold for a strike-out is high ("plainly and obviously untenable"). Practitioners should consider whether an application for **Summary Judgment (Rule 26.01)** is more appropriate if they wish to rely on evidence outside the face of the pleading.

## Template Type
Motion / Interlocutory Application

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Court Header:** Case details.
2. **Orders Sought:**
   - Pursuant to [Rule 16.21 Fed Ct / Rule 14.28 UCPR], [Paragraphs X-Y / the whole of the Statement of Claim] be struck out.
   - The [Proceeding/Claim] be dismissed.
   - In the alternative, the [Party] have leave to amend.
   - Costs (on an indemnity basis).
3. **Grounds of Application:**
   - No reasonable cause of action.
   - Evasive or ambiguous.
   - Likely to cause prejudice, embarrassment, or delay.
   - Abuse of the process of the Court.
4. **Execution:** Signature of Solicitor.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Face of the Pleading:** A strike-out application is generally decided based on the pleading itself, assuming all facts alleged are true. Do not file extensive evidence unless the ground is "abuse of process."
- **Embarrassment:** In legal drafting, a pleading is "embarrassing" if the other party cannot understand it or respond to it.
- **Specificity:** Clearly identify which paragraphs are offending and which specific ground (a-f in Rule 16.21) applies to each.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "The Respondent applies for the following orders: 1. Paragraphs 10, 11, and 12 of the Statement of Claim be struck out pursuant to Rule 16.21(1)(e) of the Federal Court Rules 2011 on the ground that they fail to disclose a reasonable cause of action."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Used to challenge "vague" or "scrambled" pleadings from the other side. Forces the opponent to clearly state their case or face dismissal, preventing wasted costs on an ill-defined dispute.
