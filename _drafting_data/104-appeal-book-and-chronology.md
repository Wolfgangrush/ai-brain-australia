## Currency Warning
Appeal Book preparation is mandatory in all Federal Court + Full Court appeals. Appellant prepares; respondent reviews + agrees content. Electronic filing via the Federal Court eFiling Portal. Pagination + indexing + searchable PDF requirements specified by Practice Note.

## Template Type
Procedural Document / Bundle

## Structural Skeleton

**APPEAL BOOK (AB)**

1. **Cover sheet**:
   - Federal Court / Full Court / High Court designation.
   - Appeal number · year · parties.
   - Volume number (if multi-volume).
   - Counsel · solicitor · date.

2. **Index of contents** — table of contents with AB pagination:
   | Tab | Document | Date | Source | AB Pagination |
   |---|---|---|---|---|
   | 1 | Notice of Appeal | YYYY-MM-DD | Court filing | 1-15 |
   | 2 | Originating Application (below) | YYYY-MM-DD | Court file | 16-25 |
   | 3 | Statement of Claim | YYYY-MM-DD | Court file | 26-55 |
   | 4 | Defence | YYYY-MM-DD | Court file | 56-90 |
   | ... | | | | |

3. **Organisation**:
   - **Section A** — Court documents (pleadings · orders · directions).
   - **Section B** — Affidavits + AEICs filed below.
   - **Section C** — Documents tendered at trial (chronological).
   - **Section D** — Expert reports.
   - **Section E** — Trial transcripts.
   - **Section F** — Reasons for judgment.
   - **Section G** — Orders appealed against.

4. **Pagination** — continuous through the AB; not by section.

5. **Tab dividers** — electronic bookmarks + physical (if hard copy required).

**LIST OF AUTHORITIES**

1. Singapore-style: HCA cases first (reverse chronological or topical).
2. FCAFC / State CoA cases next.
3. Single-judge decisions where relied upon (limited).
4. Comparative common-law (UK Supreme Court / Privy Council / NZSC) — last.
5. Statutes + regulations cited.
6. Texts (Halsbury's · Mason and Carter's Restitution · etc.) — sparingly.
7. AGLC4 citation throughout.

**CHRONOLOGY OF EVENTS** (filed with appellant's submissions)

1. **Cover sheet**.
2. **Format** — three columns: Date · Event · AB Reference.
3. **Date discipline** — ISO format · disputed dates flagged · key events bolded.
4. **Scope** — material events only; not an exhaustive AB index.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Single AB** preferred — appellant prepares · respondent reviews + agrees content.
- **Disputed-content protocol** — disagreements documented in separate side-letter; court decides if not resolved.
- **Electronic filing standards** — searchable PDF; OCR text layer; bookmarks at tab dividers.
- **Multi-volume** for large appeals — typically 5-10 volumes; pagination continuous.
- **No tactical exclusions** — comprehensive AB required; selective inclusion criticised at hearing.
- **Authorities bundle** separate from AB; chronological within topic.
- **Reading list** prepared by appellant; identifies 6-10 documents the bench should read pre-hearing.
- **Cost of AB preparation** — appellant bears; recoverable in party-party costs on success.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The appellant shall prepare an appeal book containing all documents relevant to the appeal. The appeal book shall be paginated and indexed. Parties shall use reasonable efforts to agree the content of the appeal book." (Federal Court Practice Note GPN-APP, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
AB preparation is time-consuming but critical. Disorganised ABs lose appeals — the bench cannot navigate the case. Solo practitioners should engage cooperatively with respondent's solicitors to agree content; disputes over content are usually resolved by including more rather than less. Pagination + indexing + electronic bookmarks are tedious but the difference between effective and unusable appellate advocacy.
