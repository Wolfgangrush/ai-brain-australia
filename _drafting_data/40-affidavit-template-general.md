## Currency Warning
Check state-specific rules for "Remote Witnessing." While Federal Court permits it under certain conditions, state rules (e.g. NSW Electronic Transactions Act) have specific requirements for video-link witnessing.

## Template Type
Affidavit

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Court Header:** Case details.
2. **Deponent Details:** Full name, address, occupation.
3. **The Oath/Affirmation:** "I [name] [say on oath / solemnly and sincerely affirm]..."
4. **Body Paragraphs:**
   - Paragraph 1: "I am the [Applicant/Respondent/Director of...] and am authorised to make this affidavit."
   - Subsequent Paragraphs: Chronological statement of facts.
5. **Exhibits/Annexures:** "Attached to this affidavit and marked [Initials]-1 is a copy of..."
6. **Execution Block:**
   - Place and Date of swearing/affirming.
   - Signature of Deponent.
   - Signature and details of Witness (Lawyer/JP).
7. **Certificate of Annexure:** Required on the first page of every attachment.

## Drafting Conventions
- **First Person:** Must use "I".
- **Direct Knowledge:** State facts within the deponent's own knowledge, except in interlocutory applications where "information and belief" may be permitted (if the source is stated).
- **No Legal Argument:** Do not include legal submissions or conclusions in an affidavit.
- **Signing Every Page:** Deponent and Witness must sign the bottom of every page.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "I, [Name], [Occupation], of [Address], [say on oath / affirm]: 1. I am the Plaintiff in this proceeding. 2. On 1 January 2024, I received an email from the Defendant, a copy of which is annexed and marked 'A'..."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The primary vehicle for placing evidence before a court in written form. Solo practitioners must ensure strict compliance with formal requirements to avoid the affidavit being ruled inadmissible.
