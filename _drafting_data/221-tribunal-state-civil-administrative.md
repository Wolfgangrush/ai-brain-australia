## Currency Warning
Australia has a tribunal system at Federal + State level. **Federal**: Administrative Review Tribunal (ART) — replaced Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) from October 2024. **State**: NCAT (NSW · est. 2013) · VCAT (Vic · est. 1998) · QCAT (Qld · est. 2009) · SACAT (SA) · WAST (WA, limited) · ACAT (ACT) · NTCAT (NT). Each state tribunal has specialised divisions covering consumer disputes · tenancy · administrative review · guardianship · disciplinary · privacy · planning.

## Structural Skeleton

**(A) NSW CIVIL AND ADMINISTRATIVE TRIBUNAL (NCAT)**

Divisions:
- **Consumer and Commercial Division** — consumer disputes · home building · motor vehicles · retail leases · residential tenancies.
- **Administrative and Equal Opportunity Division** — admin review · anti-discrimination.
- **Guardianship Division**.
- **Occupational Division** — disciplinary (medical · legal · pharmacy · etc.).

Procedure:
1. **Form** — varies by division.
2. **Filing fee** — variable (waivable on hardship).
3. **Conciliation** — common pre-hearing step.
4. **Hearing** — informal · documents-based or oral · adjudicator-led.
5. **Appeal** — to NCAT Appeal Panel; further appeal to NSW Supreme Court on questions of law (Civil and Administrative Tribunal Act 2013 s 80).

**(B) VICTORIAN CIVIL AND ADMINISTRATIVE TRIBUNAL (VCAT)**

Lists:
- **Civil Claims List** — up to A$15,000 (Small Claims Procedure).
- **Residential Tenancies List**.
- **Owners Corporations List**.
- **Building and Property List**.
- **Domain Name Disputes**.
- **Guardianship List**.
- **Planning and Environment List**.
- **Administrative Review List**.

Procedure: similar conciliation + hearing structure; appeals to Victorian Supreme Court on questions of law.

**(C) FEDERAL ADMINISTRATIVE REVIEW TRIBUNAL (ART)**

Divisions:
- **Migration and Refugee Division**.
- **National Disability Insurance Scheme Division**.
- **Veterans' Appeals Division**.
- **Social Security Division**.
- **Tax and Commercial Division**.
- **General Division** (residual).

Replaced AAT October 2024. Procedure:
1. **Application** — Form A1 typically; varies by division.
2. **Time-limit** — 28 days from decision (most categories; some shorter).
3. **Internal review** of original decision often required first.
4. **De novo review** of the original administrative decision.
5. **Appeal** — to Federal Court on question of law.

**(D) COMMON FILING ELEMENTS** (across CATs + ART)

1. **Heading** — tribunal · division · case number · parties.
2. **Applicant** — name · address · representative (if any).
3. **Respondent** — government agency / opposing party.
4. **Decision impugned** — date · decision-maker · operative terms.
5. **Grounds for review** — administrative-law grounds (errors of law · failure to consider · etc.) OR merits-review grounds.
6. **Reliefs sought**:
   - Set aside decision.
   - Substitute different decision.
   - Remit to original decision-maker with directions.
   - Costs (often unavailable in CAT jurisdictions; specific provisions).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Forum-specific procedure** — verify per-tribunal Practice Direction.
- **Time-limits strict** — 28 days typical for ART; varies for state CATs (often 28 days; some 60 days).
- **Self-represented friendly** — tribunals designed for lay applicants.
- **Lawyer representation** — permitted but tribunal may regulate (NCAT s 45; VCAT s 62 — leave required in some categories).
- **Costs orders** — generally NOT available (CAT general rule) except in specific circumstances or for vexatious conduct.
- **Internal review first** — for many federal decisions (Centrelink · NDIS · immigration), internal review of agency decision required before ART application.
- **De novo review** — ART exercises original decision-maker's powers; "what is the correct or preferable decision" test.
- **Appeal scope** — to court only on question of law; merits not reviewable.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The Tribunal has jurisdiction to review decisions of administrative decision-makers where the relevant enabling Act so provides. The Tribunal stands in the shoes of the original decision-maker and exercises the same powers. The Tribunal's task is to determine the correct or preferable decision on the material before it." (Administrative Review Tribunal Act 2024 + state CAT Acts, synthesised.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Tribunal practice covers wide swathes of Australian law: consumer disputes · residential tenancy · planning · administrative review · disciplinary. Solo practitioners often have major tribunal-side practice (especially in NCAT consumer-and-commercial; VCAT residential tenancies; QCAT consumer-and-trader; ART migration). Each tribunal has distinct procedural quirks; verify per matter. Costs availability is the principal commercial consideration — successful tribunal advocates often cannot recover costs even on full success.
