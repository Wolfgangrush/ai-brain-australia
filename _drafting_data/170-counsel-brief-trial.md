## Authority Tier
Primary — Legal Profession Uniform Law (LPUL) + ABA Barristers' Conduct Rules + State Bar Rules (where LPUL not adopted).

## Currency Warning
Australian profession is split (advocates → barristers; instructing → solicitors) in most jurisdictions. LPUL covers NSW + Victoria; other states have state-specific Legal Profession Acts. Senior Counsel (SC) appointed under state Bar rules. Direct briefing by clients permitted in limited circumstances under LPUL Rule 96.

## Template Type
Letter (Instructing Solicitor → Counsel)

## Structural Skeleton

1. **Letterhead** — solicitor's firm.
2. **Date · Counsel's Chambers** — addressed to specific counsel.
3. **Re** — court · case number · parties · brief description.
4. **Confidentiality recital** — *"This brief is confidential. Contents subject to legal advice + litigation privilege. No disclosure to any third party without our prior written consent."*
5. **Engagement scope**:
   - Trial advocacy.
   - Appellate advocacy (FCAFC / HCA special leave / State CoA).
   - Interlocutory application (specified).
   - Pre-trial advisory + cross-examination preparation.
   - Settlement negotiation.
   - Combination.
6. **Conflict-check confirmation request** — *"Please confirm that you and your chambers have no conflict of interest in acting for our client."*
7. **Client identification** — name · authorised representative · capacity.
8. **Factual background** — chronological narrative (1-3 pages).
9. **Procedural posture** — what has happened in the action.
10. **Strategic objectives** — what client wants to achieve.
11. **Materials enclosed** — schedule:
    - Pleadings.
    - AEICs.
    - Documents discovered.
    - Prior counsel opinions.
    - Expert reports.
    - Correspondence with opposing party.
    - Settlement positions to date.
12. **Specific instructions** — what counsel is asked to do:
    - Settle pleadings.
    - Draft written submissions.
    - Conduct cross-examination of named witnesses.
    - Lead trial advocacy.
    - Argue specific interlocutory application.
    - Provide opinion on (specific issue).
13. **Settlement authority** — client's instructions on settlement range; whether counsel has authority within range.
14. **Fee arrangement**:
    - Hourly rate · brief fee + refresher · success fee (subject to LPUL).
    - Capped fee or fixed fee where applicable.
    - GST treatment.
    - Estimated total · billing cadence.
15. **Timeline** — opinion / submission due by `<date>` · trial date · hearing dates.
16. **Privileged communications** — confirm under LPUL + Evidence Act ss 118-119 privilege between solicitor + barrister.
17. **Closing** — *"Please acknowledge receipt and confirm acceptance of these instructions."*
18. **Signature** — instructing solicitor.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Conflict check FIRST** — counsel must confirm no conflict before reading substantive papers.
- **Materials schedule comprehensive** — incomplete brief leads to incomplete advice.
- **Strategic objectives explicit** — counsel needs to know what "winning" looks like.
- **Settlement authority pre-agreed** — avoid mid-trial surprises.
- **Brief fee + refresher** — traditional for trial advocacy; daily refresher during trial.
- **Senior Counsel (SC)** — separately retained for matters of complexity; higher fee scale.
- **Privileged status** preserved under LPUL + Evidence Act.
- **Direct briefing** — under LPUL Rule 96, certain clients (sophisticated business clients) may brief direct; most matters still require instructing solicitor.
- **Bar Council rules** — fee arrangements documented in writing per LPUL + ABA Conduct Rules.
- **AGLC4 citation** — counsel's submissions / opinions use AGLC4 throughout.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"A barrister must promote and protect fearlessly and by all proper and lawful means the lay client's best interests and do so without regard to his or her own interests or to any consequences to the barrister or to any other person." (ABA Barristers' Conduct Rules + LPUL, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Solo solicitors brief specialist counsel for (a) appellate advocacy where appellate experience required; (b) complex trial advocacy with multiple witnesses; (c) Senior Counsel for high-stakes matters; (d) niche specialisms (admiralty · construction · IP · tax · native title). The brief is the foundation document — counsel's performance depends on it. Solo practitioners often under-invest in briefs; the payoff for thorough drafting is disproportionate.
