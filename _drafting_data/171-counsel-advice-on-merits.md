## Authority Tier
Primary — LPUL + ABA Conduct Rules + Evidence Act 1995.

## Currency Warning
Advice on merits is requested by instructing solicitor before committing to litigation. Documented assessment of (a) prospects of success; (b) likely quantum recovery; (c) cost-benefit analysis; (d) strategic recommendations. Subject to legal advice + litigation privilege.

## Template Type
Opinion / Memorandum

## Structural Skeleton

1. **Cover sheet** — counsel's chambers letterhead.
2. **Heading** — "Advice on Merits".
3. **Re** — client · matter · instructing solicitor.
4. **Date · Reference**.
5. **Confidentiality + Privilege recital** — *"This advice is confidential, subject to legal advice + litigation privilege (Evidence Act 1995 ss 118-119). Disclosure to any third party (other than the client and those at the firm acting on the matter) may waive privilege."*
6. **Instructions received** — summary of question posed.
7. **Materials reviewed** — schedule of documents · pleadings · affidavits · correspondence · prior advice.
8. **Background facts** — concise factual matrix.
9. **Cause(s) of action / defence available**:
   - Each cause / defence identified.
   - Elements of each.
   - Application of elements to facts.
   - Prospects of proof — strong / moderate / weak (with reasons).
10. **Anticipated defences / counter-arguments** + assessment of strength.
11. **Procedural considerations**:
    - Jurisdiction (Federal vs State; multi-jurisdiction; cross-vesting).
    - Limitation — when did cause accrue · is it time-barred · is extension / relation-back available?
    - Forum (Federal Court · State Supreme Court · District Court · ART · FCFCOA · tribunal).
    - Choice of law (where multi-state).
12. **Quantum analysis**:
    - Heads of loss.
    - Quantification methodology.
    - Likely recoverable range.
    - Respondent's ability to pay (creditworthiness · asset position · insurance).
13. **Strategic options**:
    - Commence proceedings · pre-action protocol letter · mediation · arbitration · settlement · administrative tribunal route.
    - Risks and benefits of each.
14. **Costs estimate**:
    - Pre-action.
    - Issue + pleadings stage.
    - Discovery + AEIC stage.
    - Trial.
    - Appeal.
    - Recovery prospect (party-party costs).
15. **Recommendations** — clear, ranked options with reasons.
16. **Caveats and limitations** — assumptions made · materials not yet reviewed · further investigation needed.
17. **Counsel's signature** — name · seniority (Junior Counsel / Senior Counsel) · date.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Honest assessment** — duty to give plain advice, not advocate.
- **Quantification ranges** preferred to point estimates.
- **Probability framing** — *"good prospects of success" (60-75%) · "reasonable prospects" (40-60%) · "defensive position only" (<40%)* with concrete reasons.
- **Risk identification** — known unknowns flagged.
- **Settlement framing** — practical settlement range with reasoning.
- **Cost-benefit analysis** — pursuing claim of A$50,000 with party-party costs of A$30,000 against impecunious respondent may not be economic; spell out.
- **Caveats genuine** — assumed facts stated; if assumption proves wrong, advice may shift.
- **No guarantees** — LPUL + ABA Conduct Rules prohibit guarantee of outcome.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"A barrister must inform the instructing solicitor (or the client if directly briefed) of the relative strengths of the client's case and the risks of the proceedings, in order that the client can make informed decisions about settlement and the conduct of the proceedings." (ABA Barristers' Conduct Rules, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The advice on merits is the lawyer's principal pre-litigation deliverable to the client. It documents the analysis, manages client expectations, and creates the paper trail showing the lawyer fulfilled the duty of competent advice. Solo practitioners often skip the formal advice document and proceed straight to action — risky because (a) client may later dispute that risks were disclosed; (b) cost overruns appear less justified without the up-front cost-benefit analysis. Always document the advice formally for matters above A$50,000 in dispute.
