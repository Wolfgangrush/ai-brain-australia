## Currency Warning
Australia recognises:
- **Legal advice privilege** (Evidence Act s 118) — confidential communications for the dominant purpose of giving / receiving legal advice.
- **Litigation privilege** (s 119) — confidential communications for the dominant purpose of pending or contemplated litigation.
- **Without-prejudice privilege** (s 131) — settlement negotiations.
- **Common interest privilege** — extends privilege to joint clients · co-insureds · co-defendants with shared legal interest.
- *Esso* dominant purpose test — replaced the earlier "sole purpose" test from *Grant v Downs*.

## Template Type
Procedural Document (annexed to List of Documents as Schedule 3)

## Structural Skeleton

**Title**: "Schedule 3 — Documents Withheld on Grounds of Privilege"

**Columnar table format**:

| No. | Date | Document type | Author | Recipient(s) | General description | Privilege claimed | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | YYYY-MM-DD | Email | [Author] | [Recipient(s)] | Legal advice on [generic topic] | Legal advice privilege (s 118 Evidence Act) | Communication between client and external counsel for the dominant purpose of obtaining legal advice |
| 2 | YYYY-MM-DD | File note | [Author] | [Internal] | Litigation strategy memorandum for anticipated proceedings against [generic counterparty] | Litigation privilege (s 119 Evidence Act) | Created at a time when litigation was reasonably contemplated; dominant purpose was preparation for that litigation |
| 3 | YYYY-MM-DD | Letter marked "WITHOUT PREJUDICE" | [Author] | [Recipient] | Settlement proposal | Without-prejudice privilege (s 131 Evidence Act) | Communication made in a genuine attempt to settle the dispute |

**Declaration**: "The party making this list claims privilege over the above documents on the grounds stated. The party reserves the right to amend this list if further documents come to light or further claims of privilege are made."

## Drafting Conventions
- **Sufficient description without waiving privilege** — describe the SUBJECT-MATTER generically (e.g. "advice on regulatory compliance") without revealing PRIVILEGED CONTENT.
- **Date + author + recipients** — required for the opposing party to assess the claim.
- **Document type** — letter · email · memorandum · file note · attendance note · briefing.
- **Privilege type** — legal advice / litigation / without-prejudice / common interest / joint.
- **Basis line** — short reasoned ground tied to the elements + statutory section.
- **Waiver risk** — partial disclosure of a document may waive privilege over the rest; *Mann v Carnell* (1999) 201 CLR 1.
- **In-house counsel** — privilege applies where the in-house lawyer is acting in legal capacity, not business/commercial capacity. *Pratt Holdings Pty Ltd v FCT* (2004) 136 FCR 357.
- **Dominant purpose test** — *Esso Australia Resources Ltd v FCT* (1999) 201 CLR 49.
- **Joint clients** — privilege does not protect documents between joint clients vis-à-vis each other.
- **Common interest** — preserves privilege among parties with common legal interest (e.g., insurer + insured).
- **Crime/fraud exception** — no privilege where communication is in furtherance of a crime or fraud (s 125 Evidence Act).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Evidence is not to be adduced if, on objection by a client, the court finds that adducing the evidence would result in disclosure of a confidential communication made between the client and a lawyer, or the contents of a confidential document, that was prepared, for the dominant purpose of the lawyer providing legal advice to the client." (Evidence Act 1995 (Cth), Section 118, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The privilege log is the principal battleground in litigation against well-resourced corporate parties. Australian courts apply *Esso* dominant-purpose test rigorously. Internal management memos not prepared for a lawyer are NOT privileged. *Pratt Holdings* limits in-house counsel privilege to legal-capacity communications. Solo practitioners advising corporate clients should establish documented client-team early to preserve privilege; communications labelled "legal-advice" but substantively about commercial decisions fail.
