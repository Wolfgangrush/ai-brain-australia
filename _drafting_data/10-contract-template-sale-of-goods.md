## Currency Warning
The Sale of Goods Acts are "old" statutes (mostly based on the UK 1893 Act). Practitioners must cross-reference with the **Australian Consumer Law (ACL)** (Competition and Consumer Act 2010, Schedule 2), which provides non-excludable consumer guarantees for transactions under $100,000 or for personal/household use.

## Template Type
Contract

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties:** Full legal names, ACN/ABN, Registered Office.
2. **Defined Terms and Interpretation:** Specifically "Goods", "Price", "Delivery Point".
3. **Agreement to Sell and Purchase:** The core obligation.
4. **Price and Payment:**
   - Amount and GST treatment.
   - Payment terms (e.g., 30 days from invoice).
5. **Delivery and Acceptance:**
   - Time and place of delivery.
   - Inspection rights (Sale of Goods Act s 37 NSW equivalent).
   - Rejection of non-conforming goods.
6. **Title and Risk:**
   - Passing of risk (usually on delivery).
   - Passing of title (on payment in full - Retention of Title/Romalpa clause).
7. **Warranties and Guarantees:**
   - Title, quiet possession, and freedom from encumbrance.
   - Correspondence with description/sample.
   - Quality and fitness for purpose (subject to ACL).
8. **Liability and Indemnities:**
   - Limitation of liability (carve-out for ACL non-excludable terms).
9. **Termination:** For breach or insolvency.
10. **Dispute Resolution:** Negotiation before litigation.
11. **General Provisions:**
    - Governing Law (State-specific).
    - Entire Agreement.
    - Notices.

## Drafting Conventions
- **PPSA Alignment:** Retention of Title clauses should be drafted to allow for registration as a "Purchase Money Security Interest" (PMSI) on the Personal Property Securities Register (PPSR).
- **ACL Disclaimer:** Must include mandatory wording if the buyer is a "consumer" under the ACL: *"Our goods come with guarantees that cannot be excluded under the Australian Consumer Law..."*
- **State Neutrality:** While state Acts are uniform, specify the jurisdiction (e.g., NSW) to avoid ambiguity.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "The Seller warrants that: (a) it has the right to sell the Goods; (b) the Goods are free from any charge or encumbrance in favour of any third party not declared or known to the Buyer; and (c) the Buyer will enjoy quiet possession of the Goods." (Standard warranty language derived from Sale of Goods Act 1923 (NSW) s 17).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Used when advising small business clients on supply agreements or B2B sales. Crucial for ensuring "Retention of Title" is enforceable against liquidators and that ACL mandatory notices are included for hybrid B2B/B2C sellers.
