## Currency Warning
Settlement deed is the documented end-product of a successful mediation OR private negotiation. Distinct from a consent order (file 62 — already in existing corpus). Deed provides 12-year limitation period (vs 6 years for contract) + can be enforced without proof of consideration. Singapore Convention on Mediation — Australia signed; verify ratification status for cross-border enforceability.

## Template Type
Settlement Deed

## Structural Skeleton

1. **Parties**:
   - Full identification — natural persons (full names · DOB · ABN/TFN for unrepresented entities) or corporate (full name · ACN · ABN · registered address).
   - Authorised representatives + capacity (board resolution · power of attorney annexed).
2. **Recitals**:
   - Existence of the underlying dispute (without admission of liability).
   - Conduct of mediation (date · mediator · NMAS reference) OR private settlement negotiations.
   - Parties' desire to settle the dispute.
3. **Definitions**:
   - "Effective Date" · "Underlying Claims" · "Released Parties" · "Confidential Information" · "Settlement Sum" · "Performance Date".
4. **Settlement Terms**:
   - **Payment** — amount · currency · payment schedule · interest on late payment.
   - **Non-monetary obligations** — performance · transfer of assets · IP licensing · undertakings · destruction of confidential material.
   - **Conditions precedent** — regulatory approvals · board approvals · payment receipt.
5. **Mutual Releases**:
   - Full and final settlement of all known and unknown claims arising from the subject-matter.
   - Carve-outs (criminal liability · regulatory penalties · fraud · enforcement of this deed).
6. **Discontinuance of Proceedings**:
   - Joint application to the court of seizing to discontinue (Notice of Discontinuance under FCR / UCPR).
   - Consent order under FCR Rule 41.22 OR state-equivalent (cross-reference file 62) — preserves Court's jurisdiction to enforce.
   - Each party bears own costs (typically) OR specified costs allocation.
7. **Confidentiality**:
   - Existence and terms of the deed.
   - Limited disclosure carve-outs (auditors · regulators · court enforcement · taxation authorities).
   - Press / public statements — agreed wording or none.
8. **No Admission of Liability** — explicit clause.
9. **Enforceability Mechanism — DEED EXECUTED UNDER SEAL**:
   - Executed as a Deed (not merely a contract).
   - Witnessed by independent witness (state-specific requirements).
   - Sealed where required (some states).
   - 12-year limitation period under state Limitation Acts.
10. **Singapore Convention Compliance** (for international mediated settlements):
    - Express statement: "This settlement results from mediation and is intended to be relied upon under the Singapore Convention on Mediation."
    - Mediator's signature or separate statement confirming mediation conducted.
    - International character documented.
11. **Anti-Circumvention** — parties cannot file released claims in another jurisdiction.
12. **Governing Law + Forum** — typically state law (state of execution) · forum for disputes arising from this deed.
13. **Boilerplate**:
    - Entire agreement.
    - Variation in writing only.
    - Severance.
    - Counterparts.
    - Electronic execution (under Electronic Transactions Act 1999 Cth + state equivalents).
14. **Execution Page** — formal deed execution per state requirements:
    - **Individual**: signature · witness signature · date.
    - **Corporate**: under common seal OR per Corporations Act s 127 (two directors / one director + company secretary / sole director-secretary).

## Drafting Conventions
- **DEED not CONTRACT** — executed under seal where required; 12-year limitation; no consideration required.
- **State-specific execution requirements** — verify per state of execution.
- **No admission** — explicit no-admission-of-liability clause.
- **Releases carve-outs** — preserve right to enforce deed itself · criminal · regulatory · fraud · third-party claims.
- **Tomlin-style consent order** (file 62) — where proceedings on foot, schedule terms preserves confidentiality + Court enforcement.
- **Confidentiality narrowly scoped** — typical carve-outs (auditors · regulators · enforcement · ATO).
- **Anti-circumvention** — preventing parties filing released claims in other jurisdictions.
- **Tax structuring** — settlement payments may be deductible / subject to withholding / GST in some contexts; cross-reference Income Tax Assessment Act 1997.
- **Singapore Convention** — for international cross-border; Australia signed but ratification status varies.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"A deed executed under seal or in accordance with the prescribed formalities is enforceable without consideration. The limitation period for actions on a deed is twelve years from the cause of action accruing." (State Conveyancing / Property Law Acts + Limitation Acts, synthesised.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The settlement deed is the closing document for resolved disputes. Solo practitioners must triage: (a) deed (under seal · 12-year limitation) vs contract (6-year limitation); (b) consent order vs no court involvement; (c) Singapore Convention compliance for international elements. Execution formalities per state law are critical — defective execution risks unenforceability.
