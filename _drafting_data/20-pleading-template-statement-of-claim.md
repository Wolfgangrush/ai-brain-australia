## Authority Tier
Court Rule Form (UCPR NSW / Federal Court Rules)

## Currency Warning
Note the *Civil Dispute Resolution Act 2011* (Cth) requirement for a "Genuine Steps Statement" to be filed with any originating application in the Federal Court. Failure to file can result in costs penalties.

## Template Type
Pleading / Originating Process

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Court Header:** Name of Court, Registry, Case Number.
2. **Parties:** Full names and roles (Plaintiff/Applicant, Defendant/Respondent).
3. **Relief Claimed:**
   - Specific orders sought (e.g., Damages, Specific Performance).
   - Interest (pursuant to statute, e.g., s 100 *Civil Procedure Act 2005* NSW).
   - Costs.
4. **Pleadings (Material Facts):**
   - **Introduction:** Identification of the parties and their relationships.
   - **The Contract/Duty:** Existence of the legal relationship (e.g., "By an agreement in writing dated...").
   - **The Breach:** Specific acts or omissions constituting the breach.
   - **Causation and Damage:** How the breach led to the loss claimed.
5. **Particulars:** Detailed info (e.g., "Particulars of Breach", "Particulars of Loss and Damage") following the relevant paragraphs.
6. **Signing Block:** Signature of Solicitor/Counsel.
7. **Solicitor's Certificate:** (Under LPUL) certifying there are reasonable prospects of success.
8. **Affidavit Verifying:** (If required by the specific court/rules).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Numbered Paragraphs:** Mandatory. Each paragraph should contain a single allegation of fact.
- **Material Facts, Not Evidence:** Do not plead the evidence by which facts are to be proved (UCPR 14.7).
- **Particulars:** Must be sufficient to prevent "trial by ambush."
- **Citing Statutes:** Always cite the specific section (e.g., "In breach of section 18 of the *Australian Consumer Law*...").

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "The Plaintiff claims the following relief: 1. Damages. 2. Interest pursuant to section 100 of the Civil Procedure Act 2005. 3. Costs." (Standard NSW UCPR relief format).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The foundational document for any civil litigation. Solo practitioners use this to define the scope of the dispute. Errors here can lead to strike-out applications or limited recovery at trial.
