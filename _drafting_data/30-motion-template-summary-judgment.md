## Authority Tier
Court Rule Form / Statutory Authority (s 31A Fed Ct Act)

## Currency Warning
The "no reasonable prospect of success" test under s 31A is distinct from the older "hopeless or bound to fail" common law test. Ensure submissions cite *Spencer v Commonwealth* [2010] HCA 28 regarding the application of s 31A.

## Template Type
Motion / Interlocutory Application

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Court Header:** Case details.
2. **Orders Sought:**
   - Summary judgment in favor of the [Applicant/Respondent] pursuant to [s 31A Federal Court Act / Rule 13.1 UCPR].
   - Dismissal of the [Proceeding/Defence].
   - Costs.
3. **Grounds of Application:** Brief statement of why the order is sought (e.g., "The Respondent has no reasonable prospect of successfully defending the proceeding").
4. **Supporting Documents:**
   - Affidavit of [Name] filed herewith.
   - Outline of Submissions.
5. **Hearing Details:** Date and time (if known) or "To be fixed."
6. **Execution:** Signature of Solicitor.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Interlocutory vs. Final:** Summary judgment is a final order made on an interlocutory application.
- **Evidence:** Must be supported by an affidavit that verifies the facts on which the application is based and states the deponent's belief that there is no reasonable prospect of success.
- **Threshold:** The application must address the high bar of depriving a party of their "day in court."

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "The Applicant applies for the following interlocutory orders: 1. Pursuant to section 31A of the Federal Court of Australia Act 1976 (Cth), judgment be given for the Applicant against the Respondent. 2. The Respondent pay the Applicant's costs of the proceeding."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
A powerful tool to end unmeritorious litigation early. Solo practitioners use this to protect clients from "nuisance" claims or to quickly recover debts where there is no real defence.
