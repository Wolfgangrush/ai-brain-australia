## Authority Tier
Statutory Requirement (LPUL) / Court Convention

## Currency Warning
Practitioners should check the current "Costs Assessment Guidelines" for the maximum hourly rates typically allowed by assessors in NSW, which may differ from the rates in the Costs Agreement.

## Template Type
Bill of Costs / Itemised Bill

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Header:** "Itemised Bill of Costs" or "Bill for Assessment."
2. **Summary Table:**
   - Total Professional Fees.
   - Total Disbursements.
   - GST.
   - Total Amount Due.
3. **Itemised Schedule (The Columns):**
   - Item No.
   - Date.
   - Description of Service (e.g., "Attendance on Counsel at conference").
   - Person performing the work (Solicitor, Paralegal, Partner).
   - Time spent (in 6-minute units).
   - Charge ($).
4. **Disbursements Schedule:**
   - Date.
   - Description (e.g., "Court filing fee").
   - Amount.
5. **Notice of Rights:** Mandatory text under s 192 LPUL informing the client of their right to challenge the bill.

## Drafting Conventions
- **6-Minute Units:** The standard unit of time in the Australian legal profession.
- **Specificity:** "Research" or "File management" is often rejected; use "Researching cases on s 18 of ACL" or "Collating documents for discovery."
- **GST:** Must be clearly broken out.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "Item 1. 10/05/2024. Drafting Statement of Claim. (Solicitor). 1.5 hours (15 units). $450.00."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The document used to actually get paid. If a client refuses to pay, a solo practitioner must provide this itemised bill before they can take any recovery action or proceed to costs assessment.
