## Currency Warning
Set-aside discretion liberal where respondent moves promptly, has meritorious defence, and shows reasonable explanation for default. Discretion exercised on *Evans v Bartlam* principles: (a) meritorious defence (most important); (b) reasonable explanation for default; (c) promptness; (d) prejudice to applicant. Costs of obtaining default judgment + set-aside application usually borne by respondent on success.

## Template Type
Application + Supporting Affidavit

## Structural Skeleton

**Interlocutory Application**

1. **Heading** — court · case number · parties.
2. **Title** — "Interlocutory Application — Set Aside Default Judgment".
3. **Reliefs sought**:
   - Default judgment dated `<date>` set aside.
   - Respondent granted leave to file Notice of Address + Defence within `<period>`.
   - Stay of execution pending the set-aside application.
   - Costs.
4. **Grounds** — meritorious defence + reasonable explanation + prompt application + no irremediable prejudice to applicant.

**Supporting Affidavit**

1. **Deponent** — respondent.
2. **Explanation for default**:
   - Defective service (didn't receive papers).
   - Service to wrong address.
   - Communication failure (illness · travel · solicitor error).
   - Administrative oversight.
3. **Meritorious defence** — substantive grounds; pleaded with sufficient particularity:
   - Cause of action contested (denial of facts · denial of breach · denial of quantum).
   - Affirmative defences (limitation · accord and satisfaction · contribution).
   - Counterclaim (with quantum).
4. **Prompt application** — explanation of delay (if any) between learning of judgment + filing application.
5. **No irremediable prejudice** to applicant — what can be cured by costs · undertaking · payment into court.
6. **Conditional relief proposed** — payment into court · security · partial admission.
7. **Annexures**:
   - Proposed Defence (annexed in full).
   - Evidence supporting meritorious defence (contemporaneous documents).
   - Affidavit of service of judgment (if disputed).
8. **Statutory declaration** (Statutory Declarations Act 1959 Cth or State equivalent).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Meritorious defence is the lever** — without it, even good reasons for default fail.
- **Proposed Defence must be substantive** — bare denial + boilerplate doesn't qualify; specific factual contentions required.
- **Promptness** — moves within days/weeks of learning of judgment; multi-month delays scrutinised.
- **Service defect grounds** — if proven, court SETS ASIDE judgment as of right (not discretionary).
- **Conditional set-aside** — court may impose conditions (security · payment in) where default was without excuse but defence is meritorious.
- **Costs of set-aside** — typically respondent pays applicant's costs of obtaining judgment + costs of set-aside application, on indemnity basis where default was egregious.
- **Stay of execution** — file simultaneously to prevent enforcement during hearing.
- **AGLC4** — *Evans v Bartlam* [1937] AC 473.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The Court may set aside or vary a judgment given by default on such terms as it thinks just." (FCR 2011 Rule 39.05, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The set-aside application is one of the highest-frequency litigation tasks for solo defence-side practice. Common triggers: defendant didn't receive papers (incorrect address) · NOA filed late by 1-2 days · administrative oversight · death/illness · solicitor error. The meritorious-defence pleading is the lever — without it, even good reasons fail. Solo practitioners should triage default-judgment-served clients within 48 hours.
