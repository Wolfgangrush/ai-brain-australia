## Currency Warning
Written submissions for interlocutory applications heard by Registrar / Single Judge. Short skeletons preferred — typically 5-15 pages. Pre-application correspondence + attempts to resolve consensually expected.

## Template Type
Submission

## Structural Skeleton

1. **Cover sheet** — court · case number · parties · application reference · counsel · solicitor · date.
2. **Heading / Title** — "Skeleton Submissions of the [Applicant / Respondent] for the Hearing of [Interlocutory Application] on `<date>`".
3. **Index** (if over 5 pages).
4. **Introduction** (1 paragraph) — application reference · relief sought in one sentence.
5. **Background** (1-2 paragraphs) — minimum facts for the bench.
6. **Procedural posture** (1 paragraph) — stage of the action; what has happened.
7. **Issue(s) for determination** — bulleted (1-3 questions).
8. **Legal framework** — applicable rule + leading authority for the application type.
9. **Application of law to facts** — concise; key documents flagged with bundle pagination.
10. **Anticipated counter-arguments + response** — pre-empt opposing party.
11. **Reliefs sought** — specific terms of order proposed.
12. **Costs** — basis + scale.
13. **Conclusion** (1-2 sentences).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Brevity is mandatory** — Registrars / Judges have heavy interlocutory loads.
- **One issue per ground** — separate authority for each.
- **Pre-application correspondence** — attach (if helpful) to show good-faith attempts.
- **Authority spread**: 2-3 strong authorities (HCA / FCAFC / State CoA); quote pithy operative passages.
- **No factual essays** — facts in affidavit; skeleton summarises.
- **Form of order** — attach a draft order in sought form.
- **AGLC4** — neutral citation first.
- **Genuine steps statement** — for proceedings under Civil Dispute Resolution Act 2011 (Cth), confirm CDRA compliance.
- **Cross-reference to FCR + State UCPRs** — depending on forum.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Written submissions for interlocutory applications shall be concise and focused on the issues for determination. The Court does not require extensive factual narrative in interlocutory submissions." (Federal Court PN GPN-WRTS, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The interlocutory-application skeleton is the workhorse document of Australian civil litigation. Solo practitioners should develop a template + refine per case. The bench's reading time is 5-15 minutes per skeleton; front-load the relief + operative test so order is grantable from skeleton alone.
