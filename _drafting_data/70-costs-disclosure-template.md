## Authority Tier
Statutory Requirement (LPUL) / Law Society Precedent

## Currency Warning
The $750 and $3,000 disclosure thresholds are subject to periodic review. Always check the *Legal Profession Uniform General Rules 2015* for current figures.

## Template Type
Costs Disclosure / Compliance Document

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Basis of Calculation:** Explanation of hourly rates, fixed fees, or scale costs.
2. **Estimate of Total Costs:** A range or single figure including GST and disbursements.
3. **Variables:** Factors that may increase or decrease the estimate.
4. **Billing Frequency:** When invoices will be sent (e.g., monthly).
5. **Client's Rights:**
   - To negotiate a costs agreement.
   - To receive a bill and request an itemised bill.
   - To be notified of substantial changes to the estimate (s 174(1)(b)).
6. **Dispute Resolution:** Information on how to contest a bill via the Legal Services Commissioner or Costs Assessment.
7. **Acknowledgement:** Section for the client to sign (though disclosure is a unilateral obligation).

## Drafting Conventions
- **"Genuine and Reasonable":** The estimate must be a sincere attempt to predict costs; "to be advised" is not sufficient and can lead to the costs agreement being set aside.
- **Plain Language:** Mandatory under s 174(3) to ensure the client understands.
- **Timeliness:** Disclosure must be made "as soon as practicable" after instructions are given.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "You have the right to: (a) negotiate a costs agreement with us; (b) receive a bill from us; (c) request an itemised bill within 30 days after receiving a bill that is not itemised; and (d) be notified of any substantial change to the matters disclosed." (Standard s 174 disclosure wording).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Strict compliance is non-negotiable for solo practitioners. Failure to disclose means the costs agreement is void, the lawyer cannot sue for the debt, and may face professional misconduct charges.
