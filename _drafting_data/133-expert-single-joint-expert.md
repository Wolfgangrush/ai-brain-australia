## Currency Warning
Single Joint Expert (SJE) is appointed by court direction (FCR Rule 23.16) typically where the technical issue is narrow + the cost of two party-appointed experts would be disproportionate. SJE's report is the only expert evidence on the issue absent leave for additional. Increasingly common in valuation · construction · medical-quantum cases.

## Template Type
Court Direction + Joint Instruction Letter + SJE Engagement

## Structural Skeleton

**(A) APPLICATION FOR SJE APPOINTMENT**

1. **Interlocutory Application** by either party (or by direction of court).
2. **Affidavit** establishing:
   - Technical issue narrow enough for single expert.
   - Cost-disproportionate for two experts.
   - Proposed expert (with consent + CV).
   - Parties' agreement (if reached).

**(B) COURT DIRECTION** (typical wording)

```
1. [Expert Name] of [Firm/Address] is appointed as Single Joint Expert under
   Rule 23.16 of the Federal Court Rules 2011 to provide expert evidence on
   the following issues:
   (a) [Issue 1];
   (b) [Issue 2].

2. The parties shall jointly instruct the SJE within 14 days of this order.

3. The SJE shall provide a written report by [date].

4. The SJE's fees and disbursements shall be borne equally by the parties
   in the first instance, subject to final costs orders.

5. The SJE's report shall be the only expert evidence on the appointed
   issues, unless the Court otherwise directs.
```

**(C) JOINT INSTRUCTION LETTER**

1. **Letterhead** — both solicitors' firms (or one firm with both signatures).
2. **Date · Addressee** — SJE's firm.
3. **Re** — court · case number · parties.
4. **Engagement basis** — *"Pursuant to the Order of the [Court] dated [date], we jointly instruct you as Single Joint Expert under FCR Rule 23.16."*
5. **Expert's overriding duty** — verbatim recital of Expert Witness Code of Conduct.
6. **Background facts** — agreed factual narrative.
7. **Specific questions for the expert** — verbatim from court order.
8. **Materials enclosed** — agreed bundle:
   - Pleadings.
   - Documents jointly identified as relevant.
   - Both parties' factual contentions on disputed points (clearly marked as such).
9. **Format required** — written report compliant with GPN-EXPT.
10. **Communication protocol** — SJE communicates with both solicitors simultaneously; no ex parte communications.
11. **Fees** — equal cost-sharing; specific arrangement; quarterly or milestone billing.
12. **Timeline** — agreed deadlines for report · clarifications · cross-examination availability.
13. **Signatures** — both solicitors.

**(D) SJE REPORT**

- Same format as party-appointed expert report (see file 131).
- Statement of independence — *"I have not received instructions from either party privately. All communications have been with both solicitors simultaneously."*
- Code of Conduct acknowledgment + statement of truth.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Joint instruction mandatory** — both solicitors sign + both copied on all communications.
- **No ex parte communications** with SJE — any party communicating separately must immediately notify the other.
- **Cost-sharing** — equal in first instance; subject to final costs orders.
- **Cross-examination** — both parties have right to cross-examine; SJE typically called as the Court's witness.
- **Single-expert evidence** — SJE's report is the only expert evidence on the appointed issues unless leave.
- **Departure / further evidence** — exceptional; requires leave + compelling reason.
- **Independence safeguard** — SJE may decline to act if either party attempts inappropriate influence.
- **Communication record** — all communications documented in writing.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The Court may direct that expert evidence in proceedings relating to a particular issue be given by a single expert engaged by the parties jointly, where the Court considers it appropriate having regard to the nature of the issue and the costs of expert evidence." (FCR 2011 Rule 23.16, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
SJE appointment is the cost-effective expert-evidence model for narrow technical issues — most common in valuation, quantum in personal injury, building defect causation, family law property division. Solo practitioners should propose SJE appointment in CMC where the issue is technically narrow + the alternative (two experts + joint conference) would be disproportionate. The joint instruction letter requires careful preparation to ensure both parties' factual positions are fairly presented.
