## Last Verified
2026-05-19

## Authority Tier
Statutory Form (Prescribed by Regulation)

## Currency Warning
The "Statutory Minimum" for a demand is currently **$4,000** (as of July 2021). Using the old $2,000 threshold will result in an invalid demand that can be set aside with costs.

## Template Type
Statutory Notice / Creditor's Demand

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Header:** "Corporations Act 2001 - Creditor's Statutory Demand for Payment of Debt".
2. **To:** [Company Name and Registered Office Address].
3. **The Demand:** "The [Creditor Name] (the creditor) of [Address] requires you to pay the sum of $X..."
4. **Description of Debt:** Reference to Judgment numbers or specific invoices (must be "due and payable").
5. **Warning:** Information about the 21-day period to pay or apply to set aside the demand.
6. **The "Presumption of Insolvency":** Statement that failure to comply allows the creditor to apply to wind up the company.
7. **Signature:** Of the Creditor or their Solicitor.
8. **Accompanying Affidavit (Form 7):** Required if the debt is NOT a judgment debt.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Strict Compliance:** Form 509H is a "prescribed form." Any material deviation (e.g., failing to include the notes to the company) can make the demand defective.
- **Service (s 109X):** Must be served on the registered office. Proof of posting (Registered Post) is essential.
- **Genuine Dispute:** Do not issue a demand if you know there is a genuine dispute over the debt; the company will likely get it set aside and you will be ordered to pay their legal costs.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "The company may, within 21 days after this demand is served on it, apply to the Court for an order setting aside the demand." (Mandatory statutory notice text).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The "nuclear option" for debt recovery against companies. Solo practitioners use this to bypass the long court process, as the threat of "winding up" often forces a company to pay immediately.
