## Authority Tier
Conduct Rules / Common Practice (Subject to LPUL)

## Currency Warning
Be mindful of "Reasonable Prospects of Success" requirements under s 62 of the LPUL Application Act. Threatening litigation without a genuine belief in the claim's merits can lead to professional discipline.

## Template Type
Letter Before Action / Letter of Demand

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Header:** "URGENT - LETTER BEFORE ACTION" or "NOTICE OF INTENTION TO COMMENCE PROCEEDINGS".
2. **Identification of Claim:** Reference to the contract, invoice, or incident giving rise to the claim.
3. **The Demand:**
   - Specific amount of money or specific action required.
   - Calculation of interest (if applicable).
4. **Deadline:** Clear timeframe for compliance (usually 14 days, or 7 days for urgent matters).
5. **Supporting Documents:** Reference to attached invoices or evidence.
6. **Warning of Legal Action:** "If the amount is not paid by [Time/Date], our instructions are to commence legal proceedings in the [Court Name] without further notice."
7. **Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR):** An invitation to discuss a settlement or mediate to avoid costs.
8. **Costs Warning:** Notification that the client will seek to recover court costs and statutory interest if proceedings are filed.

## Drafting Conventions
- **No Intimidation:** Avoid aggressive or bullying language (Rule 34 ASCR).
- **No Threats of Criminal Action:** Never threaten to report the matter to police to gain a civil advantage.
- **Costs Limitation:** Do not demand the debtor pay the *solicitor's costs* for writing the letter unless a contract specifically allows for it.
- **AGLC4:** While a letter, citations to statutes (e.g., Australian Consumer Law) should follow AGLC4.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "If we do not receive payment of the Debt within 14 days of the date of this letter, our client reserves the right to commence legal proceedings against you to recover the Debt, plus interest and legal costs, without further notice to you." (Standard demand wording).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The most frequent task for many solo practitioners. A well-drafted LBA often resolves disputes without the need for filing, saving the client significant time and money.
