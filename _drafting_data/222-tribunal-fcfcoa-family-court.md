## Currency Warning
FCFCOA was created 1 September 2021 by merger of Family Court of Australia and Federal Circuit Court (family-law jurisdiction). Two divisions: **Division 1** (former Family Court — complex cases; senior judges) + **Division 2** (former FCC — first-instance work; bulk of matters). Family Law Act 1975 + FCFCOA Family Law Rules 2021 govern procedure. Recent amendments (2024): Family Law Amendment Act 2023 — best-interests test simplified · ICL appointments more limited · property division framework refined.

## Structural Skeleton

**(A) DIVORCE APPLICATION**

1. **Form** — Application for Divorce (Form 1 FCFCOA).
2. **Filing** — online via Commonwealth Courts Portal; filing fee + service requirements.
3. **Sole or joint** — one or both spouses.
4. **Particulars** — marriage details · separation date · 12-month-separation requirement · arrangements for children of marriage.
5. **Service** — on respondent spouse (if sole application); both sign affidavit (if joint).
6. **Hearing** — typically at the Court · solely if no contest.

**(B) INITIATING APPLICATION (PROPERTY OR PARENTING)**

1. **Form** — Initiating Application (Form 4 FCFCOA).
2. **Reliefs sought**:
   - **Property division** — Family Law Act Part VIII (married) or Part VIIIAB (de facto).
   - **Parenting orders** — Part VII Family Law Act.
   - **Spousal maintenance** — Part VIII.
   - **Injunctions** — Part VIII Division 5.
3. **Supporting affidavit + financial statement** (Form 13) — for property.
4. **Notice of Risk** (Form 19) — for parenting matters where allegations of family violence / child abuse.
5. **Genuine Steps statement** — pre-action mediation attempted (FLA s 60I for parenting matters except in family-violence cases).

**(C) PARENTING ORDERS APPLICATION**

1. **Best-interests considerations** (FLA s 60CC — recently simplified 2024):
   - **Primary considerations**:
     - Safety of the child.
     - Benefit of meaningful relationship with both parents (subject to safety).
   - **Other considerations**:
     - Children's views.
     - Children's relationships with parents + others.
     - Effect of changes on the children.
     - Practical difficulty.
     - Capacity of each parent.
     - Family violence history.
     - Other relevant matters.
2. **Family violence** screening — mandatory (Notice of Risk Form 19).
3. **Independent Children's Lawyer (ICL)** — appointed in matters involving allegations of significant risk; appointments more limited post-2024 reforms.

**(D) PROPERTY DIVISION APPLICATION**

1. **Four-step approach** (*Stanford v Stanford* (2012) 247 CLR 108; *Hickey v Hickey* [2003] FamCA 395):
   - Identify + value asset pool.
   - Assess contributions (financial · non-financial · homemaker · parent).
   - Assess future needs (s 75(2) factors).
   - Determine just and equitable order.
2. **De facto relationships** — Part VIIIAB; 2-year cohabitation OR child of relationship OR substantial contribution OR registration of relationship.
3. **Financial statement** (Form 13) — full disclosure mandatory; non-disclosure attracts adverse inferences + costs orders.

**(E) FAMILY VIOLENCE AND PROTECTION ORDERS**

- Family Violence Orders (state-issued) — distinct from FCFCOA jurisdiction; state Magistrates' Courts.
- FCFCOA injunctions under FLA Part VIII Division 5 — overlap with state FVO.

**(F) APPEALS**

- From Division 2 → Division 1 (typically) under FCFCOA Act.
- Further appeal to Full Court of FCFCOA (Division 1) → HCA special leave.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Genuine Steps statement** mandatory for parenting matters (FLA s 60I); exceptions for family violence + risk cases.
- **Pre-action mediation** through Family Dispute Resolution Practitioners (FDRPs) — accredited mediators.
- **Section 60I certificate** (FDRP) required for most parenting applications.
- **Financial disclosure** — full + frank in property; non-disclosure leads to inferences against non-discloser.
- **Privacy** — FCFCOA proceedings are private (s 121 FLA — publication restrictions).
- **Children's voice** — Children's Best Interests Reports + Family Reports by court-appointed practitioners.
- **Costs** — typically each party bears own costs in family law (FLA s 117); exceptions for vexatious conduct or non-disclosure.
- **Section 90B Binding Financial Agreements** — pre-relationship + during-relationship + after-separation agreements; strict execution requirements.
- **Hague Convention** on international child abduction — separate jurisdiction; FCFCOA handles.
- **AGLC4 citation** — *Stanford v Stanford* (2012) 247 CLR 108 · *Hickey v Hickey* [2003] FamCA 395.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The court may make such orders as it considers appropriate altering the interests of the parties in the property, including any of the following kinds: (a) an order for a settlement of property in substitution for any interest in the property; (b) an order requiring either or both of the parties to do all acts and things necessary to enable the order to be carried out." (Family Law Act 1975, s 79, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
FCFCOA practice is a major area for solo family-law practitioners. Solo practitioners cover divorce · property division · parenting · child support · spousal maintenance · de facto matters · binding financial agreements · ICL work. The Section 60I pre-action mediation requirement is the gateway for most parenting matters. The four-step property-division approach (*Stanford*) is foundational. Recent 2023-2024 amendments to the best-interests test simplified the framework — verify current FLA s 60CC text. Privacy restrictions under s 121 FLA shape all communications about FCFCOA matters.
