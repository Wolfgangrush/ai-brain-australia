## Currency Warning
Australian appellate practice is written-submission dominant. Oral hearings typically 1-2 hours per side at Full Court; less at single-judge appeals. Written submissions decide most appeals — the bench reads them BEFORE the hearing and writes the judgment substantially from them.

## Template Type
Submission

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Cover sheet** — appeal number · parties · counsel · solicitor · date · word count · page count.
2. **Index of contents**.
3. **Introduction** (1 paragraph) — appeal context + party position in one sentence.
4. **Issues for determination** — bulleted (3-7).
5. **Statement of facts** — agreed where possible; differences flagged with pinpoint citations to Appeal Book (AB).
6. **Procedural posture** — pleadings · interlocutory orders · trial dates · witnesses called · judgment date.
7. **Decision below** — operative findings + reasoning (with pinpoint to reasons paragraph numbers).
8. **Appellant's case** — concise statement of correct conclusion.
9. **Grounds of appeal** — restated.
10. **For each ground**:
    - Legal principle.
    - Authority (HCA primary; FCAFC / State CoA secondary; comparative tertiary).
    - Application — court below's reasoning quoted + analysed with pinpoints.
    - Conclusion on the ground.
11. **Disposition sought** — set aside · vary · remit · re-trial · costs.
12. **Costs of appeal** — basis (party-party · indemnity) + scale.
13. **List of authorities** — separate, indexed.
14. **Reading list** for the bench — recommended pre-hearing reading.
15. **Conclusion** (1-2 sentences).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Page limits** — FCAFC typically 30 pages for Appellant's Submissions (15 pages for Reply); NSWCA 35 pages; verify current PD.
- **Word count** — page limit AND word count usually both stated (e.g., 30 pages AND 12,000 words for Full Court).
- **Pinpoint citations** to:
  - Appeal Book (AB pp 234-237).
  - Reasons below ([trial reasons] [45]).
  - Authorities (*Robinson Helicopter Co Inc v McDermott* (2016) 261 CLR 297 at [43]).
- **AGLC4 citation** mandatory — neutral citation first, then authorised report citation: *Robinson Helicopter Co Inc v McDermott* [2016] HCA 22; (2016) 261 CLR 297.
- **Reading list** — short (6-10 items); judges' pre-hearing time limited.
- **Authorities bundle** — separate document; chronological within topic.
- **No new evidence** — only on leave (CDJ v VAJ); reference in submissions sparingly.
- **Conciseness** — appellate benches favour focused arguments; padding penalised.
- **Reply submissions** — limited to new matters raised in Respondent's Submissions.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Written submissions shall identify the issues for determination, summarise the evidence, set out the legal arguments, and identify the authorities relied upon. They shall be concise and focused on the operative issues. Pinpoint citations to the Appeal Book and to authorities are mandatory." (Federal Court Practice Note GPN-APP, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Written submissions are THE document of the appeal — the bench reads them BEFORE the hearing and writes the judgment substantially from them. Solo practitioners often brief specialist appellate counsel for written advocacy; the solo runs case management, Appeal Book assembly, and the reading list. Tight focus on 2-4 strong grounds + meticulous pinpoint citations separates winning appellate advocacy from comprehensive-but-diffuse advocacy.
