## Currency Warning
Courts are increasingly demanding "particulars of AI use" in evidence preparation. While not a formal rule yet, practitioners should be ready to particularize how evidence (like data analysis) was generated.

## Template Type
Pleading / Procedural Document

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Court Header:** Case details.
2. **Title:** "Request for Particulars" or "Further and Better Particulars".
3. **The Request:**
   - "Under paragraph 5 of the Statement of Claim, the Plaintiff alleges [X]. Please provide particulars of [X]."
   - Identification of specific dates, times, persons present, and content of conversations.
4. **Types of Particulars:**
   - **Fraud/Misrepresentation:** High level of specificity required.
   - **Negligence:** Particulars of the specific acts or omissions.
   - **Damage:** Itemized list of losses.
5. **The Response:** "The [Party] provides the following particulars..."
6. **Execution:** Signature of Solicitor.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Purpose:** Particulars are intended to define the issues, prevent surprise, and enable the other party to know what case they have to meet.
- **Distinction from Pleadings:** Particulars supplement material facts; they are not themselves material facts.
- **Format:** Often provided as a separate document or "letter of particulars" if they are too voluminous to fit in the pleading.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "PARTICULARS OF MISREPRESENTATION: (a) The representation was made orally by Mr. Smith to Ms. Jones on 12 June 2023 at the Plaintiff's office. (b) The substance of the representation was that the machine was 'new', when in fact it was a 2015 model."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The primary tool for "interrogating" the other side's case before discovery. A solo practitioner uses requests for particulars to expose weaknesses in the opponent's story and narrow the evidence required for trial.
