## Currency Warning
The **Administrative Review Tribunal (ART)** replaced the Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) on **14 October 2024**. Any old AAT forms or references are now stale. Ensure citations refer to the *Administrative Review Tribunal Act 2024*.

## Template Type
Tribunal Application / Administrative Review

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Applicant Details:** Name, Contact, and whether a Representative is used.
2. **Decision Details:**
   - Name of the Department or Agency.
   - Date of the original decision.
   - Date of the Internal Review decision (if applicable).
3. **The Reviewable Decision:** Brief description (e.g., "Refusal of NDIS funding", "Tax Assessment").
4. **Reasons for Application:** Concise statement of why the decision is wrong.
5. **Orders Sought:** What you want the Tribunal to do (e.g., "Set aside the decision and substitute a new decision...").
6. **Request for Stay:** (Optional) If the decision should be paused pending the review.
7. **Filing Details:** Signature and Date.

## Drafting Conventions
- **De Novo Review:** The ART "stands in the shoes" of the original decision-maker. The focus is on finding the "correct or preferable" decision.
- **Internal Review Requirement:** Check if the specific statute (e.g., Social Security Act) requires an internal review before applying to the ART.
- **Time Limits:** Usually 28 days from receipt of the decision.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "I apply for a review of the decision described above. The reasons I want the decision reviewed are: [Statement of Reasons]."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Used for clients challenging federal government decisions (Centrelink, NDIS, Veterans' Affairs, Taxation). The ART is designed to be more informal than court, making it accessible for solo practitioners.
