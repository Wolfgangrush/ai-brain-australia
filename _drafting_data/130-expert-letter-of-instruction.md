## Currency Warning
Australian expert evidence framework: party-appointed experts default; single joint expert available on direction. Expert's overriding duty to the Court (Expert Witness Code of Conduct, Schedule 1 to GPN-EXPT). Reports must comply with prescribed format including statement of expertise, materials reviewed, opinions, reasons, and acknowledgment of overriding duty.

## Template Type
Letter (Instructing Solicitor → Expert)

## Structural Skeleton

1. **Letterhead** — instructing solicitor's firm.
2. **Date · Reference · Addressee** — expert's firm + name + qualifications.
3. **Re** — court · case number · year · parties · concise case description.
4. **Confidentiality recital** — *"This letter and its enclosures are confidential. The contents are subject to litigation privilege and must not be disclosed to any third party without our prior written consent."*
5. **Engagement basis** — *"We instruct you on behalf of the [Applicant/Respondent] in the above matter to provide expert evidence on [specific issue]."*
6. **Expert's overriding duty** — *"Your overriding duty under the Federal Court Expert Witness Code of Conduct (Schedule 1 to GPN-EXPT) is to the Court. This duty overrides any obligation to the party instructing you or paying you. Please confirm your understanding and acceptance of this duty in writing."*
7. **Background facts** — concise factual matrix (page-numbered for reference).
8. **Specific questions for the expert's opinion** — numbered list; each question precise · self-contained · within the expert's specialism.
9. **Materials enclosed** — schedule:
   - Pleadings (Originating Application · Statement of Claim · Defence · Reply).
   - Affidavits filed below (if any).
   - Documents discovered.
   - Prior expert reports.
   - Industry standards / codes / regulations.
   - Statute extracts relevant to the expert's domain.
10. **Format required** — written report compliant with GPN-EXPT:
    - Expert's qualifications + experience.
    - Statement of expertise.
    - Statement of materials reviewed.
    - Statement of facts assumed.
    - Opinion(s) + reasons (with literature references where appropriate).
    - Statement of any matters of which the expert is unsure / uncertain.
    - Acknowledgment of the Expert Witness Code of Conduct.
    - Statement of truth + signature.
11. **Conflict of interest** — confirm no conflict (financial · personal · professional).
12. **Fees and disbursements** — hourly rate · estimated total · billing cadence · expenses · capping arrangement (if any).
13. **Timeline** — report due by `<date>` · conference for clarification at `<date>` · joint conference of experts (if directed) · trial dates.
14. **Closing** — *"Please confirm acceptance of these instructions in writing."*
15. **Signature** — instructing solicitor.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Code of Conduct acknowledgment mandatory** — failure renders expert evidence inadmissible.
- **Letter is disclosable** under FCR Part 20 — draft accordingly; avoid client-advocacy framing.
- **Factual instructions distinct from opinion request** — the expert should opine, not advocate.
- **No leading questions** — expert's report compromised if questions are tendentious.
- **Concurrent evidence** (hot-tubbing) — increasingly used at Federal Court; expert must be willing to be cross-examined alongside opposing expert.
- **Joint conference of experts** — directed under FCR Rule 23.13; experts confer + produce joint statement narrowing issues; without prejudice.
- **Single Joint Expert (SJE)** — court may direct under FCR Rule 23.15; both parties brief jointly.
- **Common technical fields**: quantum / forensic accountancy · construction · medical · IT/cybersecurity · valuation · industry-specific (mining · pharma · aviation · agriculture).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"An expert witness has an overriding duty to assist the Court impartially on matters relevant to the expert's area of expertise. An expert witness's paramount duty is to the Court and not to the person retaining the expert." (Federal Court Expert Witness Code of Conduct, Schedule 1 to GPN-EXPT, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The letter of instruction is the most important pre-trial document for any case requiring expert evidence — its drafting QUALITY determines the EXPERT's QUALITY. Solo practitioners often under-invest in this document; the result is a meandering expert report that fails to address the actual contested issues. Pair with a draft list of questions developed with counsel and amend after expert's preliminary view.
