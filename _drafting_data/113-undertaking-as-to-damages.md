## Currency Warning
The undertaking as to damages is the consideration the applicant gives for an interlocutory injunction / freezing order / search order. If the order is ultimately found to have been wrongly granted, the applicant must compensate the respondent for loss suffered. Fortification (security for the undertaking) may be required where the applicant's capacity to pay is doubted.

## Template Type
Procedural Document / Undertaking

## Structural Skeleton

**Standard form of undertaking** (incorporated in Federal Court GPN-FRZG / GPN-SRCH and state equivalents):

```
The Applicant gives the following undertakings to the Court:

1. The Applicant will pay to the Respondent (or any third party affected
   by the order) any compensation that the Court may consider just for
   any loss the Respondent (or any third party) sustains as a result of
   this Order, where the Court ultimately finds the Order should not
   have been made.

2. The Applicant will pay the reasonable costs of any third party (other
   than the Respondent) to whom notice of this Order is given, in
   complying with this Order.

3. The Applicant will procure compliance with this undertaking by any
   party for whose benefit this Order is made.

4. The Applicant submits to such orders for the payment of compensation
   or costs as the Court may consider just.

Dated this <date> day of <month>, <year>.

________________________
[Solicitor for the Applicant on behalf of the Applicant]
```

**Fortification — additional documents where required:**

1. **Cash payment into Court** — supported by Solicitor's Trust Account undertaking.
2. **Bank guarantee** in prescribed form.
3. **Insurance certificate** — evidence of solvency.
4. **Personal undertaking** of beneficial owner (where applicant is corporate).

**Disclosure of capacity** (in supporting affidavit):

1. Applicant's assets (audited accounts · property · investments).
2. Indemnity from parent company / related entity (if any).
3. Litigation funder's undertaking (where litigation funder backs the proceedings).
4. After-the-event (ATE) insurance certificate (where applicable).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Mandatory for every interlocutory injunction / freezing / search order** — without it, no order.
- **Form prescribed in PNs** — Federal Court GPN-FRZG and GPN-SRCH provide the wording; UCPRs in each state provide state-equivalent.
- **Fortification considerations** — court may require security if applicant is:
  - Foreign-resident with limited Australian assets.
  - Corporate with thin capitalisation.
  - Litigation-funder-backed (LF typically provides its own undertaking).
- **Quantum exposure** — undertaking is at large; respondent must prove specific loss caused by the order.
- **Discharge of injunction** — triggers inquiry into damages on respondent's application.
- **Inquiry as to damages** — separate proceeding to assess respondent's loss; can be substantial.
- **Litigation funder's role** — increasingly common; LF typically gives its own undertaking + indemnifies applicant.
- **Third-party loss** — third parties affected by Mareva / search orders (e.g., banks) may also be compensated.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"An applicant who has obtained an interlocutory injunction is generally required to give an undertaking to compensate the respondent for any loss caused by the injunction where it is ultimately determined that the injunction should not have been granted. This undertaking is the price the applicant pays for the interlocutory relief." (Synthesised from *F Hoffmann-La Roche* [1975] AC 295 + Australian application.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The undertaking is the principal litigation-risk exposure for applicants seeking interlocutory relief. Solo practitioners must brief clients carefully — losing the main proceeding triggers an inquiry into damages with potentially significant exposure. Fortification considerations should be addressed PRE-application, not at the hearing. Where applicant's capacity is thin, consider:
- ATE insurance.
- Litigation funder backstop.
- Personal guarantee from beneficial owner.
- Bank guarantee for security.
