## Currency Warning
Australian discovery framework: parties produce documents under their possession / custody / power that are directly relevant to issues raised in the pleadings (FCR Rule 20.14). The Federal Court emphasises proportionality + cost-benefit (Practice Note GPN-DISC). Generic discovery routinely refused; categories or document-by-document discovery preferred.

## Template Type
Procedural Document

## Structural Skeleton

**LIST OF DOCUMENTS (FCR Form 39 / state equivalent)**

1. **Heading** — court · case number · parties.
2. **Title** — "List of Documents of the [Applicant / Respondent]".
3. **Schedule 1 — Documents in the possession of the [party]**:
   - Documents the party intends to rely upon at trial.
   - Documents directly relevant to issues in pleadings.
   - Each document: date · author · recipient · description.
4. **Schedule 2 — Documents previously in the possession of the [party]** (no longer in possession):
   - Documents disposed of / lost / destroyed.
   - When + how + current location if known.
5. **Schedule 3 — Documents claimed to be privileged**:
   - Privilege log (see file 121).
6. **Declaration** signed by the party (not solicitor) — completeness statement.
7. **Date + signature**.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Categories or document-by-document** — generic ("all documents relating to X") routinely refused.
- **Direct relevance** — narrower test than "indirect relevance" (no train-of-inquiry doctrine post-FCR 2011).
- **Continuing obligation** — duty extends throughout proceedings.
- **Privilege carve-out** — separate Schedule 3 (privilege log).
- **Electronically stored information** — emails · chat logs · cloud documents · social media; keyword + date-range parameters.
- **Native format** — emails in native format with metadata; documents in original electronic format where possible.
- **Pre-action discovery** — FCR Part 7 + state UCPR equivalents — for identifying respondent.
- **Non-party discovery** — FCR Part 20 Division 20.3 — limited scope.
- **Sanctions for non-compliance** — adverse inferences · strike-out · costs · contempt.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"A party may seek discovery of documents that are directly relevant to any issue raised on the pleadings." (Federal Court Rules 2011, Rule 20.14, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Discovery is the gateway document of any contested civil action. Solo practitioners must maintain meticulous client-document inventories from day one. The categories vs document-by-document choice is the principal litigation-strategy battleground. Proportionality is the modern emphasis; over-broad discovery requests attract costs orders.
