## Currency Warning
The *Corporations Amendment (Meetings and Documents) Act 2022* made permanent changes to electronic signatures and virtual meetings. Ensure templates reflect these modernized execution and meeting rules.

## Template Type
Contract / Governance Document

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties:** All shareholders and the Company itself.
2. **Relationship to Constitution:** Clause stating the agreement prevails over the Constitution in case of inconsistency.
3. **Board Composition and Governance:**
   - Right to appoint/remove directors.
   - Quorum and voting for board meetings.
4. **Decision Making (Reserved Matters):** List of "Special Majority" or "Unanimous" decisions (e.g., changing the business, taking on debt).
5. **Issue and Transfer of Shares:**
   - Pre-emptive rights on new issues.
   - Rights of First Refusal (ROFR) on transfers.
6. **Exit Strategies:**
   - Tag-along rights (minority protection).
   - Drag-along rights (majority power to sell).
7. **Default and Compulsory Transfer:** What happens on insolvency, death, or "Bad Leaver" events.
8. **Restrictive Covenants:** Non-compete and non-solicitation (subject to Australian "reasonableness" tests).
9. **Dividends and Funding:** Policy on profit distribution and future capital calls.
10. **Dispute Resolution:** "Shotgun" clauses, mediation, or expert determination.
11. **Execution:** Specific section for execution by companies under s 127 of the *Corporations Act*.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Section 127 Execution:** Must strictly follow s 127(1) (two directors or a director and secretary) to trigger the "assumption of regularity" for third parties.
- **Replaceable Rules:** Explicitly state which Replaceable Rules are excluded or modified.
- **Confidentiality:** Essential in a private agreement as constitutions of public companies are public documents.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "In the event of any inconsistency between the provisions of this Agreement and the Constitution, the provisions of this Agreement will prevail to the extent of the inconsistency, and the Shareholders must take all necessary steps to amend the Constitution to remove the inconsistency."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Used when setting up a new company for founders or when a new investor joins. It is the "internal treaty" that prevents deadlock and manages the departure of founders—the most common point of failure for small business clients.
