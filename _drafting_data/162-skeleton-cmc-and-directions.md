## Currency Warning
Australian courts emphasise active case management — Federal Court National Court Framework (NCF) + Practice Note CM 1. Case Management Conferences (CMCs) + directions hearings shape trial preparation. Submissions for CMCs/directions tightly focused on procedural orders sought.

## Template Type
Submission / Memorandum

## Structural Skeleton

**CMC Memorandum** (for Federal Court — typically filed before each CMC)

1. **Cover sheet** — court · case number · parties · CMC date · counsel · solicitor.
2. **Heading** — "Case Management Conference Memorandum".
3. **Status of proceedings**:
   - Pleadings filed (date · status).
   - Discovery progress.
   - AEICs status.
   - Expert evidence progress.
   - Mediation status.
4. **Issues for the CMC**:
   - Procedural directions sought.
   - Disagreements with other parties on procedural matters.
5. **Proposed timetable** — milestones with dates.
6. **Estimated trial duration**.
7. **ADR / mediation proposal** (if any).
8. **Issues requiring early determination** (preliminary questions · jurisdictional issues).
9. **Estimated costs of further interlocutory steps**.
10. **Form of directions** sought (annexure with draft order).

**Directions Hearing Skeleton**

1. **Cover sheet · title · introduction**.
2. **Procedural posture** — current stage.
3. **Outstanding issues**:
   - Each issue requiring direction.
   - Each party's position.
4. **Specific directions sought** — listed.
5. **Reasons for directions** — efficiency · proportionality · case-management principles.
6. **Anticipated objections + response**.
7. **Form of order**.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Cooperative framing** — Australian courts favour parties who engage constructively with case management.
- **Specific orders sought** — annex draft order in form sought.
- **Timetable realistic** — over-optimistic timetables that slip attract critical comments.
- **Mediation consideration** — Court will ask whether mediation has been attempted; document position.
- **Costs implications** of procedural steps — flag for budget purposes.
- **NCF (National Court Framework)** — Federal Court allocates cases to specialist National Practice Areas; CMC documents acknowledge NPA assignment.
- **Genuine Steps Statement** — Civil Dispute Resolution Act 2011 (Cth) requires statement of genuine steps taken before proceedings; relevant at CMC.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The Court will conduct case management actively. Parties are expected to cooperate in identifying issues, narrowing disputes, and proposing realistic timetables. The overarching purpose of the Federal Court Rules is to facilitate the just resolution of disputes according to law and as quickly, inexpensively and efficiently as possible." (Federal Court of Australia Act 1976, s 37M + Practice Note CM 1, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
CMCs and directions hearings are routine throughout Australian civil litigation. Solo practitioners should prepare a one-page memorandum + draft directions before each. The cooperative framing favours the better-prepared party. Aggressive positions at CMCs (refusing to mediate · over-broad discovery requests · unrealistic timetables) attract costs orders.
