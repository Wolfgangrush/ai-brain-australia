## Currency Warning
List of Authorities (LOA) is the cross-reference document for all case-law + statute + texts cited in written submissions. Filed alongside skeletal submissions + Court Book. Authorities Bundle (paper or electronic) prepared in parallel.

## Template Type
Procedural Document / Bundle Index

## Structural Skeleton

1. **Cover sheet** — court · case number · parties · counsel · date.
2. **Title** — "List of Authorities of the [Applicant / Respondent]".
3. **Index — columnar table**:

| Tab | Case / Statute / Article | AGLC4 Citation | Source | Pagination |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | *Robinson Helicopter Co Inc v McDermott* | (2016) 261 CLR 297; [2016] HCA 22 | Authorised Reports | AB Tab 1, pp 1-25 |
| 2 | *Spencer v Commonwealth* | (2010) 241 CLR 118; [2010] HCA 28 | Authorised Reports | AB Tab 2, pp 26-48 |
| 3 | *Project Blue Sky Inc v ABA* | (1998) 194 CLR 355; [1998] HCA 28 | Authorised Reports | AB Tab 3, pp 49-95 |
| 4 | Federal Court of Australia Act 1976 (Cth) s 23 | — | Legislation | AB Tab 4, pp 96-100 |
| 5 | Federal Court Rules 2011 Part 23 | — | Legislation | AB Tab 5, pp 101-115 |
| 6 | Halsbury's Laws of Australia, "Damages" [255-1000] | — | Texts (cited only) | — |

4. **Order**:
   - **Australian primary law first**:
     - HCA cases (reverse chronological or topical).
     - FCAFC / State CoA cases.
     - Single-judge decisions where relied upon (limited).
     - Tribunal decisions (rare).
   - **Statutes + regulations** cited.
   - **Comparative common-law** (UK Supreme Court · Privy Council · NZSC · Canadian SC) — where persuasive.
   - **International instruments + treaties** (where applicable).
   - **Texts** (Halsbury's · Mason and Carter's Restitution · Heydon's Cross on Evidence · Adams's Australian Trade Marks · etc.) — sparingly.

5. **AGLC4 citation throughout** — neutral citation first, authorised reporter citation second:
   - HCA: *[YYYY] HCA NN*; (YYYY) NNN CLR NN
   - FCAFC: *[YYYY] FCAFC NN*; (YYYY) NNN FCR NN
   - FCA: *[YYYY] FCA NN*; (YYYY) NNN FCR NN
   - State CoA: *[YYYY] NSWCA NN* · *[YYYY] VSCA NN* · *[YYYY] QCA NN* · *[YYYY] WASCA NN*
   - State Supreme Court: *[YYYY] NSWSC NN* · etc.
6. **Operative passages flagged** — yellow highlights / margin notes; bookmarks in PDF.
7. **Updated to date of trial** — verify case is not overruled / treated negatively.

**AUTHORITIES BUNDLE (AB)** — separate document, paginated + indexed:

- Each case at separately tabbed location.
- Each statute extract at separately tabbed location.
- Cases CITED in submissions = MUST appear in AB.
- Cases referenced for context only (not directly cited) = optional.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Length cap** — typically 30 cases for 5-day trial; verify Practice Direction.
- **Quality over quantity** — courts critical of "scattergun" authorities; cite cases counsel will engage with.
- **Pinpoint citations** in submissions — *Robinson Helicopter* (2016) 261 CLR 297 at [43].
- **AGLC4 mandatory** — failure to use correct citation format invites criticism.
- **Treatment check** — confirm each case is still good law; subsequent treatment may have qualified / overruled.
- **Texts** — sparingly; only where text is authoritative on the specific question (Halsbury's · subject-specific texts like Vines on the Law of Property).
- **Comparative** — only where Australian law silent or persuasive analogy; cited sparingly.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"A List of Authorities shall identify each case, statute, and text cited in the written submissions. The List shall use the citation format prescribed by the Australian Guide to Legal Citation (AGLC4). An Authorities Bundle containing each authority cited shall be filed with the Court." (Federal Court Practice Note GPN-WRTS + AGLC4, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The List of Authorities + Authorities Bundle are tedious to prepare but essential. Solo practitioners often delegate to junior staff / paralegals; this is appropriate provided the senior practitioner reviews for quality + subsequent treatment + AGLC4 compliance. Common errors: (a) cases not used in submissions are nonetheless included (padding); (b) cases used in submissions are NOT in the LOA (gap); (c) outdated authorities cited without verification of subsequent treatment.
