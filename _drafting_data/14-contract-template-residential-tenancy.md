2026-05-19

## Authority Tier
Statutory Form (Residential Tenancies Regulation 2019 NSW)

## Currency Warning
The standard form was updated in **September 2020**. Practitioners must ensure they are using the version that includes the "smoke alarm" and "water efficiency" disclosures. Note that as of **October 2024**, rent increases are limited to once every 12 months.

## Template Type
Contract / Statutory Form

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Landlord and Tenant Details:** Names, contact details, and agent details (if any).
2. **Premises Details:** Address and inclusions (e.g., parking, storage).
3. **Term of Agreement:** Fixed term (start/end date) or Periodic.
4. **Rent:** Amount, frequency, and method of payment.
5. **Bond:** Amount (max 4 weeks' rent) and lodgement details.
6. **Mandatory Disclosures:**
   - Smoke alarms.
   - Material facts (e.g., flood/fire history, drug crimes).
   - Water efficiency.
7. **Standard Terms (Schedule 1):**
   - Possession and rent.
   - Use of premises (peace, comfort, and privacy).
   - Maintenance and repairs (Urgent vs. Non-urgent).
   - Alterations and additions.
   - Entry by the landlord.
8. **Special Terms:** (Must not conflict with the Act).
9. **Signatures:** Landlord/Agent and Tenant.
10. **Condition Report:** Reference to the mandatory move-in report.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Prohibited Terms:** Do not include terms requiring the tenant to have the carpets professionally cleaned (unless pets are kept) or requiring the tenant to take out insurance.
- **Fair Dealing:** The "Tenant Information Statement" must be provided *before* or *at the time* of signing.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "The landlord shall provide the residential premises in a reasonable state of cleanliness and fit for habitation by the tenant... The tenant shall not use the residential premises, or cause or permit the premises to be used, for any illegal purpose." (Standard statutory terms).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Used when advising small-scale landlords or tenants in disputes. Ensuring the correct "standard form" is used is critical, as non-standard terms are often void and can lead to penalties at NCAT.
