## Authority Tier
Court Rule Form (UCPR NSW)

## Currency Warning
Ensure the witness certificate at the end of the affidavit complies with the latest **Oaths Act 1900 (NSW)** requirements, including the "identity check" and "face-to-face" certifications.

## Template Type
Affidavit

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Court Header:** Case details.
2. **Deponent Details:** Name, Address, Occupation (often a Process Server).
3. **Body Paragraphs:**
   - Para 1: Statement that the deponent is over 16.
   - Para 2: Date, Time, and Place of service.
   - Para 3: Description of the documents served (e.g., "Statement of Claim filed 10 May 2024").
   - Para 4: Method of service (e.g., "by handing them to the Defendant personally").
   - Para 5: Identity check (e.g., "I asked the person 'Are you Mr. Jones?' and he replied 'Yes'").
4. **Execution Block:**
   - Sworn/Affirmed.
   - Signature of Deponent and Witness.
5. **Witness Certificate:** (Oaths Act compliance).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Do Not Annex:** Do not attach the documents that have already been filed in court; just describe them accurately.
- **Direct Speech:** Use the exact words spoken by the person served to prove they were identified.
- **Method of Service:** Must comply with the specific rules for the document (e.g., Personal Service for an Originating Process).

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "On 15 May 2024 at 10:00am at 123 Fake Street, Sydney, I served the Defendant with the Statement of Claim. I said to the person 'Are you [Name]?' and they said 'Yes'. I then handed the documents to them."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The "proof" required before you can move for a default judgment. If the affidavit of service is defective, the registry will reject your application to proceed in the defendant's absence.
