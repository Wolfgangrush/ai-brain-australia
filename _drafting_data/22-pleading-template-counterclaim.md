## Currency Warning
In many Australian jurisdictions (like NSW), the term "Counterclaim" is replaced by "Cross-claim." Ensure the correct terminology for the registry.

## Template Type
Pleading

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Court Header:** Matches the original Statement of Claim.
2. **Title of Cross-claim:**
   - Cross-claimant (Original Defendant).
   - Cross-defendant (Original Plaintiff or Third Party).
3. **Relief Claimed:** The specific orders sought by the Cross-claimant.
4. **Pleadings (Material Facts):**
   - Reference to the original proceeding.
   - Facts giving rise to the Cross-claim (e.g., "In the event that the Defendant is liable to the Plaintiff, which is denied, the Defendant says...").
5. **Particulars:** Supporting details.
6. **Notice to Cross-defendant:** Warning about the 28-day response period.
7. **Solicitor's Certificate:** (Under LPUL).
8. **Affidavit Verifying:** (If required).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Indemnity or Contribution:** Often used where a defendant claims that if they are liable, someone else should pay them back (Contribution) or cover the full amount (Indemnity).
- **Independent Action:** A cross-claim can proceed even if the main claim is stayed or dismissed.
- **Joinder of Parties:** If the cross-claim is against a new party, that party must be joined to the proceeding.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "The Cross-claimant claims against the Cross-defendant: 1. Judgment for the sum of $X. 2. In the alternative, contribution or indemnity in respect of any amount for which the Cross-claimant is found liable to the Plaintiff."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Used to "turn the tables" on a plaintiff or to bring in a third party (like an insurer or sub-contractor) who is the "real" party responsible for the loss.
