## Last Verified
2026-05-19

## Authority Tier
Statutory Requirement (LPUL) / Law Society Precedent

## Currency Warning
Ensure the agreement reflects the **Legal Profession Uniform General Rules 2015**. Any conditional (no-win, no-fee) agreement must have a "cooling-off" period of 5 clear business days.

## Template Type
Contract / Compliance Document

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties:** Law Practice and Client.
2. **Scope of Work:** Detailed description of the instructions (e.g., "Advice and representation in the matter of X v Y").
3. **Professional Fees:**
   - Hourly rates (by seniority).
   - Fixed fee (if applicable).
   - Uplift fees (only for conditional agreements, max 25%).
4. **Disbursements:** Explanation of third-party costs (filing fees, barrister's fees, travel).
5. **Billing Arrangements:** Frequency and payment terms.
6. **Trust Money:** Authority to withdraw money from trust to pay invoices.
7. **Termination of Agreement:**
   - By the client (at any time).
   - By the law practice (for "just cause" or with 14 days' notice).
8. **Retention of Documents:** Policy on keeping files (usually 7 years).
9. **Governing Law:** Jurisdiction (e.g., NSW).
10. **Acceptance:** "You may accept this agreement by signing and returning it, or by continuing to provide us with instructions." (Note: Conditional agreements *must* be signed).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Writing Requirement:** Must be in writing (s 180 LPUL).
- **Transparency:** Avoid "hidden" costs. Every billing item must be traceable to the agreement.
- **Client Rights:** Must incorporate or be served with the **Costs Disclosure (s 174)**.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "This document is an offer to enter into a costs agreement with you. You have the right to negotiate this agreement with us. You also have the right to receive a bill and request an itemised bill."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The "protection" for the lawyer's income. A valid costs agreement is the only way a solo practitioner can ensure they are paid for their work and protected from "cost assessment" reductions based on scale.
