Court Rule Form (UCPR NSW)

## Currency Warning
The Court will generally not make consent orders that are "confidential" or "sealed" without a specific legislative basis. If settlement terms are confidential, keep them in a separate Deed and use the Consent Order only for the "dismissal" or "payment" terms.

## Template Type
Court Form / Settlement Order

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Court Header:** Case details.
2. **Title:** "Consent Judgment" or "Consent Order."
3. **The Orders:**
   - Order 1: "Judgment for the Plaintiff against the Defendant for $X."
   - Order 2: "The Defendant pay the Plaintiff's costs agreed at $Y."
   - Order 3: "The proceedings be otherwise dismissed."
4. **Signature Block:** Signatures of BOTH parties' solicitors.
5. **Party Details:** (If more than two parties).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Joint Signature:** Unlike a Notice of Motion, a Consent Order *must* be signed by all parties affected by the order.
- **Finality:** A consent judgment has the same effect as a judgment after a trial; it can be enforced via writ or garnishee order.
- **Liberty to Apply:** Often useful to include "Liberty to apply on 48 hours' notice" in case there is a dispute over the implementation of the orders.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "The Court gives judgment/makes the following orders by consent: 1. Verdict for the Plaintiff in the sum of $50,000. 2. Each party to bear their own costs."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The "clean" way to end a case after a successful mediation. It avoids the cost of a final hearing and gives the client an enforceable court order rather than just a private contract.
