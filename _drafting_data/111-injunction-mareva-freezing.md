## Currency Warning
Mareva (freezing) jurisdiction in Australia: applicant must show (a) good arguable case on the merits; (b) defendant has assets within or outside the jurisdiction; (c) real danger of dissipation. Worldwide Mareva relief available subject to *Cardile* principles. Federal Court has GPN-FRZG standard form + harmonised state procedure post-*Cardile*. Almost always commenced ex parte; full inter partes return date within 7-14 days.

## Template Type
Application + Supporting Affidavit + Draft Freezing Order (Federal Court standard form)

## Structural Skeleton

**Application** (combined Originating + Interlocutory)

1. **Heading** — Federal Court / State Supreme Court · case number · parties.
2. **Title** — "Interlocutory Application — Freezing Order (Mareva Injunction)".
3. **Reliefs sought**:
   - Order restraining the respondent from disposing of, dealing with, or diminishing the value of, his assets within Australia [/ worldwide] up to the value of A$`<amount>`.
   - Ancillary disclosure order — respondent to disclose all assets (location · value · nature) within 7 days.
   - Liberty for respondent to apply on 48 hours' notice.
   - Liberty for third parties (banks) to apply.
   - Costs of the application reserved.
4. **Undertaking as to damages** offered by applicant (see file 113).
5. **Penal notice** mandatory.

**Supporting Affidavit**

1. **Deponent** — applicant / authorised representative.
2. **Good arguable case** — detailed pleading of underlying claim + documentary evidence of liability.
3. **Respondent's assets** — bank account references · property · shares · listed companies of which respondent is a director / shareholder · cryptocurrency holdings.
4. **Real danger of dissipation** — evidence: prior unexplained asset transfers · dishonesty · fraud allegations · evidence of asset relocation · history of evasion.
5. **Worldwide Mareva grounds** (if sought) — Australian assets insufficient · respondent's evasion of jurisdictions · *Cardile* principles.
6. **Cross-undertaking as to damages** — applicant's solvency / fortification of undertaking · evidence of capacity to pay damages if Mareva ultimately unjustified.
7. **Full and frank disclosure** — paramount; disclose any weakness in the case.
8. **Annexures** — contracts · correspondence · banking documents · ASIC company searches · land title searches · evidence of dissipation.
9. **Statutory declaration** (Statutory Declarations Act 1959 Cth or State equivalent).

**Draft Freezing Order** (Federal Court GPN-FRZG standard form)

1. Penal notice.
2. Specific assets identified up to A$`<amount>`.
3. Carve-outs:
   - **Ordinary living expenses** — specified amount per week.
   - **Ordinary business expenses** — specified amount per month.
   - **Legal expenses** — for defending the proceedings.
4. Disclosure order (assets list within 7 days).
5. Service mode + timing.
6. Return date for inter partes hearing.
7. Liberty to apply on 48 hours' notice.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Federal Court GPN-FRZG standard form** is mandatory — deviations require leave.
- **Ex parte mandatory** in practice — otherwise respondent dissipates.
- **Full and frank disclosure duty** — paramount.
- **Penal notice** prominent.
- **Carve-outs** must be specified at the time of grant.
- **Service of the order** within 7 days; respondent has right to apply to discharge.
- **Worldwide Mareva** — Australian courts cautious; require strong evidence of cross-border dissipation pattern.
- **Cross-undertaking fortification** — common where respondent's business reputation at stake.
- **Cardile third-party** Mareva — limited circumstances where third party holds respondent's assets.
- **AGLC4 citation** — *Cardile* (1999) 198 CLR 380 · *Cardile v LED Builders Pty Ltd* [1999] HCA 18.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"A freezing order may be made by the Court on the application of an interested party in any proceeding where the Court considers it appropriate. The order may operate to restrain the disposal of, or dealing with, the assets of a prospective judgment debtor where the Court is satisfied that there is a danger that any prospective judgment in favour of the applicant will be wholly or partly unsatisfied because of dissipation of assets." (FCR 2011 Rule 7.32, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The principal asset-preservation tool in fraud, breach-of-trust, and high-value contract claims. Solo practitioners must run a 48-72 hour drill: gather documentary evidence + ASIC searches + asset trace + draft affidavit + brief counsel. The full-and-frank-disclosure obligation is the principal trap — disclose every weakness to the ex parte judge. Federal Court GPN-FRZG standard form simplifies drafting but the affidavit work is intensive.
