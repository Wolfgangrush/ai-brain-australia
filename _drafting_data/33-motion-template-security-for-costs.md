## Authority Tier
Court Rule Form / Procedural Power

## Currency Warning
Courts are reluctant to order security if it would "stultify" the claim (prevent the plaintiff from proceeding). The defendant must bring the application promptly; delay is a significant factor in the court's discretion.

## Template Type
Motion / Interlocutory Application

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Court Header:** Case details.
2. **Orders Sought:**
   - The Plaintiff provide security for the Defendant's costs in the amount of $X by [Date].
   - Security be provided by [method, e.g., payment into Court or bank guarantee].
   - The proceeding be stayed until the security is provided.
   - If security is not provided by [Date], the proceeding be dismissed.
   - Costs of the motion.
3. **Grounds:**
   - Plaintiff is ordinarily resident outside Australia.
   - Plaintiff is a corporation and there is reason to believe it will be unable to pay costs.
   - Plaintiff is suing for the benefit of another person.
4. **Supporting Documents:**
   - Affidavit in Support (setting out impecuniosity and estimated costs).
   - Costs Estimate (itemized).
5. **Execution:** Signature of Solicitor.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Stay of Proceedings:** Crucial to include a stay order; otherwise, the defendant must keep preparing for trial while the security issue is unresolved.
- **Promptness:** The application should generally be made before the first significant costs are incurred (e.g., before discovery).
- **Amount:** Usually sought in "stages" (e.g., up to the end of discovery, then another stage for trial).

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "The Defendant applies for the following orders: 1. The Plaintiff provide security for the Defendant's costs in the sum of $X. 2. Until such security is given, the proceeding be stayed." (Standard UCPR Order).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
A strategic "shield" used when defending against a foreign or impecunious plaintiff. Prevents the client from being "blackmailed" into settlement by the threat of unrecoverable legal costs.
