## Authority Tier
Regulator Template / Statutory Framework (ACL)

## Currency Warning
Service contracts are heavily impacted by the **ACL Consumer Guarantees** (Part 3-2, Division 1). Unlike warranties, these guarantees cannot be excluded, restricted, or modified for consumer-level transactions.

## Template Type
Contract

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties:** Service Provider and Client.
2. **Scope of Services:** Detailed description (often in a Schedule).
3. **Term:** Start date, end date, or "until completion."
4. **Fees and Expenses:**
   - Fixed fee, hourly rate, or milestone payments.
   - GST treatment.
   - Reimbursable expenses.
5. **Performance Standards:**
   - "Due care and skill" (matches ACL statutory guarantee).
   - Specific Service Level Agreements (SLAs).
6. **Intellectual Property:**
   - Ownership of pre-existing IP.
   - Ownership of "Developed IP" (Client-owned vs. Licensed).
7. **Confidentiality:** Protection of client data and provider methods.
8. **Liability and Indemnity:**
   - Limitation of liability (must respect ACL s 64).
   - Proportionate liability (noting s 151H *Civil Liability Act 2002* NSW equivalent).
9. **Termination:**
   - For convenience (notice period).
   - For cause (breach).
10. **Dispute Resolution:** Escalation process.
11. **Governing Law:** Australian State jurisdiction.

## Drafting Conventions
- **ACL Compliance:** Do not attempt to exclude the guarantee that services will be rendered with "due care and skill" for contracts under $100,000.
- **Independent Contractor Status:** Explicit clause stating the provider is an independent contractor, not an employee (to mitigate "sham contracting" risk).
- **Insurance:** Requirement for Professional Indemnity and Public Liability insurance.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "The Service Provider must perform the Services: (a) with due care and skill; (b) in accordance with all applicable laws; and (c) within a reasonable time (where no time is specified)." (Derived from ACL sections 60, 61, 62).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Standard B2B or B2C engagement for consultants, IT providers, or tradespeople. Ensures clear expectations on "deliverables" and manages the risk of IP disputes.
