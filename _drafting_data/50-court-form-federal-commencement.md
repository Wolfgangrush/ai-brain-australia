## Authority Tier
Court Rule Form (Federal Court Rules 2011)

## Currency Warning
Ensure compliance with **Central Practice Note (CPN 1)** regarding the "Concise Statement" method of commencement, which is often preferred over a full Statement of Claim for commercial cases.

## Template Type
Court Form / Originating Process

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Form 1 (Title):** The standard "top half" of all Federal Court documents including Registry, Division, and Party names.
2. **Form 15 (Originating Application):**
   - **Details of Claim:** "The Applicant claims the relief set out in this application."
   - **Orders Sought:** Numbered list of final orders.
   - **Interlocutory Orders:** (If any) e.g., for early discovery or injunctions.
   - **Claim for Interlocutory Relief:** (If applicable).
   - **Applicant's Address for Service:** Crucial for receiving court documents.
3. **Execution:** Signature of Solicitor.
4. **Notice to Respondent:** Standard warning that failure to attend may lead to judgment in their absence.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Joinder:** Ensure all necessary parties are joined in the Title (Form 1).
- **Accompanying Documents:** Must be filed with a **Statement of Claim** OR an **Affidavit** (Rule 8.05), plus a **Genuine Steps Statement** (Form 16).
- **Concise Statement:** If using the CPN 1 method, the Concise Statement replaces the Statement of Claim and should be no more than 5 pages.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "To the Respondent: The Applicant applies for the relief set out in this application. This application will be heard by the Court at the time and place specified below." (Standard Form 15 Notice).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The "gatekeeper" form for entry into the federal jurisdiction. Used for Corporations Act matters, Intellectual Property, Fair Work claims, and Trade Practices (ACL) disputes.
