## Currency Warning
Charging Orders attach the debtor's beneficial interest in: (a) shares of Australian-incorporated companies (registered with ASIC); (b) registered Australian land (registered with State Land Titles Office); (c) certain interests in trusts. Two-stage: provisional charge (ex parte) + absolute charge (inter partes).

## Template Type
Two-stage Application

## Structural Skeleton

**Stage 1 — Provisional Charging Order (Ex Parte)**

*Interlocutory Application*

1. **Heading** — court · case number · parties.
2. **Title** — "Interlocutory Application — Provisional Charging Order".
3. **Reliefs sought**:
   - Provisional charging order over [shares / land / specified asset] of the judgment debtor up to value of A$`<amount>`.
   - Costs.
4. **Affidavit**:
   - Judgment + outstanding balance.
   - Asset identified — share certificate / land title reference / registered details.
   - Beneficial ownership of debtor (often subject of dispute).
   - Service intentions.

**Stage 2 — Charging Order Absolute (Inter Partes)**

- Service on debtor + interested third parties (co-owners · mortgagees · trustees).
- Hearing to consider objections.
- If absolute, charge registered:
  - **Shares** — notice to company secretary + ASIC register annotation.
  - **Land** — caveat / charge registered with State Land Titles Office.
- Subsequent sale order for realisation.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Shares — Australian-incorporated companies only** — ASIC register essential; UEN / ACN.
- **Land** — registered with State Land Titles Office (NSW LRS · Vic Land Registry · QLD Department of Resources · etc.).
- **No charging order on superannuation** — protected by SIS Act.
- **No charging order on Centrelink** — protected by Social Security Act.
- **Family-home considerations** — joint tenancy with spouse may attract spousal-protection issues.
- **Statutory exemptions** — debtor's principal place of residence has some protection under bankruptcy regime (by analogy).
- **Subsequent sale** — separate order for realisation; equity of redemption preserved until sale.
- **Currency conversion** — foreign-currency judgments registered at exchange rate on registration date.
- **PPSR consideration** — security interests in personal property registered with PPSR may take priority over charging order.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The Court may, for the purpose of enforcing a judgment or order requiring a person to pay money, make an order imposing on any specified property of the judgment debtor a charge for securing the payment of the judgment debt." (FCR 2011 Part 41 Division 41.4, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Charging orders are the principal remedy against debtors whose principal asset is shareholding (private companies · listed shares) or real estate (property investors · founders of private companies). Solo practitioners should identify chargeable assets during the underlying proceedings via discovery + ASIC searches + Land Titles searches. The state-specific land-registration procedures + the ASIC company-share registration vary slightly; verify current per-state requirements.
