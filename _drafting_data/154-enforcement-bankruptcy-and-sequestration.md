## Currency Warning
Sequestration order under Bankruptcy Act s 43 is the principal personal-bankruptcy enforcement remedy where the judgment debt exceeds A$10,000 (threshold under Bankruptcy Regulations; verify current as periodically updated). Available where debtor has committed an "act of bankruptcy" within the last 6 months — most commonly failure to comply with a Bankruptcy Notice (s 41).

## Template Type
Bankruptcy Notice + Creditor's Petition + Supporting Affidavits

## Structural Skeleton

**(A) BANKRUPTCY NOTICE** (precursor to petition)

1. **Form** — Form 1 (Bankruptcy Regulations).
2. **Sum due** — must exceed prescribed threshold (currently A$10,000).
3. **Identification of debt** — final judgment or order.
4. **Period for compliance** — 21 days from service.
5. **Personal service** on debtor (or in accordance with Court direction for substituted service).
6. **Effect of non-compliance** — commits act of bankruptcy under s 41; ground for petition.
7. **Issuing authority** — Federal Court Registrar (typically).

**(B) CREDITOR'S PETITION**

1. **Heading** — Federal Court / FCFCOA · bankruptcy jurisdiction · case number · parties.
2. **Title** — "Creditor's Petition".
3. **Petitioning creditor** — name + capacity.
4. **Debtor** — name · address · ACN/ABN if business · occupation.
5. **Act of bankruptcy** alleged — typically non-compliance with Bankruptcy Notice.
6. **Sum due** — principal + interest + costs (separately itemised).
7. **Reliefs sought**:
   - Sequestration order under Bankruptcy Act s 43.
   - Appointment of trustee (Official Trustee in default; private trustee with consent).
   - Costs (added to bankruptcy estate).

**(C) SUPPORTING AFFIDAVITS**

1. **Debt verification affidavit** — particulars of debt + judgment + outstanding.
2. **Service affidavit** — Bankruptcy Notice service + 21-day non-compliance.
3. **Act of bankruptcy affidavit** — particulars of the act + when committed.

**(D) HEARING + EFFECT**

- Bankrupt has right to oppose petition (typically on basis of: debt not due · debt paid · dispute · counterclaim · setoff · solvency).
- If sequestration order made:
  - Property vests in trustee.
  - Bankrupt cannot manage business / be director without leave.
  - Cannot travel without trustee permission.
  - 3-year automatic discharge (subject to objection).
  - AFSA + trustee administer estate.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Threshold** — A$10,000 minimum (verify current; periodically updated).
- **6-month window** — petition must be presented within 6 months of the act of bankruptcy.
- **Bankruptcy Notice form strict** — defects fatal; common errors are misstated sum + service-date errors.
- **Substituted service** — if personal service impossible, apply to Court for substituted service.
- **Disputed debt** — bankruptcy is not a debt-collection mechanism for disputed debts; courts dismiss petitions on disputed debts (*Re Lewer* (1957) 99 CLR 619; *Wenkart v Commissioner of Taxation* (1996) 70 FCR 365).
- **Counterclaim / setoff defence** — if debtor has bona fide cross-claim, petition typically dismissed.
- **Costs of petition** — bear by petitioning creditor initially; recoverable from estate.
- **Bankrupt's protection** — personal property under exemption thresholds (vehicle · tools of trade · household items).
- **Family home** — vests in trustee BUT spouse's interest preserved; trustee sells subject to spouse's share.
- **PPSA interests** — pre-existing security interests registered on PPSR have priority over trustee.
- **Superannuation** — protected; not part of bankrupt estate.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"If a debtor commits an act of bankruptcy, the Court may make a sequestration order against the estate of the debtor on a creditor's petition presented within 6 months after the commission of the act of bankruptcy." (Bankruptcy Act 1966, s 43, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Personal bankruptcy is the principal enforcement remedy against insolvent individual debtors with non-disputed debts above the threshold. Solo practitioners commonly use bankruptcy as a credible threat (Bankruptcy Notice issued; debtor pays before petition) more often than as the final remedy. The disputed-debt defence is the principal failure ground — bankruptcy is not a substitute for civil litigation on contested debts.
