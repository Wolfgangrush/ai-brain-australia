## Currency Warning
Australian trial advocacy is written-submission dominant. Opening submissions filed at trial commencement; closing submissions filed post-evidence (typically 14-21 days). Judgments substantially written from closing submissions; oral closings refine but do not replace.

## Template Type
Submission

## Structural Skeleton

**Opening Submissions** (filed at trial commencement)

1. **Cover sheet** — court · case number · parties · counsel · solicitor · date.
2. **Index of contents**.
3. **Introduction** (1 paragraph) — case context · party position in one sentence.
4. **Trial issues** — bulleted list (typically 3-7 issues); each issue framed as a question.
5. **Agreed facts** — cross-referenced to Agreed Statement of Facts (where one exists).
6. **Disputed facts** — concise statement of disputes; how each side's evidence will address.
7. **Witness list + order** — names · who calls · expected duration.
8. **Documents bundle reference** — Court Book pagination.
9. **Authorities bundle reference** — index + key authorities flagged.
10. **Legal framework**:
    - For each cause of action: elements + leading authority.
    - For each defence: elements + leading authority.
11. **Application of law to facts** — each issue addressed; party's case on each.
12. **Reliefs sought**:
    - Declaratory.
    - Monetary (with quantum + interest under FCAA s 51A or state equivalent).
    - Injunctive.
    - Costs (party-party · indemnity grounds).
13. **Conclusion** (1 paragraph).

**Closing Submissions** (filed post-evidence)

1. **Cover sheet + date · page count · word count**.
2. **Introduction** — recap of evidence heard + party's confirmed position.
3. **Trial issues revisited** — each addressed with reference to evidence given.
4. **Evidence analysis**:
   - For each fact issue: which witnesses testified · whose evidence preferred · why · cross-examination breakdowns (with transcript pinpoints).
   - Documentary evidence weighed against witness evidence.
5. **Credibility findings sought** — particular witnesses to be disbelieved + reasons.
6. **Legal arguments revisited** — refined in light of evidence.
7. **Authority application** — case-law applied to factual findings sought.
8. **Quantum** — computed with reference to evidence (expert reports · accounting records).
9. **Costs**:
   - Basis (party-party · indemnity).
   - Calderbank offer history (if relevant).
   - Conduct grounds for indemnity (frivolous claim · refusal to settle reasonable offer · misconduct).
10. **Disposition sought** — specific terms of order proposed:
    - Judgment for `<sum>` against `<respondent>` with interest from `<date>` until payment.
    - Declarations.
    - Injunctions.
    - Costs.
11. **Form of judgment** annexed — short paragraph form for the Court to adopt.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Page limits** — Federal Court typically 80-150 pages for 5-day trial; verify GPN-WRTS.
- **Word count** — page limit AND word count usually both stated.
- **Pinpoint citations**:
  - Court Book (CB pp 234-237).
  - AEICs (AEIC of [Witness] paras 12-15).
  - Transcript (T 234/15 — page 234, line 15).
  - Authorities (*Robinson Helicopter* (2016) 261 CLR 297 at [43]).
- **AGLC4 citation** mandatory.
- **Concede minor issues** — focus on the determinative issues.
- **Address every key piece of opposing evidence** — silence on damaging documents signals weakness.
- **Cross-examination breakdowns** — quote operative exchanges; explain reliability issues.
- **No new evidence in closings** — only argument on evidence already in the record.
- **Reply submissions** — usually permitted within a short window after opposing party's closings; limited to new matters.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Written submissions shall identify the issues for determination, summarise the evidence, set out the legal arguments, and identify the authorities relied upon. They shall be concise and focused. Pinpoint citations to the evidence and to authorities are required." (Federal Court Practice Note GPN-WRTS, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The closing submission is THE document of the trial — judgments are written largely from it. Solo practitioners should allocate substantial post-trial time to drafting (3-7 working days for complex case). Common mistakes: (a) re-presenting AEIC narrative without engaging with actual evidence given; (b) failing to address opposing party's strongest documents; (c) treating quantum as an afterthought.
