## Currency Warning
Writ of execution against goods (also called Writ of Sale) is the principal execution against movable property of the judgment debtor. Executed by the Sheriff (Federal Court · NSW · VIC) or equivalent state enforcement officer. 12-year enforcement period under Limitation Act + state equivalents; revival required after expiry.

## Template Type
Application / Court Form

## Structural Skeleton

**Request for Issue of Writ**

1. **Heading** — court · case number · parties (Judgment Creditor + Judgment Debtor).
2. **Title** — "Request for Issue of Writ of Execution against Goods".
3. **Identification of judgment** — date · sum awarded · interest · costs.
4. **Sum remaining due** — principal + accrued interest + costs assessed - any partial payment.
5. **Locations of goods to be seized**:
   - Known business / residential addresses.
   - Identifiable specific items (vehicles by registration · machinery).
6. **Annexures** — sealed copy of judgment · cost certificate · proof of partial payments (if any).
7. **Signature** — solicitor.
8. **Filing fee** — current FCR Schedule 1 fee.

**Writ of Execution** (sealed by Registrar)

- Authorises Sheriff to seize movable goods up to value of judgment debt + interest + costs of execution.
- **Excludes**:
  - Tools of trade up to prescribed value (state-specific).
  - Necessary household items.
  - Clothing.
  - Provisions for debtor and dependents (per Bankruptcy Act exemption schedule by analogy).
- Directs sale by public auction after notice period (typically 21 days minimum).
- Surplus (if any) returned to debtor.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Pre-action asset trace** — identify movable assets before issue (ASIC registers · land titles · personal property securities register PPSR · vehicle registries).
- **Sheriff coordination** — execution scheduled with sheriff's office; cost borne by judgment creditor initially.
- **Locked premises** — court order required for forced entry; usually requires further application.
- **Third-party claims** — third parties claiming ownership file interpleader; halts sale.
- **Exemption schedule** — narrower than UK; tools-of-trade limit modest.
- **Cost of execution** added to judgment debt — sheriff fees · storage · advertising · auction commission.
- **State variation** — sheriff procedures differ slightly across states; verify state-specific rules.
- **Federal vs State writs** — Federal Court judgments execute through state Sheriff under FCR Part 41; state-court judgments execute through state-specific procedures.
- **Combined with garnishee + EJD** — most effective strategy uses Writ + garnishee + EJD in parallel.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"A writ of execution against goods is an order to a Sheriff requiring the Sheriff to seize and sell goods of the judgment debtor up to the value of the judgment debt." (FCR 2011 Part 41, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The default execution remedy for commercial judgments. Practical effectiveness depends on the debtor's movable-asset base — limited against insolvent or asset-light debtors. Solo practitioners should advise creditor clients that Writ combined with examination-of-judgment-debtor (file 153) provides the best information yield even where physical execution yields little. State sheriff procedures vary; verify current state-specific rules before filing.
