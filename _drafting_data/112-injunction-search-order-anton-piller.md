## Currency Warning
Search orders (Anton Piller orders) granted sparingly. Three pre-conditions: (a) extremely strong prima facie case; (b) very serious damage potential / actual; (c) clear evidence respondent has incriminating documents / things in possession AND real possibility of destruction. Order executed by independent supervising solicitor (NOT a party's solicitor) per harmonised standard form. Federal Court GPN-SRCH supplies the standard order template + procedural safeguards.

## Template Type
Application + Supporting Affidavit + Draft Search Order (Federal Court GPN-SRCH standard form) + Supervising Solicitor Engagement

## Structural Skeleton

**Application + Affidavit** (combined; ex parte)

1. **Heading** — Federal Court · case number · parties.
2. **Title** — "Interlocutory Application — Search Order (Anton Piller)".
3. **Reliefs sought**:
   - Order permitting the applicant's representatives to enter specified premises between specified hours to search for and seize copies of specified documents / things.
   - Independent supervising solicitor designated (named + accredited).
   - Respondent ordered to permit entry · produce items · provide passwords + access to electronic devices.
   - Items seized to be held in custody of supervising solicitor pending further order.
   - Respondent's right to refuse entry pending taking legal advice (2-hour window).
   - Liberty to apply on 48 hours' notice.
   - Costs of the application reserved.
4. **Undertaking as to damages** offered + fortified if reputational damage is high.
5. **Penal notice** prominent.

**Supporting Affidavit**

1. **Deponent** — applicant with personal knowledge.
2. **Extremely strong prima facie case** — documentary evidence + admissions of liability + evidence of fraud / counterfeit / breach.
3. **Serious damage** — quantified loss · ongoing infringement · trade-secret misuse · imminent reputational damage.
4. **Respondent in possession of incriminating material** — specific identification + evidence of possession.
5. **Real possibility of destruction** — respondent's character · prior destruction · evidence of cover-up attempts · timing pressure.
6. **Less intrusive alternatives** — explanation of why a production order or Mareva insufficient.
7. **Supervising solicitor's engagement letter** — independent solicitor (typically partner-grade) + experience + availability + estimated cost.
8. **Cross-undertaking** — fortified where respondent's business reputation at stake.
9. **Annexures** — evidence of liability · evidence of risk of destruction · supervising solicitor's CV.
10. **Statutory declaration**.

**Draft Search Order** (Federal Court GPN-SRCH standard form)

1. Penal notice.
2. Categories of documents / things specified with precision.
3. Independent supervising solicitor identified.
4. Search procedure — supervising solicitor reads order aloud in respondent's presence; respondent's right to legal advice (2 hours); search executed under supervision.
5. Items seized held by supervising solicitor.
6. Confidentiality + no-press provisions.
7. Return date for inter partes hearing within 7-14 days.
8. Cost of search execution (applicant bears initially).

## Drafting Conventions
- **Highest threshold of any civil remedy** — courts grant exceptionally.
- **Independent supervising solicitor** mandatory — provided by an independent firm; partner-grade; experience with Anton Piller.
- **Specific identification** of documents / things — generic "all documents relating to X" categories refused; specific files / file types / hard-drives required.
- **Respondent's right to refuse entry** for short period to take legal advice — typically 2 hours.
- **Service of order + reading aloud** by supervising solicitor in respondent's presence.
- **No press / no third parties** — strict confidentiality until further order.
- **Cross-undertaking fortification** common.
- **Cost of supervising solicitor** — A$15,000-A$50,000 typical for a single execution.
- **Electronic devices** — protocols for imaging hard drives + smartphones; forensic preservation.
- **AGLC4** — *Long v Specifier Publications* (1998) 44 NSWLR 545.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"A search order may be made by the Court where the applicant has shown an extremely strong prima facie case, serious damage potential, clear evidence that the respondent has incriminating documents or things in his possession, and a real possibility that the respondent may destroy such material before any inter partes application can be made." (Synthesised from FCR 2011 Rule 7.42 + GPN-SRCH + Anton Piller principles.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Used in (a) IP infringement (counterfeiting · trade-secret misuse); (b) breach of post-employment confidentiality / restraint clauses; (c) computer-fraud cases. The supervising-solicitor cost is significant; applicants should pre-engage a firm with Anton Piller experience. The "less intrusive alternatives" hurdle is the principal refusal ground — applicants must show why production orders or Mareva insufficient.
