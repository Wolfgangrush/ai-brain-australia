## Currency Warning
In the Federal Court, a Reply must be filed within **14 days** of service of the Defence (Rule 16.33). In NSW UCPR, it is also 14 days (Rule 14.4). Late filing may require leave of the Court.

## Template Type
Pleading

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Court Header:** Consistent with previous pleadings.
2. **Response to Defence:**
   - Admitting, denying, or not admitting new facts raised in the Defence.
   - Joinder of issue (e.g., "The Plaintiff joins issue with the Defendant on the Defence").
3. **Pleading to New Material:** If the Defence raises a new fact (e.g., a "release" or "accord and satisfaction"), the Plaintiff must plead the facts that defeat that new material (e.g., "The release was obtained by duress").
4. **Signing Block:** Signature of Solicitor/Counsel.

## Drafting Conventions
- **No Mere Joinder of Issue:** Do not file a Reply that simply denies everything in the Defence; a joinder of issue is implied if no Reply is filed (UCPR 14.27). Only file if you need to plead *new* facts in response to the Defence.
- **No Departure:** A Reply must not contradict the Statement of Claim. If the Plaintiff needs to change their primary case, they should **amend** the Statement of Claim instead.
- **Conciseness:** Keep the Reply focused strictly on the new matters raised by the Defendant.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "As to paragraph 12 of the Defence, the Plaintiff: (a) admits that a document titled 'Release' was signed; but (b) says that the Release is void for lack of consideration."

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Used to respond to "gotcha" defences like limitation periods or contractual waivers. Essential for ensuring the Plaintiff's version of those specific events is before the court.
