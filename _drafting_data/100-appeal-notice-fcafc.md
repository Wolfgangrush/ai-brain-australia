## Currency Warning
Appeals to the Full Court of the Federal Court of Australia (FCAFC) from single-judge decisions. Notice of Appeal must be filed within **21 days** of the judgment / order (FCR Rule 36.03). Extensions on application only with compelling reasons (FCR Rule 36.05).

## Template Type
Court Form / Notice of Appeal

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Heading** — Federal Court of Australia · "On Appeal from a Single Judge of the Federal Court of Australia" · appeal number · year · parties (Appellant + Respondent).
2. **Form 121** under FCR 2011 — Notice of Appeal.
3. **Identification of orders appealed**:
   - Court below (single-judge or FCFCOA appellate division).
   - Date of judgment / order.
   - Operative orders appealed (verbatim recital).
4. **Grounds of appeal** (numbered):
   - Question of law (where appeal is from FCFCOA general federal jurisdiction).
   - Mixed fact and law.
   - Pure fact (limited; *Warren v Coombes* principle applies in equity).
5. **Orders sought from Full Court**:
   - Allow appeal.
   - Set aside orders below.
   - Substitute order (specified) OR remit to single judge.
   - Costs of appeal + costs below.
6. **Service** — on respondent within 7 days of filing; on intervening parties.
7. **Filing fee** — verify current FCR Schedule 1 fee.

## Drafting Conventions
- **21-day clock starts on date of order** — not on reasons; FCR 36.03.
- **Permission requirement** — most appeals as of right (FCAA s 24); but interlocutory orders need leave (FCAA s 24(1A)).
- **Findings of fact** — Full Court will not disturb absent demonstrable error (*Robinson Helicopter Co Inc v McDermott* [2016] HCA 22).
- **Mixed fact and law** — Full Court more willing to intervene.
- **Stay of execution** — separate application; not automatic.
- **AGLC4 citation** — neutral citation first: *[YYYY] FCAFC NN* / *[YYYY] FCA NN* / *[YYYY] HCA NN*.
- **No new evidence** — only on Full Court leave (*CDJ v VAJ* [1998] HCA 67 fresh-evidence principles).
- **Cross-appeal** — respondent files within 21 days of being served.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"The Full Court of the Federal Court of Australia has jurisdiction to hear and determine appeals from judgments of the Court constituted by a single Judge exercising the original jurisdiction of the Court." (Federal Court of Australia Act 1976, s 24(1), paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The principal appellate vehicle from single-judge Federal Court decisions. Solo practitioners must triage immediately: 21 days is unforgiving. Grounds drafting is the critical task — vague grounds drafted on a "we'll work it out" basis routinely struck out. Strong grounds focus on (a) legal error in applying the test; (b) failure to consider material evidence; (c) plainly wrong findings inconsistent with documentary record.
