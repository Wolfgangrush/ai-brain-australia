## Currency Warning
Deed of Company Arrangement (DOCA) is the principal rehabilitation outcome of Voluntary Administration. Binding on all creditors (per s 444D) including secured creditors who voted FOR or did not vote (subject to specific limits). Court approval not required (creditor vote sufficient) but Court may intervene under s 445D-445F.

## Template Type
Deed (Statutory Form)

## Structural Skeleton

1. **Parties**:
   - Company (under Voluntary Administration).
   - Deed Administrator (formerly Administrator).
   - (Where applicable) Proponent — typically director · related party · third-party investor offering rescue funding.
2. **Recitals**:
   - Company subject to Voluntary Administration from `<date>`.
   - Administrator's Section 439A Report recommended DOCA.
   - Creditors' Second Meeting resolved by majority in number AND majority in value to enter DOCA.
   - Deed effective from `<date>`.
3. **Definitions**:
   - "Effective Date" · "Termination Date" · "Reporting Period" · "Distribution Pool" · "Creditors" · "Excluded Claims".
4. **Property of the Company**:
   - All property subject to DOCA.
   - Excluded property (specific identified items).
5. **Sources of Funding**:
   - Existing company assets.
   - Proponent contribution (cash · loan facility · asset transfer).
   - Recoveries from voidable transactions.
   - Recovery of insurance proceeds.
6. **Creditor Claims**:
   - All Admitted Claims (per Administrator's adjudication).
   - Treatment categories (priority creditors · secured creditors · ordinary unsecured · related-party creditors).
   - Distribution priorities (statutory + DOCA-specific).
7. **Distribution to Creditors**:
   - Pro-rata distribution within priority ranks.
   - Dividend rate (cents in the dollar).
   - Timing of distributions.
8. **Release of Claims**:
   - Each creditor's claim against the company released upon final distribution.
   - Carve-outs (criminal · regulatory · personal claims against directors not released).
9. **Directors' Position**:
   - Directors may continue trading subject to Deed Administrator supervision.
   - Directors' personal liability for insolvent-trading not affected (s 588GA safe harbour may apply).
10. **Deed Administrator's Powers**:
    - Continue or cease trading.
    - Sell assets.
    - Pursue voidable-transaction recoveries.
    - Investigate director conduct.
11. **Termination of DOCA**:
    - On completion of all obligations (s 445C).
    - By creditor resolution (s 445A).
    - By Court order (s 445D — non-compliance, oppression, public interest).
12. **Effect of Termination**:
    - DOCA satisfied → company continues; creditors' claims released.
    - DOCA terminated for non-compliance → liquidation typically follows.
13. **Reporting + Information Rights**:
    - Quarterly reports to creditors.
    - Annual report to ASIC.
14. **Variation of DOCA**:
    - By creditor vote (s 445F).
    - By Court order (s 445E).
15. **Execution** — as a deed; per Corporations Act s 127.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Creditor vote threshold** — majority in number AND majority in value of voting creditors (s 600A); ties broken by chair's casting vote.
- **Related-party creditors** — distinguishable; courts may scrutinise related-party voting (s 445C).
- **Secured creditors** — DOCA does not bind secured creditors who voted AGAINST (s 444D); separate negotiation required.
- **Priority creditors** (employees · ATO · etc.) — statutory priorities preserved.
- **Voidable-transaction recoveries** (uncommercial transactions · unfair preferences · uncommercial loans) — Corporations Act ss 588FA-588FJ.
- **Court intervention** — creditor or interested party may apply for relief; common where DOCA is oppressive or skews against creditor group.
- **Termination if non-compliance** — automatic in some cases; otherwise creditor resolution or Court.
- **Tax treatment** — capital + revenue distinctions; cross-reference Income Tax Assessment Act 1997.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"A deed of company arrangement, when executed, binds the company, its officers and members, the deed's administrator, every creditor of the company who would have been a creditor of the company on the relevant day in relation to the deed, and every owner or lessor of property used or occupied by the company." (Corporations Act 2001, Section 444D, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The DOCA is the principal rehabilitation vehicle in Australian insolvency. Solo practitioners advising clients in distress should consider DOCA as the desirable outcome of VA; advising creditors should consider DOCA as a possible recovery path. The principal craft is structuring the DOCA to balance going-concern preservation against creditor recovery — over-aggressive DOCAs that favour proponents fail at the creditor vote.
