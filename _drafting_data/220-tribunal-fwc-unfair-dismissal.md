Primary — Fair Work Act 2009 + FWC Rules 2013.

## Currency Warning
Unfair dismissal jurisdiction under FW Act Part 3-2. Strict 21-day filing deadline from dismissal (s 366(1)(a)). Eligible employees: those with minimum employment period satisfied (6 months small business; 6 months other; 12 months for small business — check current Small Business Fair Dismissal Code threshold of < 15 employees). High-income threshold excludes employees earning > the annual indexed amount (verify current — approximately A$175,000 + as of recent reviews).

## Template Type
Tribunal Form (Form F2) + Statement

## Structural Skeleton

**Form F2 — Application for Unfair Dismissal Remedy**

1. **Online filing** via FWC online portal at fwc.gov.au; manual filing available.
2. **Applicant identification** — name · NRIC equivalent (TFN not required) · address · contact details · employment commencement + termination dates.
3. **Respondent (Employer) identification** — business name · ABN · address · contact person.
4. **Employment particulars**:
   - Job title + role.
   - Hours of work.
   - Award / Enterprise Agreement coverage.
   - Salary (basic · allowances · annualised total).
   - Probationary period (if applicable).
5. **Termination particulars**:
   - Date of termination.
   - Method (verbal · written · summary).
   - Reason given by employer.
6. **Grounds for unfair dismissal** (FW Act s 387):
   - **Harsh, unjust or unreasonable** (the test).
   - Sub-factors:
     - Valid reason for dismissal (related to capacity or conduct).
     - Notification of the reason.
     - Opportunity to respond.
     - Support person allowed at meetings.
     - Warnings about poor performance (if performance-related).
     - Procedural fairness in process.
     - Size of employer (relevance to procedures expected).
     - Absence of dedicated HR (for very small employers).
7. **Remedy sought**:
   - **Reinstatement** (primary remedy under FW Act).
   - **Compensation** (if reinstatement impracticable; capped at 26 weeks' wages or half the high-income threshold — verify current).
8. **Filing fee** — current fee (verify FWC website; waivable on hardship grounds).
9. **Signature** — applicant.

**Supporting Statement**

1. **Background** — employment history.
2. **Dismissal narrative** — facts surrounding termination.
3. **Why dismissal was unfair** — applied to s 387 factors.
4. **Reinstatement vs compensation** — preferred remedy + reasons.
5. **Mitigation** — efforts to find alternative employment post-dismissal.
6. **Documents annexed** — employment contract · payslips · termination letter · warnings · correspondence.

## Drafting Conventions
- **21-day deadline strict** — extensions only in exceptional circumstances (s 366(2) "exceptional circumstances" test).
- **High-income threshold** — verify current figure annually; excludes employees above threshold from FWC jurisdiction (unless covered by Modern Award / EA).
- **Small Business Fair Dismissal Code** — separate framework for < 15 employees; compliance with Code is defence (s 388).
- **Minimum employment period**:
  - Small business (< 15 employees): 12 months.
  - Other employers: 6 months.
- **Self-represented friendly** — FWC designed for lay applicants; lawyer representation requires Commission permission under s 596 (typically granted).
- **Conciliation conference** — mandatory; held within ~14 days of filing; most matters settle at this stage.
- **Hearing** — if not settled at conciliation, listed for arbitration hearing.
- **Remedies**:
  - **Reinstatement** with continuity of service + back-pay.
  - **Compensation** — calculated under s 392; capped at 26 weeks (or high-income equivalent).
- **General Protections (FW Act Part 3-1)** — alternative or parallel jurisdiction for "adverse action" (broader than unfair dismissal); 21-day filing also; reverse onus of proof on employer.
- **Bullying / sexual harassment** — separate FW Act regimes (Part 6-4B + recent expansions); different application form (F72 + F72A).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"A person has been unfairly dismissed if: (a) the person has been dismissed; and (b) the dismissal was harsh, unjust or unreasonable; and (c) the dismissal was not consistent with the Small Business Fair Dismissal Code; and (d) the dismissal was not a case of genuine redundancy." (Fair Work Act 2009, s 385, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
FWC unfair dismissal is the highest-volume employment-law jurisdiction in Australia. Solo practitioners' role is pre-filing strategy (assess merit + advise on lodgement timing) + Form F2 drafting + conciliation representation + hearing advocacy. The 21-day deadline is unforgiving. Solo practitioners must also assess General Protections alternative (broader scope, reverse onus, different remedies). Bullying / harassment regimes are increasingly important post-2023 expansions.
