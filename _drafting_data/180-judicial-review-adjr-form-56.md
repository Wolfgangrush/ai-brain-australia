## Authority Tier
Primary — ADJR Act 1977 + FCR 2011 + Judiciary Act + state-equivalent JR statutes.

## Currency Warning
Judicial review of Commonwealth administrative decisions under ADJR Act 1977 (Form 56 application — Federal Court). State administrative decisions reviewed under state JR statutes (NSW: ADJR-equivalent under Supreme Court Act 1970 s 69; Vic: Administrative Law Act 1978). **ART replaced AAT from October 2024** — internal merits-review tribunal for many decisions before JR. Constitutional writs available under Constitution s 75(v) against Commonwealth officers.

## Template Type
Court Form (Federal Court Form 56) + Supporting Affidavit

## Structural Skeleton

**(A) APPLICATION UNDER ADJR ACT (Form 56)**

1. **Heading** — Federal Court · case number · year · applicant + respondent (decision-maker + responsible Minister).
2. **Form 56** — Application for an Order of Review under ADJR Act.
3. **Identification of decision impugned**:
   - Date of decision.
   - Decision-maker (named office-holder · agency).
   - Operative terms of decision.
   - Whether the decision was made under enactment (ADJR jurisdictional requirement).
4. **Standing** — applicant's interest (typically a person aggrieved · directly affected · special-interest applicant).
5. **Reliefs sought**:
   - Order quashing the decision.
   - Mandamus / mandatory order.
   - Prohibition.
   - Declaration of unlawfulness.
   - Costs.
6. **Time-limit** — 28 days from date of decision (extendable on application with compelling reason; ADJR Act s 11).
7. **Service** — on decision-maker + responsible Minister + AGS (Australian Government Solicitor for Commonwealth matters).

**(B) GROUNDS OF REVIEW (ADJR Act s 5)** — applicant pleads one or more:

1. **Breach of natural justice** (procedural fairness — *Kioa v West* (1985) 159 CLR 550).
2. **Procedures required by law were not observed** (statutory procedure breach).
3. **Decision-maker had no jurisdiction** (lack of authority).
4. **Decision was not authorised by enactment** (ultra vires).
5. **Decision involved error of law** (whether or not on face of record).
6. **Decision was induced by fraud**.
7. **No evidence to justify decision** (or no probative evidence).
8. **Decision was otherwise contrary to law**.
9. **Improper exercise of power** — including:
   - Bad faith.
   - Failing to consider relevant matters.
   - Considering irrelevant matters.
   - Acting on a directive (delegated discretion).
   - Acting at the direction of another person.
   - Inflexible application of policy.
   - Result was unreasonable (Wednesbury · *Minister for Immigration v Li* (2013) 249 CLR 332).

**(C) SUPPORTING AFFIDAVIT**

1. **Deponent** — applicant or authorised representative.
2. **Factual basis** for each ground.
3. **Procedural history** — original decision · internal review (if any) · ART decision (if applicable) · administrative steps taken.
4. **Exhaustion of administrative remedies** — applicant has exhausted available internal review + tribunal merits review.
5. **Standing evidence** — applicant's interest documented.
6. **Annexures** — decision being challenged · reasons (if obtained under ADJR s 13).

## Drafting Conventions
- **28-day time-limit** — strict; FCR Rule 31.05.
- **Statement of reasons** — applicant may request under ADJR s 13 before issuing; helpful for grounds drafting.
- **Standing** — *AMS v AIF* (1999) 199 CLR 160 + *Onus v Alcoa* (1981) 149 CLR 27 — broader than "person aggrieved" in some contexts.
- **Decision-maker as respondent** — named office-holder + responsible Minister; AG may intervene.
- **Federal vs State JR** — Federal Court for Commonwealth decisions; state Supreme Court for state decisions.
- **ART exhaustion** — for decisions appealable to ART (Administrative Review Tribunal post-2024), exhaust ART first.
- **Stay of decision** — separate application; not automatic.
- **Costs** — typically party-party for successful applicant; AGS opposes routinely.
- **AGLC4 citation** — Constitutional + administrative law cases (*Kioa v West* · *Minister for Immigration v Li* · *Plaintiff M61/2010E v Commonwealth* (2010) 243 CLR 319).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"A person who is aggrieved by a decision to which this Act applies that is made under an enactment may apply to the Court for an order of review in respect of the decision on any one or more of the grounds set out in this section." (ADJR Act 1977, s 5(1), paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
JR is the principal challenge tool against Commonwealth + state administrative decisions: (a) migration decisions; (b) social-security decisions; (c) regulatory decisions (ASIC · APRA · ATO); (d) licensing decisions; (e) merits-review tribunal decisions (post-ART decisions). Solo practitioners must triage the 28-day window + ART exhaustion at intake. The grounds-of-review framework (ADJR s 5) is reasonably standardised; the principal craft is selecting the strongest 2-4 grounds + supporting affidavit.
