2026-05-19

## Authority Tier
Regulator Template (Fair Work Ombudsman) / Statutory Framework (Fair Work Act 2009)

## Currency Warning
Contracts must be updated to reflect the "Closing Loopholes" amendments to the *Fair Work Act 2009* (effective late 2023/2024), particularly regarding the definition of casual employment and the right to disconnect.

## Template Type
Contract / Letter of Engagement

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties:** Employer name/ABN and Employee name.
2. **Position and Duties:** Job title and requirement to perform duties faithfully.
3. **Type of Employment:** Full-time, Part-time, or Casual (critical distinction).
4. **Commencement Date:** Start date and (if applicable) end date for fixed-term.
5. **Probationary Period:** Duration (usually 3-6 months) and notice during probation.
6. **Remuneration:**
   - Base salary/wage.
   - Superannuation (compliance with Superannuation Guarantee).
   - Method and frequency of payment.
7. **Hours of Work:** Ordinary hours and overtime provisions.
8. **Modern Award / Enterprise Agreement Coverage:** Explicitly naming the applicable Award.
9. **Leave Entitlements:** Reference to National Employment Standards (NES) for Annual, Personal/Carer's, and Long Service Leave.
10. **Confidential Information and Intellectual Property:** Protection of employer assets.
11. **Termination of Employment:** Notice periods (must meet or exceed NES minimums).
12. **Policies and Procedures:** Incorporating by reference (but stating they are not contractual terms).
13. **Governing Law:** Usually the State of the principal place of business.

## Drafting Conventions
- **NES Supremacy:** Every Australian employment contract must include a clause stating that the contract is subject to the NES and that any term less favorable than the NES is void.
- **Fair Work Information Statement:** A mandatory clause acknowledging that the employee has been provided with the Fair Work Information Statement.
- **Casual Loading:** For casual contracts, the 25% loading must be clearly identified as being in lieu of leave entitlements to satisfy the "casual loading" offset rules.

## Fair-Use Excerpt (if applicable)
> "Your employment is governed by the terms and conditions in this agreement and the [Name of Award]. To the extent that a term of this agreement is less favourable than a provision of the National Employment Standards (NES), the NES will prevail." (Standard protective clause).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The primary tool for small business clients hiring staff. Ensures compliance with the complex "Fair Work" system and minimizes "sham contracting" or "underpayment" risks by correctly identifying Award coverage.
