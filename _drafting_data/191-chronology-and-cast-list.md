## Currency Warning
Chronology, cast list, and reading list filed alongside opening submissions and the Court Book. They orient the bench before evidence opens. Mandatory in complex cases; routine in all trials.

## Template Type
Procedural Documents (suite of three)

## Structural Skeleton

**(A) CHRONOLOGY OF EVENTS**

1. **Cover sheet** — court · case number · parties · counsel · date.
2. **Title** — "Chronology of Events".
3. **Format — three-column table**:

| Date | Event | Reference (CB / AEIC) |
|---|---|---|
| YYYY-MM-DD | Applicant incorporated | CB Tab 1, p 3 |
| YYYY-MM-DD | Contract signed between Applicant and Respondent | CB Tab 5, p 23 |
| YYYY-MM-DD | First instalment paid | CB Tab 12, p 87 |
| YYYY-MM-DD | Respondent alleged breach | CB Tab 18, p 134; AEIC of [Witness] paras 12-15 |

4. **Date discipline**:
   - Use ISO format (YYYY-MM-DD).
   - Distinguish documented dates from approximate / disputed dates.
   - Highlight key events in bold.
5. **Scope** — material events only; not exhaustive document index.

**(B) CAST LIST**

1. **Cover sheet**.
2. **Title** — "Cast List of Persons Referred to in the Pleadings and Trial".
3. **Format — table**:

| Name | Role | Capacity at material time | Witness for | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mr X | Director of Applicant | Authorised signatory of contract | Applicant | AEIC dated YYYY-MM-DD |
| Ms Y | Sole shareholder of Respondent | Beneficial owner | Respondent | Not called as witness |
| Mr Z | Auditor | External auditor for Applicant | Applicant | Expert report dated YYYY-MM-DD |

4. **Grouped by side** — Applicant's persons · Respondent's persons · neutral third parties.
5. **Indicate relationship** between persons (e.g., father / son · employer / employee).

**(C) READING LIST**

1. **Cover sheet**.
2. **Title** — "Pre-Trial Reading List for the Court".
3. **Estimated reading time** — total hours.
4. **Recommended pre-trial reading order**:

| Document | CB Reference | Reading time (min) |
|---|---|---|
| Statement of Claim | CB Tab 2, p 16-45 | 15 |
| Defence | CB Tab 3, p 46-78 | 15 |
| AEIC of Applicant's main witness | CB Tab B1, p 200-280 | 45 |
| AEIC of Respondent's main witness | CB Tab B2, p 281-360 | 45 |
| Contract dated YYYY-MM-DD | CB Tab 5, p 23-40 | 10 |
| Expert report on quantum | CB Tab D1, p 600-650 | 30 |
| Skeletal opening submissions | Separately filed | 30 |

5. **Total estimated reading time** — typically 4-8 hours.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Single document or three separate** — Australian practice varies; some courts prefer a single composite document, others separate filings. Verify with associate before trial.
- **Joint preparation** — parties often jointly prepare; failing agreement, each party files own.
- **Without prejudice to disputes** — chronology marks disputed dates clearly; cast list flags disputed roles.
- **Update during trial** — supplemental chronology may be filed after evidence-in-chief if new dates emerge.
- **Tab + page references** mandatory — every entry tied to CB pagination.
- **No advocacy** — these are orientation documents; arguments go in submissions, not in the chronology.
- **AGLC4** for any case references in the documents.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Parties shall, prior to trial, file a chronology of events, a cast list of persons, and a recommended reading list for the Court. These documents shall be concise, accurate, and referenced to the Court Book." (Federal Court Practice Note GPN-WRTS, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The chronology and cast list reduce trial-bench confusion; the reading list manages the judge's pre-trial preparation. Solo practitioners often skimp on these — and pay the price in oral-hearing time spent orienting the bench. Investment of 4-6 hours assembling these documents pays back in trial efficiency. Joint preparation with opposing counsel signals professional cooperation and is favoured by the bench.
