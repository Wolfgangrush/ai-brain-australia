## Currency Warning
Australian mediation framework: (a) CDRA 2011 imposes genuine-steps obligation before commencing certain Commonwealth-jurisdiction proceedings; (b) NMAS provides accreditation framework for mediators; (c) Federal Court routinely orders mediation under FCR Part 28; (d) State courts have parallel regimes. Settlement Convention on Mediation (Singapore Convention) — Australia signed but ratification status varies; check current ratification.

## Template Type
Pleading / Position Paper (WITHOUT PREJUDICE — for mediator only)

## Structural Skeleton

1. **Header**:
   - Mediator's name + NMAS accreditation reference.
   - Parties.
   - Date of mediation session.
   - Marking: **"WITHOUT PREJUDICE — FOR MEDIATOR'S EYES ONLY"** (Evidence Act s 131 + NMAS confidentiality).
2. **Subject-Matter of the Dispute** — 2-3 paragraphs of factual narrative.
3. **Procedural Posture**:
   - Court proceedings on foot (court · case number · stage) OR pre-action.
   - Court-ordered mediation OR contractual mediation OR voluntary.
4. **Client's Position on the Merits**:
   - Key facts asserted.
   - Cause of action / defence relied upon.
   - Statutory + case-law basis.
5. **Quantum**:
   - Claim value (principal · interest · costs).
   - Cost-of-litigation estimate (if continuing).
6. **BATNA (Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement)**:
   - Likely outcome at trial / award.
   - Time to judgment (12-24 months from current stage).
   - Enforcement risk (especially cross-border).
7. **Settlement parameters**:
   - Aspirational outcome.
   - Reasonable outcome.
   - Walk-away threshold (CONFIDENTIAL TO MEDIATOR — not shared with opposing party).
   - Non-monetary terms (apology · ongoing relationship · NDA · injunctive relief).
8. **Annexures** — key contractual documents · correspondence · expert valuations · prior settlement offers.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Two versions** — (a) shared with opposing party (filtered, no BATNA / walk-away); (b) mediator-only version with full BATNA.
- **WITHOUT PREJUDICE marking** mandatory under Evidence Act s 131.
- **Concise** — typically 8-15 pages.
- **Agreed facts vs disputed facts** — focus on dispute.
- **Confidentiality + privilege** — Evidence Act + NMAS protect communications from subsequent court use.
- **Court-ordered mediation** — failure to engage in good faith risks adverse costs orders.
- **Singapore Convention** — for international cross-border mediated settlements; check Australia's ratification status.
- **CDRA Genuine Steps Statement** — for Commonwealth-jurisdiction proceedings, statement of genuine steps taken before proceedings; mediation attendance can be a genuine step.
- **AGLC4** — *Field v Commissioner for Railways for NSW* (1957) 99 CLR 285 (without-prejudice privilege).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Evidence is not to be adduced of a communication that is made between persons in dispute, or between one or more persons in dispute and a third party, in connection with an attempt to negotiate a settlement of the dispute, or to negotiate the substance of any settlement." (Evidence Act 1995 (Cth), Section 131, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Mediation is now routine in Australian civil litigation — court-ordered + contractual + voluntary. Solo practitioners should treat mediation seriously — refusing to engage in good faith risks adverse costs orders + adverse inferences. The two-version discipline (filtered for opposing party, full for mediator) is the key drafting craft. NMAS-accredited mediators provide structured process; ad-hoc mediations require greater preparation.
