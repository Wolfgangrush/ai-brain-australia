## Currency Warning
Court Book preparation is mandatory in all Federal Court + most state Supreme Court trials. Applicant prepares; respondent reviews + agrees content. Electronic filing standards: searchable PDF · OCR text layer · bookmarks at tab dividers. Federal Court eLodgement portal for filing; state-court portals vary.

## Template Type
Procedural Document / Bundle

## Structural Skeleton

**COURT BOOK (CB) — TRIAL BUNDLE**

1. **Cover sheet**:
   - Court designation.
   - Case number · year · parties.
   - Volume number (if multi-volume).
   - Counsel · solicitor · date.

2. **Index of contents** — table of contents with CB pagination:

| Tab | Document | Date | Source | CB Pagination |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Originating Application | YYYY-MM-DD | Court file | 1-15 |
| 2 | Statement of Claim | YYYY-MM-DD | Court file | 16-45 |
| 3 | Defence | YYYY-MM-DD | Court file | 46-78 |
| ... | | | | |

3. **Organisation**:
   - **Section A** — Pleadings (Originating · Statement of Claim · Defence · Reply · Counterclaim · amendments).
   - **Section B** — AEICs (each party's affidavits + witness statements).
   - **Section C** — Documents relied upon at trial (chronological order within topic).
   - **Section D** — Expert reports.
   - **Section E** — Joint Statement of Experts (file 132).
   - **Section F** — Agreed Statement of Facts (where applicable).
   - **Section G** — Photographs · plans · technical drawings · audio-visual transcripts.

4. **Pagination** — continuous through the CB; not by section.

5. **Tab dividers** — physical / electronic bookmarks.

6. **Native format vs printed** — eLodgement supports searchable PDFs; native format for audio / video / spreadsheets attached separately.

7. **Joint Court Book** preferred — Federal Court strongly encourages parties to agree a Joint CB to avoid duplication.

8. **Disputed-document protocol** — documents whose authenticity / admissibility is disputed marked clearly; admissibility addressed in opening submissions or by Voir Dire.

## Drafting Conventions
- **Joint CB preferred** — Australian courts encourage parties to agree a single Court Book.
- **AGLC4 citation** for any case-law indexed in CB.
- **Chronological vs topical** — for documents, chronological in each topic typically clearest.
- **AEIC cross-references** — each AEIC paragraph that references a document should cite CB pagination.
- **Skeleton cross-references** — written submissions should cite CB pagination for every factual reference.
- **Electronic filing standards** — searchable PDF; OCR text layer; bookmarks at tab dividers.
- **Voluminous CBs** — multi-volume; pagination continuous; clear volume markers.
- **Native-format attachments** — audio · video · large spreadsheets attached as separate exhibits; CB has reference + transcript / summary.
- **PPSR / Land Title / ASIC documents** — original-form copies preserved.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
"Parties shall prepare a Court Book containing all documents that the parties intend to rely upon at trial. The Court Book shall be paginated and indexed. Parties shall use reasonable efforts to agree the content of the Court Book." (Federal Court Practice Note GPN-WRTS, paraphrased.)

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The Court Book is the principal physical / electronic artifact of the trial. Solo practitioners must invest significant time in assembly — disorganised CBs lose cases by losing the judge. Joint CBs are the norm in commercial cases; engage cooperatively with opposing solicitors. Pagination + indexing are tedious but critical — a 3,000-page CB with bad navigation is unusable at trial.
